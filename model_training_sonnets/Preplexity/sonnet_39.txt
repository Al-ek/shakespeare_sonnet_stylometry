XXXIX

The nightingale, withdrawn from summer’s cheer,
Still keeps within her breast the note of spring;
So I, though parted from thy presence dear,
Retain thy love as music lingering.
When absence strews its frost upon my breast,
I warm me with the embers of thy tone;
And though no lips respond to mine request,
Thy memory speaks where once thy voice had shone.
O cruel space that parts us with such art,
To measure miles between one soul’s desire!
Yet love, unbodied, mocks all maps of heart,
And lights afar with undiminish’d fire.
So distance fails, for where my thought doth move,
There flies my heart, the courier of my love.
