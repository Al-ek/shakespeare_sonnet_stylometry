V

The pen grows weak where passion would be strong,
And falters where the heart would speak most true;
No measured line can hold such boundless song,
Nor ink contain the depth I feel for you.
Yet still I strive, though words be poor and few,
To trace thy worth in some enduring frame,
For silence seems a greater wrong to do
Than risk the failure of imperfect name.
So take these lines, though humble be their art,
As shadows cast by something far more bright,
For what I cannot utter in my part
Lives fuller still beyond the reach of sight.
And if they fail, let this one truth remain:
My love exceeds what verse can e’er contain.
