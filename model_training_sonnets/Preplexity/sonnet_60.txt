LX

Farewell is but a prelude to return,
A pause that deepens meaning in the sound;
For sweetest bonds through absence still must yearn,
And truth through parting more secure is found.
I go not lost, but circled by thy grace,
A wandering moth that finds its flame afar;
No distance builds the wall time dares to trace,
No shadow dims the constancy we are.
The world may change, its banners fall or rise,
The tide may drown, the mountain bend and fade;
Yet love endures where mortal motion dies,
And makes of death another serenade.
Farewell? then bright beginning be thy part,
For what departs still lingers in the heart.
