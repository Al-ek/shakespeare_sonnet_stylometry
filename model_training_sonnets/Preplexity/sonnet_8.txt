VIII

Sweet contradiction governs all my thought,
For joy and grief together take their seat;
What once I shunned, now eagerly is sought,
And absence makes thy presence more complete.
I curse the hours that keep us far apart,
Yet bless them too, for they increase desire;
They wound me deep, yet strengthen still my heart,
And turn cold longing into living fire.
Thus love delights in paradox and play,
In weaving pain with pleasure so entwined,
That neither stands alone nor fades away,
But both together shape the lover’s mind.
So must I bear what none can disentangle:
A blissful peace within a restless tangle.
