V

If fame be fortune’s loud and fickle call,
Then let me live unknown, but sweetly known;
For better still to love and lose it all,
Than reign a king upon a joyless throne.
The crown weighs heavy where no heart has part,
And praise is cold that warms no gentle breast;
Give me one smile that stirs a poet’s art,
And I shall count my penance richly blest.
Not every light must burn for men to see,
Nor every heart kept pure be sung in time;
Yet in thy grace the truth reveals to me,
That love alone makes all the dust sublime.
So let them shout their names upon the sky,
I’ll whisper thine and find eternity.
