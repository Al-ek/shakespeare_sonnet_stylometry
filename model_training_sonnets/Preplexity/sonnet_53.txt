LIII

If music die, the silence takes its part,
And finds new voice within the spirit’s ear;
No tune but echoes faintly through the heart,
To make remembrance still its messenger.
So should thy song fall quiet evermore,
The hush itself would hum our tender lay;
And every breeze that passes by my door,
Would chant thy love though lips forget the way.
The sweetest notes are those that rest unstrung,
Where breath alone keeps melody alive;
For though no harp be touch’d by living tongue,
The soul repeats it still when dreams revive.
So may our music linger though we part,
Its rhythm timed to echo through the heart.
