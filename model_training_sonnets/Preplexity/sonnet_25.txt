XXV

The clock’s cold hand reproves the lazy day,
And marks the waste of youth’s untether’d flight;
Yet hold I dear each pause that might delay
The dusk between our greetings and goodnight.
Time’s cruel glass may show us soft decay,
And faintly blur the fires that once were known;
But though the frame of flesh and blood give way,
The soul, unmeasured, stands by love alone.
Where minutes die, remembrance builds her shrine,
And there thy likeness, ageless, keeps its seat;
Each breath I draw declares thy pulse is mine,
Each tear, a vow renewed by loss made sweet.
So count no hours—our season blooms entire,
For hearts unaged grow young through true desire.
