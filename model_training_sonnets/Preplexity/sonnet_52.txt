LII

The child of hope hath many mothers false,
Each promise feeds but wanes before the sun;
Yet true belief no counterfeit recalls,
For faith once born by love, cannot be undone.
So let the sophists reason out their end,
And prove the stars are weak, the heavens vain;
For though they talk, no syllable can bend
The will whose trust defies their proud disdain.
I hold thy truth as light within the storm,
A torch that mocks the thunder’s empty sound;
No doubt can fade what certainty keeps warm,
No dark can hide the glow in mercy found.
Belief is not of thought but heart’s estate,
And mine stands fixed, whatever men debate.
