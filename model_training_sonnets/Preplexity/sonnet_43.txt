XLIII

I met the dawn, her blush upon my face,
And asked if day could show me aught so fair;
She smiled and bade me seek some other place,
For only thou possess’st that wonder rare.
The sun, grown jealous, hid behind a cloud,
As if ashamed to burn where thou art known;
And every creature kept its silence proud,
Admitting beauty reigns in thee alone.
Then dark I prayed, “Descend, O blessed moon,
And lend thy light to frame her perfect hue”;
Yet even night withheld her kindly boon,
For fear her glow should pale the gift of you.
Thus nature’s host, in reverent discontent,
Must yield its art to thine omnipotent.
