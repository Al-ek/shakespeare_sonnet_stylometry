LV

Though oft I fear the silence of thy thought,
When lips are still and eyes not dared to speak,
I know within thy calm there dwelleth aught,
Of power the loudest soul could never seek.
For words but shadow feeling’s truer shrine,
And noise obscures what pure affection means;
A quiet vow achieves the most divine,
And strength lies hidden where restraint convenes.
So talk not, love, if talking tarnish’s peace,
Let silence reign between our hearts instead;
The soundless bond shall all our might increase,
Till mute devotion crowns what speech had fled.
In stillness lives a truth none can deny,
The loud are less, the silent nearer sky.
