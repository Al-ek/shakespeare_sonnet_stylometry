LVIII

Thou wert the fire that lit the author’s brain,
The thought that shaped my scattered musings whole;
I wrote, but every verse began thy name,
And ended near the echo of thy soul.
Each metaphor I bent became thine own,
Each rhyme sought rhythm from thy breathing tone;
Till all the page, though meant for worldly eyes,
Turned love’s confession hidden in disguise.
So now if fame should grant its passing cheer,
I ask not that my words be stood in glass;
They live through thee, their reason made sincere,
Their truth preserved when worldly glories pass.
I work, I write, yet all I truly mean,
Is love made infinite through mortal scene.
