XVIII

No man may own the hour that love begins,
Nor steal the dusk in which it softly ends;
It lives within the marrow of our sins,
And purifies what earthly thought contends.
So strange a fire that burns yet yields no ash,
So calm a storm that conquers without sound;
Where hearts collapse in one tremendous flash,
And crowns are made from ruin newly found.
If heaven judges such a mortal flame,
Then let me stand accused before its bar;
For though I lose the world in love’s brief name,
I gain the truth of what we ever are.
To burn for love is still the purest art,
And all the rest mere echo from the heart.
