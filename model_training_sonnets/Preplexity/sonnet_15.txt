XV

The passing hours like shadows swiftly flee,
And leave no trace of where their steps have been,
Yet each one seems more full of thee to me,
As if thou dwell’st within what lies unseen.
For time itself appears to take thy shape,
And every moment bears thy gentle mark;
No fleeting second ever can escape
The quiet glow thou castest through the dark.
Thus do I live within a world transformed,
Where all I touch or see reflects thy grace,
And every common thing becomes adorned
With something of thy presence in its place.
So time, once thief, now proves a faithful friend,
That brings me nearer thee with every end.
