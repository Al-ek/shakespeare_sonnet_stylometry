X

The mirror shows what time would have me see,
A face less bright than once it used to be;
Yet in thine eyes I find another truth,
Where age is crowned with an enduring youth.
For thou dost look beyond the outward frame,
And find a beauty time cannot impair,
A deeper grace no years can ever tame,
Nor sorrow dim, nor fortune strip or wear.
So learn I then to trust thy vision more
Than that which glass or fleeting glance imparts,
For love reveals a richer hidden store
That lies concealed within our mortal parts.
And in that sight I gladly now agree:
I am most fair when thou beholdest me.
