XVII

No painter’s art nor sculptor’s patient skill
Could e’er express what in thy being lies;
Their works, though fair, must bend to mortal will,
And fade beneath time’s slow-consuming skies.
But thou art wrought of something more divine,
A living form no artifice can claim,
Where beauty seems not borrowed nor confined,
But springs eternal from a secret flame.
Thus all creation seems but faint beside
The truth that in thy presence stands revealed,
And every crafted wonder must abide
In lesser place, its borrowed glory sealed.
For thou surpassest all that art can do:
A masterpiece no hand but love e’er knew.
