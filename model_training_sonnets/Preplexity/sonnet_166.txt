XII

O cruel sleep, that steals my will away,
And shuts me from the sight I most adore;
In dreams thou dost my dearest truth display,
Yet waking, robs me what I dreamed before.
Why give such taste of blessing to the tongue,
Only to turn and mock with empty hand?
Is love to fools or spirits only sung,
That mortal hearts can never understand?
Yet still I chase thy mist through midnight’s glass,
And pray tomorrow brings the dream again;
For though it fade as fleeting shadows pass,
Its echo lives within my constant pain.
I’d rather dream of love that never stays,
Than live one hour in unenchanted days.
