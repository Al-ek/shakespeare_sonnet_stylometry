XLV

The merchant counts his gain by mortal pence,
The prince his crown by homage vast yet vain;
But I, possessed of thy rich excellence,
Need measure not, for mine transcends his gain.
Each look of thine is coin beyond compare,
Each smile a kingdom none on earth could buy;
The poorest hour with thee grows sovereign rare,
And wealth itself must blush where thou art nigh.
O greedy world, how small thy triumph seems,
Where love bestows what treasure cannot boast!
Thy golden age is poor beside my dreams,
Whose heaven lies upon one mortal host.
I count thy touch as worth all worlds combined,
And leave the earth to seek its soul in thine.
