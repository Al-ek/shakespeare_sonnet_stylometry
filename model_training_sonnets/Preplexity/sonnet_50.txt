L

The chime, at even tolling soft and low,
Doth count the heartbeats of a world in rest;
I stand and watch the solemn moments flow,
And think of thee, whom time hath still caress’d.
For though the bell’s deep mourning speaks of age,
Its echo hums as lullaby to me;
Each sound repeats as if from wisdom’s page,
That peace abides where truth and love agree.
The hour may pass, yet never hast thou gone,
Thy memory outlives the passing tone;
And while the night renews the fading dawn,
Thy name, like light, endures though flesh be flown.
I bless the sound that marks each fleeting year,
For every toll recalls thee always near.
