XXI

When twilight weaves its silver o’er the sea,
And weary stars unpin their golden flame,
My heart turns back to thine, eternally,
Though distance mocks the bond it cannot tame.
The tide forgets not moonlight’s faithful hand,
Nor shall my soul forget thy whispered name;
Though mortal dust obeys the world’s demand,
Love writes its oath beyond all earthbound shame.
The lilies fade, their fragrance left behind,
A ghost that lives though bloom and stem decay;
So with my thought of thee — though frail decline’d,
Still breathes its beauty through each passing day.
For death may hush the world and all its art,
But not the pulse that lingers in my heart.
