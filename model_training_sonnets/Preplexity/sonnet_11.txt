XI

The wind may boast it shapes the bending trees,
And seas proclaim their power against the land,
Yet greater force than these commands with ease
The yielding heart that love alone can strand.
No storm so fierce, no tide so strongly driven,
As that which moves the soul toward its delight;
It sweeps aside all barriers earth has given,
And claims its course as both its will and right.
Thus am I borne upon thy gentle sway,
A captive glad to follow where thou lead’st,
Nor wish I freedom from that sovereign way,
For in such bonds the truest freedom breeds.
Let nature rage; her strength is but a part
Of that more mighty tempest in the heart.
