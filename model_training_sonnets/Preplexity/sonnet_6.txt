VI

The night conspires with silence to reveal
What daylight hides beneath its busy guise;
In darkness truths more softly make appeal,
And whisper what the waking world denies.
Then do I feel thy absence most severe,
When all is still and nothing bids me stray,
For in that hush thy voice seems drawing near,
Though thou art leagues and lifetimes far away.
O strange deceit, that loss should so increase
The sense of presence stronger than before,
As if the heart, denied its outward peace,
Creates within what it had sought in store.
Thus distance serves but love’s deep paradox:
It binds more fast while seeming to unfix.
