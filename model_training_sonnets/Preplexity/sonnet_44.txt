XLIV

The winter came and took our roses hence,
Its breath a sword that froze the tender stem;
Yet still within my thought the garden dense
Abides intact — the bloom still bright as gem.
For love doth outlive season, storm, and frost,
And kindly mocks the ruin-time would show;
Each fallen leaf marks not what’s truly lost,
But writes our constancy in silent snow.
The walls are bare, the paths with shadow met,
No bird remains to charm the vacant bower;
Yet in the void my heart can ne’er forget,
The echo sweet of that remembered hour.
So let the fields be gray, the skies untrue,
Love’s spring abides in every thought of you.
