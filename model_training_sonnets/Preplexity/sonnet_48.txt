XLVIII

Why should I fear the mocking tongue of fame,
Whose breath is fraud, whose laurel shuns the just?
It builds with sound the monuments of shame,
And leaves the living virtue in the dust.
Let others yearn for echo loud and vain,
For marble pride that time shall soon consume;
Thou art the truth that none may yet disdain,
A bloom that none can press to wither’s doom.
For fame grows faint when wind forgets to roar,
But love stands bright when silence takes the field;
And what endures when clamour rings no more,
Save hearts whose faith is all their chosen shield?
The greatest name beneath the mortal sun,
Is love that lives when reasons are undone.
