So often have I dreamed thy gentle face,
That waking seems a lesser, faded thing;
Thy memory leaves too bright, too strong a trace,
And reason bends beneath imagining.
If dreams be false, then let me falsehood keep,
For what is real when shadow feels so sweet?
The heart remembers what the eyes may weep,
And makes illusion’s gold in sorrow’s heat.
So though the morn may bid my fancy flee,
And daylight claim the rights of sober men,
I’ll hold my dreams as truth enough for me,
And build thy smile again and yet again.
For love unseen still dwells where vision ends,
And fools are wise who cherish phantom friends.
