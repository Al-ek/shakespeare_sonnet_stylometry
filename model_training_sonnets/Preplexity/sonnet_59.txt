LIX

The wise seek balance, temper’d by the day,
And call it peace to dwell in moderate reign;
But love, too vast, no limit can obey,
Nor learn to curb the tide it would sustain.
Let caution speak with quiet scholar’s breath,
And tell of measured passion’s proper course;
Still shall my heart outbid the fear of death,
And rush like river seeking sky’s great source.
I know not thrift in gifts of tenderness,
Nor would I hoard the joy that freely came;
To love in full, though life may love the less,
Is virtue’s height though folly holds the blame.
Restraint may guide, yet boldness builds above,
For wisdom ends where glory starts in love.
