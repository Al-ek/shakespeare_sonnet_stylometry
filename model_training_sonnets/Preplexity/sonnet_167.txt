XIII

My pen grows weary, yet my thought still burns,
As if the ink itself were my true soul;
For every line some hidden heart returns,
And from its cage the silent doves unroll.
Should words grow faint, then let their spirit stay,
Alive in those who read with hearts unsealed;
For poetry, though soft, can still convey
The might of all that time has yet concealed.
So if the dawn find me with verse unmade,
And night descend before my rhyme is done,
Know this—that glory is not soon repaid,
But rests in struggle ere the work is won.
The poet’s task is endless as the sea,
And each new word a deep eternity.
