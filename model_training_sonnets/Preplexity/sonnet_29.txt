XXIX

I walk’d among the dead of ancient years,
Their epitaphs a whisper half forgot;
Each name once bright in joy, or dimmed in tears,
Now equal lies beneath the selfsame lot.
And pondering thus how swift all glories fade,
I almost cursed the tender love I bore;
But then thy thought within my soul was laid,
And all the tombs grew blossom’d as before.
For what is loss but promise in disguise,
That souls shall meet when matter’s reign has end?
So bloom these graves anew before mine eyes,
As if their dust through memory did mend.
If love must die, let death in mercy prove,
That dying lives forevermore in love.
