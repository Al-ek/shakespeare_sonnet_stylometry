XLII

O Memory, thou tyrant sweet and strong,
That chains me willing to what’s long expired;
Thou keep’st my joy alive though mourned so long,
And feeds my flame when reason’s cold, retired.
I plead for mercy, yet I hold thee fast,
For in thy thrall I taste the ghost of spring;
Thy echo brings the past where hope was cast,
And all the loss becomes a living thing.
How cruel-love, that never dies but dreams,
And breathes new life to grief with every sigh!
Yet bless’d the wound whose healing softly gleams,
As proof that souls can never truly die.
So bind me still, thou kindly-torturing power,
To live in yesterday’s unending hour.
