XXXIII

As pilgrim foot grows sore by distant mile,
Yet stronger longs the heart toward holy place,
So I through wearied time endure a while,
Till faith beholds again thy blessed face.
The road is rough, yet sweetened by the dream,
That every step draws nearer thy repose;
No pain so sharp but love shall dull its beam,
And make fruition perfume of its woes.
Then shall we meet beyond this crossing plain,
Where thought is action, and where joy is law;
And earthly partings count no more as pain,
For flesh shall yield to spirit free of flaw.
O hasten, hour, thou slow-retarded grace,
That ends all loss in one complete embrace.
