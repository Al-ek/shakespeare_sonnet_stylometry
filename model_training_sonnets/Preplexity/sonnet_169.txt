XV

When envy bites upon another’s fame,
And bitter tongues betray the heart’s decay,
I pity more the curser than the blamed,
For hate consumes the hour it would dismay.
What joy to mock the sun when one is cold,
Or scorn the feast one never dared to taste?
Their own despair defines their envy’s hold,
And turns their hope to satire, youth to waste.
Then let me rather bless the deeds of men,
And find my worth in what I can commend;
For praise once given comes back home again,
And envy dies where love becomes a friend.
One candle lights another’s steadfast glow,
Yet none grows dim for sharing what they know.
