XXXV

No constant wave but shapes a changing shore,
No steady flame but dances as it burns;
So hearts must shift, though rooted evermore,
And learn through change the love that still returns.
I mourn not when thy moods, like seasons, range,
For even tempests cleanse the clouded air;
And growth is born through all that seems but change,
As winter’s frown prepares the summer fair.
Let others seek a stillness cold and dead;
I crave the life that trembles, moves, as thine.
Though tides may ebb, their faith is never fled,
They kiss again the land, and call it mine.
Change is the breath by which true love endures,
The pulse that keeps affection swift and pure.
