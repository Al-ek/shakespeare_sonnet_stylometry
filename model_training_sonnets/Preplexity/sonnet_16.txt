XVI

The voice of doubt may whisper soft and low,
And bid me guard against what I might lose,
Yet love replies with strength it does not show,
And leaves no space for fear or for excuse.
For what is loss when all is freely given?
And what is risk when nothing is withheld?
In such a state the soul is nearer heaven
Than when by caution it is tightly held.
Thus do I choose the path that love demands,
Though reason warn of dangers yet to be,
For better far to fall by willing hands
Than stand untouched yet void of ecstasy.
And if I fall, I fall where I would stand:
Within the shelter of thy gentle hand.
