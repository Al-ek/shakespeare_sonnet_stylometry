III

When silence speaks more sweet than any song,
And absence hangs like perfume on the air,
Then love in secret weaves its thread so strong,
The heart grows captive though no chains are there.
No eye hath seen the blush that longing paints,
No ear the sigh that steals from hidden tongue;
Yet all the heavens know their whispered saints,
Whose prayers renew the old when minds are young.
So if thou think’st my spirit far away,
Know distance binds as much as it divides;
The stars themselves keep lovers from decay,
Their burning proof in endless dusk resides.
The world may part what hands would make complete,
But souls once joined, not even death may cheat.
