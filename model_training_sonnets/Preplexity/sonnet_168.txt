XIV

The summer once did promise endless peace,
But autumn claimed her right of quiet death;
The golden fruit gave way to bare increase,
And frost replaced the sun’s forgiving breath.
So changes all, and all that’s dear must change,
For time is lord of motion and of rest;
Yet in that dance, so cruel yet so strange,
Our grief is tempered, and our loss impressed.
Thus shall I bless the turning of the year,
For in decline new bloom prepares to rise;
What falls in soil returns without our tear,
And death itself renews the paradise.
The falling leaf doth whisper as it goes,
That ending serves the seed from which it grows.
