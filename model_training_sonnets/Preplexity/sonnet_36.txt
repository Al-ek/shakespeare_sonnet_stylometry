XXXVI

Though age shall paint its frost upon thy hair,
And silver every sunlight of thy youth,
I’ll love thee then, as now, more deep, more fair,
For beauty ripens when it leans on truth.
The rose too rash is quickly overblown,
But autumn’s bloom lives longer, heart to heart;
And though bright cheeks by passing time are known,
Their grace remains in every tender part.
So fear not mirrors that betray decay,
For mirrors lie where constancy abides;
The soul, once seen, no wrinkle can betray,
It walks unshadow’d by the hour that chides.
Let hush of years adorn thee, not destroy,
Age crowns affection with enduring joy.
