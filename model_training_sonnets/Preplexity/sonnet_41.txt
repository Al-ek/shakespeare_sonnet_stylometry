XLI

The gentle brook that winds through meadows green,
Reflects thy grace as sky within its breast;
And all that’s fair in nature seems but seen,
To honour thee, whom beauty hath confess’d.
Each blossom bends as though to pay thee prayer,
The breeze doth whisper, “She alone is known”;
While even noon, too proud for soft repair,
Grows mild beneath thy splendour’s tempered tone.
If gods did walk once more among the fields,
They’d wear thy semblance for their dear disguise;
Since mortal form no holier image yields,
Than thy meek glory seen through mortal eyes.
The world adorn’d would scarce need heaven’s art,
If heaven itself could boast so pure a heart.
