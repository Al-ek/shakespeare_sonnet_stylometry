XVIII

When silence falls between thy heart and mine,
It speaks more loud than any words could frame;
For in that hush a truth begins to shine,
Unburdened by the need of spoken name.
No language then is needed to convey
The depth of feeling shared in quiet gaze,
For what is felt admits no grand display,
Nor seeks the fleeting praise that speech might raise.
Thus do we find within that gentle space
A fuller bond than words could e’er impart,
Where soul meets soul in unadorned embrace,
And knows itself reflected in the heart.
So let the world in ceaseless clamor move:
We speak most truly when we do not prove.
