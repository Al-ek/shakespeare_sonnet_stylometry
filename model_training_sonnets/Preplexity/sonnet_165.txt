XI

The city hums, yet none can hear the strain
That lovers sing beneath the crush of feet;
Their whispered vows dissolve in summer rain,
But secret fire survives in crowded heat.
Among the noise, two eyes may form a world,
And one sweet glance a kingdom undefined;
Though life be swift, its banners still unfurled,
Where hearts defy the armies of the mind.
So let them pass, the multitudes of care,
Each chasing ghosts through alleys paved in gold;
One breath of love makes all the air more fair,
And fills the soul when days are sad and cold.
For though the world forget our fleeting time,
A single spark redeems the whole sublime.
