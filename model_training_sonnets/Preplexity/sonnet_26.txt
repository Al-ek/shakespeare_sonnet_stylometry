XXVI

No painter’s hand could match thy fleeting hue,
Nor poet’s scroll contain such boundless grace;
For truth, that shyly hides from mortal view,
Finds plainest dwelling in thy mortal face.
When first I saw thee, time forgot its course,
And all the world seemed held in breathless span;
I felt creation stir its secret source,
As if begun anew in sight of man.
Now every meadow sings thy whispered name,
And every wind recalls thy sighing tone;
If I be dust, then dust shall crave the same,
To cling to thee and never be alone.
For life is but the echo of thy voice,
That bids the soul remember why rejoice.
