XXXVII

How fondly do I watch the morning grow,
And count each hour that leads my gaze to thee;
As sailor strains where brighter currents flow,
Though lost at times upon the misted sea.
Each beam that breaks upon thy hidden door,
Is wealth to me that kings could never own;
Though wanting speech, my heart demands no more,
Than sight of thee to feel I’m not alone.
So may I stand, as hill beneath thy sun,
Content to glow though silent as a stone;
Thy warmth bestows all honours to be won,
And shines through me as mine were but its own.
O patient heart, endure thy light apart,
To live for love is brighter than to start.
