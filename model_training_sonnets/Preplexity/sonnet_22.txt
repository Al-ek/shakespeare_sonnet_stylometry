XXII

Thy voice did season silence like the spring,
And made the barren air a choir divine;
Each word a bud, each pause a murmuring,
Where hope took root and dared at last to shine.
How oft did grief unmake my steadfast soul,
Till thou didst dress it new in sweeter guise!
What surgeon’s art could heal such heart made whole
Save gentle speech beneath thy patient eyes?
Yet time, that thief, unclasps bright hands too soon,
And steals our hours to fill his hungry grave;
But I, who loved beneath the yielding moon,
Shall ransom love, though none the debtor save.
For every fleeting breath thy lips have spent,
Records itself in mine as testament.
