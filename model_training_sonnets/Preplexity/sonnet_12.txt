XII

What once I feared I now embrace with ease,
And what I sought I scarcely now require;
For love has taught me how the soul can please
Itself by yielding wholly to desire.
No measured caution tempers now my will,
No guarded thought restrains what I would prove;
I find a deeper wisdom in the thrill
That comes of trusting wholly in my love.
Thus reason bows to something more refined,
A truth no rigid rule could e’er contain,
Where heart and soul in harmony combined
Transform what once seemed folly into gain.
So stand I changed, yet more myself than ever,
By losing all, I find myself forever.
