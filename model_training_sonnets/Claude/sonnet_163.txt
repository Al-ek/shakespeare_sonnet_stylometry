IX

Is it for fear to wet a widow's eye
That thou consumest thyself in single life?
Ah, if thou diest childless, then must cry
The world itself, as though it were thy wife.
For beauty's waste is mourned by all that see it,
The unploughed field, the candle never lit,
And all who witness loveliness agree it
Should leave some offspring worthy of the wit.
A widow's tear may dry before the morrow
And grief may find another face to rest on,
But beauty's loss bequeathed to common sorrow
Leaves no one living with a fairer breast on.
  Then wed thyself to time and time's desire,
  Or leave the world one candle short of fire.
