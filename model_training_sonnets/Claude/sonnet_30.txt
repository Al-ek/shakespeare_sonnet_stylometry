XXX

When I am gone and scattered into dust,
And all this flesh hath rendered its account,
When what I was is claimed by what it must,
And I am nothing more than memory'sount,
Then look not for me in the churchyard cold,
Among the marble and the weeping stone,
But find me in the verses growing old,
That loved thee better than mere blood and bone.
For what is flesh but temporary lease
On something that was never truly mine?
The soul that loved thee begs no stone for peace,
But lives hereafter in each finished line.
  Seek not for me where hollow silence rings,
  But here, where love hath lent the language wings.
