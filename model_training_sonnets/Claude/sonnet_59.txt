LIX

If I have in some earlier and greener time
Bestowed my heart on one who wore thy face
And set my youthful hand to careless rhyme
In some forgotten, undeserving place,
Forgive that younger man his wasted fire,
He did not know the thing he was rehearsing,
He thought he held the full of his desire
And knew not the true text he was versing.
For all my previous loving was but draft,
The rough and groping study of a form
I had not mastered yet, nor known the craft
Of setting down the genuine and the warm.
  Those loves were but the scales a student plays—
  In thee alone I found the song to praise.
