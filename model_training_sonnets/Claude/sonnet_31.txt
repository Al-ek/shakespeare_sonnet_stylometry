XXXI

How many times have I rehearsed this speech
Within the theatre of my own dark mind?
And every time the words stay out of reach,
And leave me dumb with feeling undefined.
For what the heart conceives in its full flood
Cannot be carried whole to tongue and air,
It loses something in the transit—blood
Becomes mere water by the time we're there.
So take this pale ambassador I send,
These verses limping toward what words can't hold,
And know that what they mean is without end,
And what they cannot say was also told.
  Between the lines where silence makes its home,
  There lives the love that language cannot own.
