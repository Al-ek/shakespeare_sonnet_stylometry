XVII

Who will believe my verse in times to come
If it contain the wonders that thou art,
And future ages, disbelieving, thumb
These pages, calling me a poet's fraud?
Though heaven knows my verse is as a tomb
Which hides thy life, and shows not half thy worth,
If I could write the beauty of thy bloom
The age to come would say I lied on earth.
So should my lines, rehearsed by future tongues,
Be scorned as old men's flattery, raving verse,
The fading echo of spent poet's lungs
Who praised the beautiful and cursed the hearse.
  But were some child of thine to read this art,
  Thou'dst live in lines, and in a living heart.
