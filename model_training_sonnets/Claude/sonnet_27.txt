XXVII

I have been absent when thou needed me,
As stars are absent in the face of day,
Not gone, but blotted by vicinity
Of brighter urgencies that held my way.
Forgive the sun for hiding what it hides
Not out of malice but its sovereign light,
Forgive the tide for following its tides,
And me for failing thee this single night.
For absence is not always cold intent,
And silence is not always kin to scorn,
Sometimes the man who loves is merely spent,
And crawls to rest before another morn.
  Judge me not by the empty hours past,
  But by the love that holds and means to last.
