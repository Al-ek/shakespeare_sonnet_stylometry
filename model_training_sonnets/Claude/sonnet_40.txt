XL

Now as the years have settled into grace
And passion's fire hath found its longer burn,
I find thee more beloved in this place
Than when my heart had first begun to learn.
For early love is rich with trembling awe
But poor in knowledge of the thing it holds,
It loves the surface and mistakes the raw
For depth, before the deeper depth unfolds.
But I who now have read thee like a book
Worn soft with turning in my daily hand,
Who know each page by its familiar look
And love thee better now I understand—
  Time hath not robbed this love of any light,
  But made it see thee clearer in its night.
