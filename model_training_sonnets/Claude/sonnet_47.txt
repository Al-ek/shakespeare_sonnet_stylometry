XLVII

Thou art the argument I cannot win,
The case I plead and every time concede,
I enter with my evidence and chin
Held high, and leave with nothing but my need.
For reason builds its tower fine and straight
But love doth undermine the very ground,
And all my logic staggers at the gate
Of one plain look from thee, without a sound.
I am no worse philosopher for this,
Nor less a man for laying down my case,
For there are truths beyond what argument is,
That reason reaches not, but feeling, grace.
  The wisest thought I've had since knowing thee
  Was knowing that thou art too wise for me.
