III

Look in the glass and tell me what you see,
A face that time hath fashioned out of light,
A form the years have shaped so tenderly
The very stars do pause to mark the sight.
Yet glass can only show the present face,
It cannot hold the image once you're gone,
And when thy beauty fades without a trace,
What evidence of thee shall be passed on?
Thy mother, looking on thy face today,
Doth see her own youth mirrored in thine eyes,
And when thou art as old and worn as clay,
So shall thy child behold thee with surprise.
  Renew thyself in one who bears thy name,
  Lest all thy beauty perish like a flame.
