XIV

Not from the stars do I my judgment pluck,
Nor could I read the heavens' lettered face,
Nor prophesy of harvests, blight, or luck
By reading what the constellations trace.
Yet from thine eyes my learning hath proceeded
And thou hast been my zodiac and chart,
Thy brow the almanac I have most heeded,
Thy voice the compass steady at my heart.
But in thine eyes this constant truth I find:
That beauty paired with virtue bears true treasure,
While beauty stripped of goodness leaves behind
A fairness short of its own truest measure.
  Foretell thee this, and let the stars say so:
  Truth beauty keeps, and beauty's where truths go.
