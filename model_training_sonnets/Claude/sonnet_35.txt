XXXV

The world grows loud with those who know the most
And silence all the room with their own noise,
Who summon certainty like a trusted ghost
And drown the careful thought in the din of voice.
But wisdom that I've gathered from thy side
Is quieter than these loud professors make,
It does not boast but rather stands aside,
And speaks only when something true's at stake.
I've learned more from the patience of thine eye
Than from the volumes stacked in learned halls,
More from the steadiness of thy reply
Than from the echo of proud marbled walls.
  The truest knowledge wears a humble face,
  And I found mine in thy most quiet grace.
