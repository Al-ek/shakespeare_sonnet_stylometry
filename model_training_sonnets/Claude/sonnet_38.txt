XXXVIII

When fortune smiled, thou wert the sweeter still,
And when she frowned, no sweeter thing than thee,
Through every summit and each lesser hill
Thou wert the single constancy I'd see.
I have watched lesser loves collapse in rain,
And friendships that fair weather found so sound
Dissolve the moment hardship showed its face,
And leave the sufferer on colder ground.
But thou hast been the rare and rugged vine
That clings the closer when the storm blows hard,
Whose root hath deepened past the frost's design,
And turns its face to spring from winter scarred.
  In thee I've found what proverbs only claim—
  A love that burns the same through sun and flame.
