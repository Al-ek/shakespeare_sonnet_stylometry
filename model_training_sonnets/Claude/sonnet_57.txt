LVII

The art of leaving well I never learned,
And goodbyes find me clumsy as a child,
I fumble at the door for what I've earned
In parting words, and give thee something mild.
I mean to say the grandest of all things
And end by speaking of the morning cold,
I reach for what the deepest feeling brings
And bring thee something ordinary and old.
Perhaps the leaving is its own confession
Of all the love too large to pass the gate,
That backs away in wordless dispossession
And lets the silence carry all its weight.
  Then read my awkward goodnights as they are—
  The speechless love that cannot hold them far.
