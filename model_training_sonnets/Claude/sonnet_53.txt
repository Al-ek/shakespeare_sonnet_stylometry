LIII

When I was young I thought that love would come
In lightning flash and overwhelm the sense,
That trumpets would precede it, and a drum,
And all would clarify in an instant dense.
But thou crept in like light beneath a door,
Gradual and quiet, easily missed,
And I looked up one day and loved thee more
Than all the sudden storms I'd catalogued and kissed.
The loves that strike like lightning burn as brief,
And leave the scorched earth wondering where they went,
But this slow-entering, soft, and patient grief
Of loving thee hath known no such descent.
  Beware the love that comes in too much haste—
  The best ones grow too slowly first to taste.
