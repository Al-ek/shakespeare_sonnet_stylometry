VIII

Music to ears attuned with love is sweet,
Yet thou who lovest music art not glad,
Why dost thou turn from what should make thee beat
With joy the pulse that nature always had?
If concords please thee, why then dost thou choose
To be a single note upon the air?
For in a chord, no instrument doth lose
Its voice, but each one rings beyond compare.
Thou art the eldest string upon the lute,
The melody that leads the other parts,
Yet thou art silent and the lute is mute,
That should be playing to a thousand hearts.
  One note is sweet, but harmony is more,
  Thou art the song the world is waiting for.
