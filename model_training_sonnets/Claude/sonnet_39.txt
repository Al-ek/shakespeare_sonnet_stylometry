XXXIX

Think not that I shall tire of praising thee
Because the theme grows long with passing years,
For love renewed finds endlessly to see,
And wonder is not wasted where it wears.
The sky gives not apology for dawn
Because the sun hath risen there before,
Nor doth the nightingale conceal its song
For having sung the self-same notes in store.
So I return again with willing pen
To the same face, the same beloved ground,
To map thee as the cartographer again
Who draws the known and loves each contour found.
  Forgive the poet his repeated theme—
  He hath no other subject worth his dream.
