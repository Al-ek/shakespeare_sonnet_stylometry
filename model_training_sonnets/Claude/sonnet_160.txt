VI

Then let not winter's frost undo the rose
Before the alchemist hath drawn its essence,
For beauty hid in cold and silence goes
To ruin without offering its presence.
What use is fairness kept in idle store,
Forbidden to the eye that aches to see it,
A shuttered house upon a gilded shore
Where no glad visitor may come to free it?
Make use of time before the lease runs out,
Before the landlord reclaims what he lent thee,
For beauty's tenure is beyond all doubt
The shortest gift that heaven ever sent thee.
  Lend out that loveliness ere winter's chill
  Forecloses on the beauty time may still.
