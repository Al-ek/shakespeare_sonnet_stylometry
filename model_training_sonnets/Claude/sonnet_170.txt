XVI

But wherefore do not mightier means than verse
Give battle to the ravage of the years,
Which mightier far might beauty's fall reverse
And leave the world fewer cause for tears?
The painted portrait or the sculptor's art
May better body forth thy likeness true,
And outward forms do better play the part
Of keeping still the freshness of thy hue.
Yet neither brush nor chisel can convey
The living sweetness of thy living breath,
Nor hold the warmth that crumbles to decay
When time at last delivers beauty's death.
  Then let thy living self make living art,
  And give the world another living heart.
