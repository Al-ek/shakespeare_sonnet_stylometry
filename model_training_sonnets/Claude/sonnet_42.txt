XLII

I do not love thee for the obvious things,
Though obvious enough thou art to charm
The eye of any man that beauty stings
And sets the idle heart to sweet alarm.
I love thee for the motion of thy thought
When something catches in thy working mind,
For what thy silence holds and leaves untaught,
For what thy laughter leaves a step behind.
I love thee for the weight of what thou'st borne
And how that bearing never bent thy grace,
For every morning after every torn
And ragged night that left no visible trace.
  The surface thou dost wear is fine enough,
  But it is all beneath that makes thee love.
