XVI

But wherefore do not thou a mightier way
Make war against the harvest-time of age?
The ink that dries upon the written page
Cannot so truly hold thy summer's day
As the fair garden of thy body might,
Which life may copy with a living pen,
More richly drawn than any verse I write,
And more alive to all beholding men.
These lines I paint are but a shadow-craft,
An old man's effort in a fading art,
But life that springs from love's own living shaft
Outlives the pen and the impoverished heart.
  Give life to life, and leave behind a print
  More fair than any verse could dare to hint.
