XXIV

What strange arithmetic is love, that adds
By taking, and by giving grows the less?
My heart, once whole, now walks in willing shreds,
And calls this ruin its own happiness.
I gave thee all I owned and felt increased,
I spent myself and found myself more full,
And in the lavish giving was released
From all the selfish coils that once did pull.
This paradox doth mock the merchant's craft,
Who hoards to gain and counts himself the wise,
Yet he who spends himself shall find, when daft
Economics fail, love's truer prize.
  For he who keeps his heart shall find it thin,
  But he who gives it gains the world therein.
