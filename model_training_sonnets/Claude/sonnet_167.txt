XIII

O that thou wert thyself, but, love, thou art
No longer thine own once the lease expires,
And when the hour of reckoning parts
From all the beauty that the eye admires,
What acceptable audit canst thou leave
When time demands the balance of thy gift?
To die without a son is but to grieve
The empty world thou leavest adrift.
Beauty's bequest requireth an executor,
Some child to be the steward of that store,
A worthy heir to be the prosecutor
Of all the fairness thou wert fashioned for.
  You had a father: let thy son proclaim,
  That thou wert born, and let him wear thy name.
