LVI

I have been jealous of the common air
That passes freely where my passage fails,
That moves through every curl of thy bright hair
And follows where my devotion pales.
I've envied books that occupy thine eyes
For hours I might have been the one to hold,
The songs that find in thee their readier prize
And tell thee things I've never yet been bold.
What foolishness the jealous lover spends
On rivals that are neither flesh nor will,
For air is only air when daylight ends,
And books fall closed, and songs at last go still.
  But I endure past air and song and page—
  A living love outlasts each rival's stage.
