LVIII

Not in the feast nor at the table set
With all the pomp of celebrated days,
Not at the height of life's most golden yet
Nor when the crowd hath turned to offer praise—
But in the ordinary failing hour,
When hope hath run to its last quarter turn,
When wit abandons and the will grows sour
And even patience has no more to burn—
It is in this most unremarkable place
That love declares what love is really made of,
Not candles and the feast's well-lighted face,
But what remains when all such things have faded.
  The love that holds in failure and in drought
  Is the only love worth singing about.
