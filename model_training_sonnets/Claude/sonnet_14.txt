XIV

Not from the stars do I my judgment glean,
Though I confess I sometime look above
To read what futures in their courses mean
And ask them what they prophesy of love.
But when I look upon thine eyes alone
I read far more than any star can write,
For in those eyes is summer wholly known,
And in their dimming, winter's cold is bright.
Thy beauty is the prophecy I trust,
Above the orbits of the nightly sphere,
For stars must keep their courses as they must,
But beauty's course is all too briefly here.
  If from thine eyes truth's richness can be read,
  Let truth beget the beauty that they've bred.
