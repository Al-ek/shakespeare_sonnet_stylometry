I

When golden time doth wear his summer crown,
And fields lie rich with all their harvest's store,
Shall beauty's bloom be left to wither down
And leave the world no richer than before?
The rose that drinks the morning's silver dew
Must give its sweetness ere the petal falls,
Lest nature's art be lost to winter's view
And no sweet fragrance echo through cold halls.
Then let not youth be hoarded like a miser
Who locks his gold where no eye may behold it,
For time shall make thee of thyself no wiser
If thou dost clutch that beauty and withhold it.
  Give freely what the seasons freely lent,
  Or die in debt, thy fairness misspent.
