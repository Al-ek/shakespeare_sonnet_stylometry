XXXVI

If thou shouldst tire of me as seasons tire
Of their own long and labored overstay,
If summer wearies of its own bright fire
And autumn longs to give itself away,
Then go as gently as the light departs
When evening makes no argument with night,
Without the breaking scene that breaks two hearts,
But soft, as dusk concedes its last to light.
I would not hold thee fast against thy will,
For love constrained is love in name alone,
And I had rather suffer the long still
Than keep thee caged within my heart of stone.
  Let love be freely given, freely gone,
  And what remains was real and worth the moan.
