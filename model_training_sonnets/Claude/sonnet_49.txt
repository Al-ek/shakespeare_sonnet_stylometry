XLIX

When I have erred, as erring men must do,
And given reason for thy just complaint,
And all the wrong was plainly mine, and true,
And I was guilty without the shade of saint,
I ask not to be pardoned past the debt,
Nor that thou smooth the fault away too fast,
But only that the error not be set
As testimony that must always last.
For men are not the sum of their worst days,
Nor is the whole revealed by any part,
And love that cannot hold us through our grays
Hath only loved the easy of our heart.
  Condemn the act, if act deserves it well,
  But keep some room for more than this to dwell.
