XLV

My tongue hath flattered where it should have held,
And silence would have served thee better far,
I have with easy words the truth dispelled
And lit the room whilst hiding every scar.
Forgive the coward hiding in the kind,
Who chose thy comfort over thy true good,
Who dressed the wound and left the cause behind,
And praised where he in honesty not should.
Henceforth let me be surgeon, not the nurse,
Who tells thee plainly where the trouble lies,
Whose love declares itself in honest verse
And does not comfort thee with pretty lies.
  For what a friend conceals to spare thee pain
  Shall find thee unprepared when truth doth rain.
