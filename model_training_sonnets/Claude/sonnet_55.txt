LV

The famous and the mighty have their stones,
Their tombs befit their vast and swollen pride,
And kings rest well among their gilded bones
With marble witnesses on every side.
But thou, who hast no monument to stand
Against the wearing patience of the years,
Art kept alive by this my willing hand
That writes thee here past all of time's arrears.
What pyramid hath lasted past its fame?
What column stands unstirred by any crack?
But love set down in verse doth keep thy name
Beyond the breaking of the stone and back.
  So whilst a tongue remains to read this line,
  The world shall know a beauty that was thine.
