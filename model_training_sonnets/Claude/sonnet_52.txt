LII

Some poets write of love as though it were
A constant summer flooding without rest,
All heat and gold and never a demur,
One long and undivided sunlit fest.
But honest love hath autumn in its year,
And winter too, when warmth stays out of sight,
When neither speaks of what they feel from fear,
And days grow short and blue toward the night.
I will not paint for thee a truer false,
Nor promise what no season ever keeps,
But tell thee love survives its intervals
And finds the spring again as spring always creeps.
  The love worth having is the love that knows
  All weathers, and through every weather grows.
