VII

Lo, when the dawn first climbs the eastern hill
And casts its long and golden light abroad,
Each mortal eye doth gaze with eager will
Upon the brightened world and its reward.
But when the sun hath reached his highest throne
And pours his full meridian blaze around,
The very worship that the morning shone
Is hushed, and lesser awe doth more abound.
So thou, whose beauty crests the golden noon,
Shalt find the watching world grown somewhat cold,
For mortal admiration turns too soon
And gives its warmest glance to what is old.
  Unless thou beget one to take thy place,
  Thy setting sun shall find no mourner's face.
