V

Those hours that frame the day with gentle light
And keep the sundial turning toward the sun
Shall all too soon give passage to the night
And leave thee wondering how the bright hours run.
For time's swift hand doth paint and then doth blur,
And summer's warmth is borrowed, never owned,
The fairest flower must eventually stir
Its petals loose and find itself dethroned.
But in the child that bears thy gentle name
Thy spring returns when winter strips the tree,
And beauty lives and burns its sweetest flame
In those that rise to be what thou shalt be.
  Distil thy sweetness ere the season ends,
  And what time takes, through lineage time amends.
