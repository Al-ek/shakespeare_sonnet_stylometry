XV

When I consider every thing that grows
Holds but a moment in perfection's light,
And all that prospers under heaven knows
That glory is the prelude to the night;
And when I think how men are like the stage
Where every player struts his brief-lit part,
Then the conceit of time's relentless rage
Doth move to pity this poor human art.
Thy fair youth stands against the hand of age
In constant battle on the field of years,
And I, who love thee, turn another page
And write thee living past all grief and fears.
  I graft thee new when age hath worn thee thin,
  In this my pen, thy beauty doth begin.
