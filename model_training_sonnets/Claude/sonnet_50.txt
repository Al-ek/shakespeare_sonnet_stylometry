L

I am not brave in any soldier's way,
No battlefield hath known my trembling boots,
I have not stared down death in light of day
Nor followed glory past its shallow roots.
But I have loved thee when the loving cost,
Have stayed when easier men had found the door,
Have searched for thee when thou wert nearly lost
And crossed the hardest country to restore.
If this be coward's work, then mark me so,
And give the hero's laurel to the bold,
I'll wear my quiet courage where I go
And call it valor enough when I grow old.
  Not all the wars worth winning bear a drum—
  Some are fought in silence for the loved ones.
