X

For shame, deny that thou dost harbour love
For any self that would outlive its day,
Grant if thou wilt thou art beloved of
Too few to make thy dying worth delay.
Is it not plain? Doth not the silence ring
Of children absent, of the empty chair,
Of all the seasons spent not offering
The richness nature planted with such care?
Make war no longer on thy better self,
That self which would in future ages grow,
Pull beauty's gift down from the idle shelf
And spend it well before the cold winds blow.
  Change thy cold thoughts, and let a warmer mind
  Consider those who shall be left behind.
