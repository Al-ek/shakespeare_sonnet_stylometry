XII

When I do count the tolling of the clock
And mark the hours surrendered to the past,
And see the bold oak bending in the shock
Of years that strip the beauty from the mast,
When summer's wealth lies buried under frost
And the proud lark sings lower than before,
I wonder then at beauty tempest-tossed
And sailing toward that unforgiving shore.
Then of thy beauty ask: who stands to keep it
When time hath laid his scythe against the stem?
No chest of gold is safe enough to sleep it,
No wall so thick it shall not fall for them.
  Yet brave verse holds what all else lets decay,
  And gives thy beauty one more borrowed day.
