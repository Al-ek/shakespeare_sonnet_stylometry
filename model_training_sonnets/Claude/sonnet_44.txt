XLIV

I used to court the extraordinary,
And counted common hours as hours lost,
I found the daily world too ordinary
And paid no heed to what such blindness cost.
But thou hast been my schooling in the plain,
My education in the overlooked,
The sudden sweetness of an evening rain,
The grace of bread, the warmth of being cooked.
I am a different pupil now than then,
Who brings his full attention to the small,
Who knows that heaven is not found, but when
It comes, it comes through cracks in common walls.
  Teach me still the ordinary art—
  It is the rarest lesson of the heart.
