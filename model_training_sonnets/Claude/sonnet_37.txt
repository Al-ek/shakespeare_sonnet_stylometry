XXXVII

My rival comes with finer coat than mine,
With easier manners and a readier tongue,
He pours his praises out like Spanish wine
And sings the songs that I have left unsung.
I cannot match the polish that he wears,
Nor mimic the swift grace with which he speaks,
I bring thee only what my poor art dares,
And what sincerity most humbly seeks.
But know that where his brightness is the art,
Mine is the unadorned and honest thing,
He brings thee performance, I bring thee my heart,
And let thee judge what gift is worth the ring.
  For gilded words may catch the early eye,
  But naked truth is what holds love thereby.
