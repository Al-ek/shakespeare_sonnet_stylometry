XXVIII

The clock moves forward but my heart moves back
To where thou stood'st the morning we first met,
When all the world was verdant, naught was black,
And time had not yet dealt its first regret.
How young we were in all our ignorance,
How rich we were in all our want of art,
We stumbled into love by happy chance
And learned its grammar as we learned its heart.
Now older, wiser, heavier with years,
I would not trade this weight for younger days,
For all the knowledge purchased here with tears
Was worth the coin, and worth the fevered maze.
  The green of youth was sweet but quickly past,
  I'll take the autumn gold that learned to last.
