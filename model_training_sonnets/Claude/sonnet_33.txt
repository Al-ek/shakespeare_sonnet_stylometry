XXXIII

Beware the flatterer with honeyed tongue
Who paints thee fairer than the angels are,
Who strings his praises like a lute well strung
And makes thee all his verses' guiding star.
For flattery is hunger in disguise,
And he who lifts thee highest on his tongue
Hath measured out the distance for thy rise
And waits below to catch thee when thou'rt flung.
But I who love thee in thy imperfection,
Who note thy flaws and love thee none the less,
Whose devotion wants not thy correction
But only thee in thy true nakedness—
  Believe not him who calls thee without flaw,
  But trust the one who loves thee with each one he saw.
