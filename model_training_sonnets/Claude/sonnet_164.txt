X

For shame, deny that thou dost love not any,
Thou art so occupied with self's regard,
That murderous thoughts, where others cherish many,
Have built about thy heart a frozen guard.
Grant that thou wilt be taken by the earth,
As all must be who walk this mortal way,
At least provide the world with beauty's birth
Before thy beauty crumbles to decay.
Let that which shelters in thy pleasant face
Be more than only shelter for thyself,
Repair thy mind and give it a new grace
By lifting beauty off its lonely shelf.
  Make thee another self for love's sweet sake,
  That beauty may persist when thou dost wake.
