XIX

Devouring time, blunt thou the lion's paws
And make the earth devour her own sweet brood,
Pluck at the teeth of age with iron claws
And burn the phoenix in her ancient wood.
Make glad and sorry seasons as thou fleets,
And do what'er thou wilt, swift-footed time,
To the wide world and all her fading sweets,
And all that stands beneath the morning's prime.
But I forbid thee one most heinous wrong,
Carve not with thy swift hours my love's fair brow,
Nor draw the lines that make her young life long
With the rough hand of age and winter's vow.
  Yet do thy worst, old time, despite thy art,
  My love shall live in these lines of the heart.
