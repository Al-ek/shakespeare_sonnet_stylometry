XXI

When silver frost doth claim the morning bough,
And autumn strips the oak of verdant pride,
So time doth steal the bloom from beauty's brow,
And all that flourished once is set aside.
Yet in these lines I build thee halls of stone,
Where neither frost nor tempest shall intrude,
Where thou shalt reign immortal and alone,
Untouched by age's cruel solicitude.
For marble cracks and monuments must fall,
And brass corrodes beneath the weeping sky,
But verse endures beyond the final call,
And beauty lives where beauty's praises lie.
  So whilst I breathe and eyes have strength to see,
  This page shall hold what time would take from thee.
