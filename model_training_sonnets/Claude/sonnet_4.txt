IV

Unthrifty spender of the self's great store,
Why dost thou hoard what nature begs thee spend?
Beauty held tight will leak out through the door
And leave thee nothing but an empty end.
The miser hoards his gold behind a wall
And in the hoarding loses what he saves,
For when he dies his treasure pays for all
The worms that dine upon him in his grave.
So thou, who art the richest of thy kind,
Whose worth exceeds what numbers can contain,
If thou to leave no heir art so inclined,
Then all that nature lent she takes again.
  Lend out thy beauty's use, and nature's gift
  Shall give thee back a joy no gold can lift.
