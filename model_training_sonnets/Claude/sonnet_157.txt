III

Look in the glass and tell me what thou seest:
A borrowed face from some ancestor's store,
The very form that nature holdeth dearest,
Reproduced from some forgotten shore.
Thy mother in thee walks with borrowed grace,
Thy father's brow doth arch above thine eye,
And all thy kin do haunt that mirrored face
Like voices calling where no walls are nigh.
If thou withholdst the gift that nature gave thee,
Then nature's chain is broken at thy link,
And all the beauty that the ages gave thee
Shall perish where the unremembered sink.
  Be kind to time and time shall be so kind
  As leave some echo of thee still behind.
