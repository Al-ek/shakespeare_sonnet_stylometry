XLVI

There is a season between love and sleep
When all the day's hard clamour settles down,
And in that tender interval we keep
The gentlest hours the passing night hath known.
We talk of nothing much and everything,
The small report of what the hours held,
Some half-remembered air one of us sings,
The easy silence that is never felled.
This is the chamber where the truer bond
Is quietly and without fanfare sealed,
Not in the public declarations fond,
But here, in what the private hours revealed.
  The world applauds the grand and public vow,
  But love is built in evenings such as thou.
