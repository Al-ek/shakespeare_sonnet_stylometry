LX

So let this fortieth sonnet close the book
Upon these twenty verses and their toil,
And if thou find some worthy thing to look
Upon herein, then let it ease thy moil.
I make no claim to rival what was made
By greater hands in greater ages set,
Whose golden lines shall never dim or fade
Whilst lesser poets labour, stumble, fret.
But lesser love is love in full despite,
And smaller candles push against the dark,
And he who cannot make the sun still might
Set down a word that leaves its modest mark.
  Take then these lines for what they humbly are—
  Not sun, not moon, but love's more modest star.
