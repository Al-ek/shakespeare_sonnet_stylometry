XX

A face of perfect form by nature fashioned,
With every gentle grace and quality,
A heart with all of love's sweet compass rationed,
A spirit touched with rare divinity.
Thine eyes, more bright than any star's cold fire,
Do light the chamber of the soul within,
And all who look on thee must feel desire
Not base or low, but where true loves begin.
Nature first made thee mistress of my sighing,
Then added to thy soul so rare a part,
That I am left in admiration, trying
To find the measure of so large a heart.
  And since she gave thee all that I adore,
  She left me nothing, wanting nothing more.
