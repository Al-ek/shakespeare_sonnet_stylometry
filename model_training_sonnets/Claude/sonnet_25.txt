XXV

Let those who seek for love in courts of state
Pursue it through the halls of gilded power,
And let ambition serve as cupid's bait
For those whose hearts are measured by the hour.
But I require no throne to frame my joy,
No herald cry my passion to the street,
My love wants neither trumpet nor decoy,
But finds itself in quiet made complete.
A garden small, a table set for two,
A candle's trembling light to see thee near,
Thine eyes upon me and the evening dew—
These are the luxuries I hold most dear.
  Give kings their crowns and let the courtiers fawn,
  My heaven needs no more than thee and dawn.
