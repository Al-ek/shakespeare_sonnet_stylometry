XI

As swift as youth declines to middle age,
So middle age must fall to years and grey,
Therein is written beauty's final page,
And what was summer turns to winter's day.
But those that leave behind the gift of life
Do give to time a beauty that endures,
The child between a loving man and wife
Is nature's answer to what death obscures.
Let those whom nature did not bless with form
Be barren if they will, and so expire,
But thou wert made to weather every storm,
And thou wert made to fill the world with fire.
  Print beauty's copy ere the copy fade,
  And show the world the image that was made.
