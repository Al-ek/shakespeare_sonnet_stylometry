V

Those hours which shaped the loveliness of thee
And filled thine eyes with light as clear as stone,
Will work in time to undo what they made free
And leave thee altered, unremembered, lone.
For beauty once was summer's dearest guest
But summer does not linger past its time,
And autumn strips the branches of their best
And winter robs the garden of its prime.
But if the summer's perfume be distilled
And kept within a vial of careful art,
Then though the flower be with cold frost killed,
Its essence still may warm a winter heart.
  Make beauty's essence, leave thy living trace,
  Lest winter steal all record of thy face.
