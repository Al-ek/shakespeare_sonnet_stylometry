IV

Unthrifty loveliness, why dost thou spend
Upon thyself alone what all should share?
Beauty's account hath not a world to lend
When nature sends no interest to bear.
Thou art the steward of a costly treasure
Bequeathed to thee by seasons gone before,
And if thou spendst it solely for thy pleasure
The vault lies empty at the final shore.
Then traffic with thy gifts, lay out thy feature,
Give beauty's coin where it may bear some fruit,
For nature's bounty, squandered past all measure,
Leaves only silence where there once was lute.
  The miser dies no richer than the poor,
  And beauty buried beauty doth ignore.
