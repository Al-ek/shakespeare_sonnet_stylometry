XIX

Devouring time, blunt thou the lion's paws,
And make the earth devour her own sweet brood,
Pluck out the fangs of tigers without pause
And burn the long-lived phoenix where she stood.
Make glad and sorry seasons as thou wilt,
And do whate'er thou wilt to this wide world,
But I forbid thee one most wretched guilt:
Let not one line from beauty's page be furled.
O carve not with thine hours the lovely brow,
Nor draw no lines of age upon that face,
But in thy course let beauty's self endow
The coming world with all its present grace.
  Yet do thy worst, for in these lines I see
  That beauty lives, and lives eternally.
