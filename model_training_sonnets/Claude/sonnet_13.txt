XIII

O that thou wert thyself! But, love, thou art
No longer thine when once thy season ends,
Against this coming ruin of thy heart
Thou shouldst prepare, and make thyself amends.
So fair a house as thou dost well inhabit
Should not be left to fall in disrepair,
Thou shouldst not let the winter freely grab it
And strip the walls and strip the windows bare.
Who lets so fair a house stand all alone
And makes no effort for its careful keeping,
But leaves the garden to the weed and stone
While all within falls slowly into sleeping?
  Dear love, the lease upon thy beauty's due,
  And when it's passed, what shall remain of you?
