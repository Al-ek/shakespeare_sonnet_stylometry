IX

Is it for fear the world should mark thy change
That thou consumest beauty all alone?
That would be strange, and passing very strange,
For beauty shared is beauty more than shown.
The widow weeps for what she once possessed,
The widower mourns the house that once was full,
But thou, unwed, hast never known the best
Of what it is to feel love's gentle pull.
The world will mourn thee not for being lone,
But for the beauty wasted in thine hand,
The children never born, the seeds not sown,
The gifts returned to nature's open land.
  Look to thyself, and in thyself beget
  A world that shall not let thy face forget.
