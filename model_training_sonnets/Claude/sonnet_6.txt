VI

Then let thy beauty not lie cold in earth
Unwillingly, as a miser hides his gold,
But render back the treasure of thy worth
Before the tale of all thy years be told.
So shall thy beauty pass through beauty's door
And in thy children live another age,
Refracted tenfold from its primal store,
As music outlasts the voice that sang the stage.
What loss is beauty kept behind a lock?
What good is summer's bounty left unharvested?
The empty house ticks quiet as a clock,
No child to warm the rooms and give them rest.
  Beauty not shared is beauty badly spent,
  The gift not given mocks the giver's intent.
