VIII

Music, that silver tongue of sorrow's art,
Why dost thou smite me with such sweet complaint?
Thou art the very mirror of my heart,
Yet leave me stronger when I think me faint.
The single string that vibrates all alone
Sings only half the melody it could,
But strings in concert find a richer tone
That fills the hall with sound that's twice as good.
Thou art that solitary note untwinned,
Unmarried to the harmony thou'rt owed,
A melody that wanders with the wind
And has not found the voice to share its road.
  In union strings make more than what they are,
  And one lone note is less than one lone star.
