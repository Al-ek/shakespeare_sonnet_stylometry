XVII

Who will believe my verse in time to come
If it were filled with all thy beauty's worth?
Would the deaf ages hear, the future dumb
Not scorn such praise as overreaching mirth?
Should I describe thine eyes as bright as noon,
Thy lips as full as summer on the vine,
They'd say I spoke extravagance too soon,
And lay my verses with the poets' shrine.
But if some child of thine, grown old and tall,
Should live to read these pages I have writ,
He'd find the face immortal after all,
The beauty carried forward, every bit.
  Then live, beget, and let the future see
  What I could only touch imperfectly.
