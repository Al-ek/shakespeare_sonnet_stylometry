XXII

Thou art the sun that gilds my waking thought,
The warmth that breaks the cold of reason's night,
And every line I've penned, each verse I've wrought,
Was guided by the lantern of thy light.
How can I reckon worth in coin or land,
When all the world's abundance pales beside
One glance bestowed by thy most gracious hand,
One word that flows upon affection's tide?
The miser counts his gold with trembling joy,
The king his conquest measures, realm by realm,
But neither gold nor conquest can employ
A heart that thou alone dost overwhelm.
  In poverty of all else I am blessed,
  For I am rich who holds thee in his breast.
