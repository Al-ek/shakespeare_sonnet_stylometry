XVIII

Shall I compare thee to a summer's dawn?
Thou art more gentle and more softly made,
The morning mist is quickly swept and gone,
And every light of summer is afraid.
Sometimes too bright the eye of morning shines,
And often is its gilded thread unmade,
And every beauty past the twilight pines,
By chance or nature's turning, worn and frayed.
But thy soft sweetness shall not fade away,
Nor lose the grace that in thine aspect lives,
Nor shall dark autumn steal thy golden day
When in this verse thy lasting beauty thrives.
  So long as breath can read and eyes behold,
  So long lives this, and thee it holds enrolled.
