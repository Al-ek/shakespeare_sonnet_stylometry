XXVI

Why doth the rose not know its own perfume?
Or why doth beauty never see itself?
The lily grows indifferent to its bloom,
And sets its sweetness on no private shelf.
So thou, unknowing of the grace thou wear'st,
Dost move among us as a common thing,
While we who see thee are with wonder searest,
And stand like subjects before their sovereign king.
O teach me how to bear what thou dost bear
With such unconscious and becoming ease,
To move through time and never once be snared
By mirrors, nor by any will to please.
  For all that strains to be what nature gave
  Hath lost the gift before it cleared the cave.
