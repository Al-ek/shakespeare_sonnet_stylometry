VII

Behold the sun at the ascendant hour
As he climbs upward from the morning sea,
Each pilgrim eye pays tribute to his power,
And all the world attends his majesty.
But when at noon he tops the topmost stair,
And men look upward with unshaded eye,
The steepness of the path he now must bear
Pulls him at last down from the evening sky.
So in the morning of thy golden age
All eyes do follow where thy beauty bends,
But thou must write the final aging page
When daylight dwindles and the twilight ends.
  Without a heir to carry on the light,
  Thy setting sun shall fall to endless night.
