XX

A woman's face, with nature's own hand made,
Hast thou, though mastered first in love's own school,
Whose gentle heart has never been betrayed
By fashion's whim or vanity's blunt tool.
Thine eye, more bright than theirs, less false in rolling,
Gilding the object whereupon it stays;
A man in bearing, woman's grace controlling,
Doth steal men's eyes and women's hearts with grace.
And for a woman wert thou first created,
Till nature drawing thee fell soft in love
And by addition thee a mind donated
More richly furnished than the stars above.
  Since nature shaped thee thus for love's own sake,
  Let love repay the gift that she did make.
