XLIII

What is the distance between two who love
But measure only counted in the mind?
The body travels far as stars above
And yet the heart may leave the leagues behind.
I've stood beside thee and have felt thee gone,
And lain a hundred miles away and near,
For closeness is a thing we do, not don,
A practice, not the province of the mere.
Then let us not confuse proximity
For union, nor assume that distance severs,
The coldest bed holds more than you and me
If we have made ourselves each other's weathers.
  True distance is the silence that we keep
  When we lie close and cannot fall to sleep.
