XLI

The morning asks no payment for its light,
Nor doth the tide demand what shores it greets,
The stars exact no toll for guiding night,
And rain falls free upon the dusty streets.
So why do I account for every grace
That passes between us like a common breath,
As merchants tot their ledgers, case by case,
And weigh what lives against the cost of death?
Love is no commerce and the heart no vault
Where tenderness is locked in measured store,
It is the open field without a fault
Of fence or gate or well-considered door.
  Give freely then, and freely be received,
  For love in ledgers is love disbelieved.
