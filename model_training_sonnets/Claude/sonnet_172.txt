XVIII

Shall I compare thee to a summer's morning,
When mists lie soft across the river's face,
When birds send up their first and tender warning
That light is coming to its proper place?
Thou art more temperate and more composed
Than June's bright fever or July's full blaze,
More constant where the summer eye is dozed
And beauty nods through long and hazy days.
The rough winds of occasion shake thee not,
And life's complexion doth not fade in thee,
Nor lose possession of the warmth thou'st got,
Nor bow to chance or fortune's cold decree.
  So long as breath doth animate this art,
  So long shall live the beauty of thy heart.
