XXXII

I have loved thee in the minor keys of life,
Not only in the grand and gilded hour,
But in the daily texture, common strife,
The ordinary morning, the small bower.
I've loved thee in thy tiredness at day's end,
When beauty's lamp burns low but does not cease,
When thou hast nothing left of self to spend,
And all thou art is asking to find peace.
These are the hours when love declares its worth,
Not in the festival or easy grace,
But in the quiet corner of the earth
Where weariness seeks out the patient face.
  The love that serves thee only in thy shine
  Is not the love that I would call most mine.
