LI

The years do not diminish but refine,
As wine improves in darkness, kept from air,
And what was merely pleasant once turns fine
With time's peculiar and patient care.
I am not what I was when first we met,
The green hath deepened into something staid,
And yet the better for the passing, yet
More truly myself beneath the years' tirade.
Thou too hast changed and changed into the more,
And I have loved each version as it came,
The girl, the woman, opening like a door,
Each year a room that held a different flame.
  We are not what we were, and praise be given—
  We are the richer for the years we've lived in.
