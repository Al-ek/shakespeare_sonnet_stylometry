II

Those eyes that burn like candles in the night,
Which set my wandering heart upon its course,
Shall they grow dim and lose their amber light
As rivers thin and weaken at their source?
I fear not death, nor all his hollow scorn,
Nor winter's hand upon the greening bough,
But this I fear: that thou who wert this morn
Art less thyself than thou wert yesterday thou.
Yet love doth find in ruin something steady,
A constancy beneath the falling leaf,
And when the world is not, my heart is ready
To be thy monument against all grief.
  In these poor lines thy name shall find its keep,
  And beauty wake long after beauty's sleep.
