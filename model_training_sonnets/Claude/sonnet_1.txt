I

When morning breaks upon the frosted hill
And drives the dark from off the silver fen,
The world awakens, warm and wanting still
The touch of those who shall not come again.
So beauty, waking in thine eyes of grey,
Doth chase the shadows from my troubled breast,
Yet like the sun that burns and fades away,
Must thy fair face decline toward the west.
Then gather now what summer doth bestow,
Let not the season pass thee unobserved,
For winter cometh fast with ice and snow
To take what time hath carelessly reserved.
  Consume the hours while their sweetness stays,
  And in thy children, multiply thy days.
