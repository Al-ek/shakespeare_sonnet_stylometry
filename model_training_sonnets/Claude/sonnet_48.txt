XLVIII

Let not the mirror be thy morning's guide,
Nor let its cold opinion set the tone,
For mirrors flatten all that lives inside
And make thee but a surface, glass, and bone.
The face thou wear'st is but the outermost
Of what thou art, the thinnest of thy rings,
And what I love of thee I love thee most
Where no reflection reaches, hides, or clings.
Thou art the depth behind the looking glass,
The warmth the silver surface cannot hold,
The living breath that will outlast the brass
And silver frame when both are dark and cold.
  Then turn from what the mirror thinks of thee,
  And let me show thee what mine own eyes see.
