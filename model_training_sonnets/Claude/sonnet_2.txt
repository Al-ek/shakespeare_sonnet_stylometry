II

The rose that blooms in June doth not enquire
If it shall bloom again come following spring,
But spends its colour, scent, and sweet desire
Upon the air, a momentary thing.
So thou, whose cheeks do bear that rosy hue,
Whose lips do hold the fragrance of the dawn,
Shouldst lavish freely what was given you
Before the frost hath come and beauty's gone.
For nature doth not lend but to reclaim,
And all that's borrowed must at last be paid,
The face that once did set the heart aflame
Shall cool and wither slowly into shade.
  Give freely what thou hast while yet it lasts,
  Else beauty dies when beauty's season passes.
