XXIII

As one who steps upon a frozen lake
And hears the ice protest beneath his tread,
So do I fear each measured step I take
May crack the fragile ground on which we've fed.
For love is thin beneath the weight of truth,
And truth is sharp enough to split the seam
Between the tender promise made in youth
And what harsh daylight breaks of night's fond dream.
Yet onward still I press my careful foot,
For standing still were just as dangerous,
And cowardice hath never borne the fruit
That grows from crossing what would frighten us.
  Then let the ice give way, the cold be deep—
  No frozen shore is worth the warmth I seek.
