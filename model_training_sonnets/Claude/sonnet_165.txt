XI

As fast as thou shalt dwindle, so shall grow
In some new face the features that thou bearest,
And all the beauty that the years bestow
Shall be preserved in one thou holdst most dearest.
Herein lies wisdom, nature, and the art
Of those who love not selfishly but truly,
Who carry in their labours nature's heart
And keep the world replenished, whole, and newly.
Let those whose beauty nature hath not graced
Live out their seasons without issue's debt,
But thou art made too fairly to be erased
And nature would not have thee thus forget.
  She carved thee out with love's most patient care,
  Print her bequest in one as bright and fair.
