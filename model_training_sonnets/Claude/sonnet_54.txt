LIV

I think of all the words I never said,
The compliments that formed and were not spoken,
The tender things composed inside my head
And swallowed back before their seal was broken.
What cowardice attends the feeling man
Who will not let his sentiment be aired,
Who loves in full but runs away and can
Not bear to show the size of what he's cared?
Henceforth I mean to speak before the thought
Retreats into the corner where it hides,
To give thee what the moment freely brought
Before the coward in me it derides.
  I owe thee all the words I kept inside—
  This verse begins to pay that long-held tide.
