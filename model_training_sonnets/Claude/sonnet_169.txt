XV

When I consider every thing that grows
Holds in perfection but a little time,
And how the stage of this great world bestows
Its brief applause and then withholds the rhyme,
I see that men as plants increase and fade,
And wear their brave state out of memory,
And concord's cheering voices at the last jade
And leave the fairest things in vacancy.
Then in the strife against oblivion's art
I pledge my verse to fight on beauty's side,
And waging war upon all time apart,
I graft new life where beauty would have died.
  All that is thine, time takes with cruel art,
  Yet thine my verse shall hold against his heart.
