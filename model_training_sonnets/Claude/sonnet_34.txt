XXXIV

There is a kind of grief that has no grave,
No body to be mourned, no date to mark,
The loss of what we had not strength to save,
The guttered candle, not yet wholly dark.
I mourn a version of myself long spent,
A younger heart that dared without a net,
Who loved without the cold of discontent
And had not learned the grammar of regret.
Yet I would not restore that careless boy
Who knew not what he had until he lost,
For reckless love is rich in reckless joy
But counts not at the outset what it costs.
  Better this older love that knows the price,
  And pays it still, and pays it always twice.
