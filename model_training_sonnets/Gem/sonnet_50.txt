L

Upon the road of life I weary grow,
And count the miles that lead me from thy side;
The very horse I ride doth seem to know,
That in my heart a heavy grief doth hide.
He plods along with dull and heavy pace,
As if he felt the burden of my moan;
For he can see that from thy lovely face,
The light of joy from all the world is flown.
The bloody spur cannot provoke his speed,
Nor make him haste toward my journey’s end;
For well he knows that in my hour of need,
I only wish to turn and meet my friend.
    So every step I take away from thee,
    Is like a death that comes to torture me.
