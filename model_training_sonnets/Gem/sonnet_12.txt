XII

My love is like a ship upon the main,
By tempests tossed and driven from her course;
She seeks a harbor from the wind and rain,
But finds no mercy in the ocean’s force.
Thy favor is the shore she longs to reach,
The quiet bay where she may drop her sail;
But cruel rocks upon the sandy beach,
Threaten to break her with a sudden gale.
Oh, be the lighthouse on the jagged reef,
To guide my spirit through the blinding foam;
Bring to my weary heart a quick relief,
And lead the wanderer to a peaceful home.
    For if thou leave me to the mercy of the sea,
    I shall be lost and drowned in wanting thee.
