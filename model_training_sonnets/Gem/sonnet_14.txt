XIV

When I am called to give a final count,
Of all the minutes I have spent on earth,
How shall I sum the totalized amount,
And prove my life was of a little worth?
I have no titles to my humble name,
No vast estates or coffers filled with gold;
I never sought the fickle breath of fame,
Nor let my heart to vanity be sold.
But I shall say I loved thee with a fire,
That outshone all the lanterns of the night;
That thou wert source of every high desire,
And all my darkness was consumed by light.
    If love be credit in the book of grace,
    I shall find heaven in thy remembered face.
