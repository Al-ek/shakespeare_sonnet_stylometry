VIII

O! how I faint when I of thee do write,
Knowing a better spirit uses thy name,
And in the praise thereof spends all his might,
To make me tongue-tied speaking of thy fame.
But since thy worth, wide as the ocean is,
The humble as the proudest sail doth bear,
My saucy bark, inferior far to his,
Upon thy broad main doth wilfully appear.
Yet if I fail to reach the distant shore,
And sink beneath the waves of thy disdain,
I shall not ask for any mercy more,
But find a comfort in my secret pain.
    For even if my verse should be forgot,
    Thy name shall live though mine be mentioned not.
