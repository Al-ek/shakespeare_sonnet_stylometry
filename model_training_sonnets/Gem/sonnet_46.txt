XLVI

Mine eye and soul are at a constant strife,
Which part of thee should be their chief delight;
The one doth love the beauty of thy life,
The other adores the splendor of thy light.
My sight would claim thy form for its own prize,
And feast upon the grace of thy sweet face;
But then my soul with holy ardor cries,
That in thy spirit is a better place.
A jury of my thoughts is call'd to hear,
And give their verdict on this double claim;
They find that both to me are passing dear,
And both are worthy of a lover’s name.
    As thus: thy form is food unto mine eye,
    Thy soul the breath by which my heart doth fly.
