XLII

As some poor traveler o'er the desert sand
Doth seek the shadow of a cooling palm,
So doth my spirit in this barren land
Desire the comfort of thy presence calm.
For when the storms of life do loudest roar,
And clouds of sorrow blot the cheerful day,
I look to thee upon the distant shore,
To guide my wandering vessel on its way.
Thou art the anchor in my troubled sea,
The steady compass that directs my soul;
No tide of grief can pull me far from thee,
Nor keep me from my love’s desired goal.
    Though miles divide us and the way be long,
    The thought of thee doth make my spirit strong.
