XLIX

Not with the sun, that base and common light,
Shall I compare the luster of thine eye;
For he must hide his face in gloomy night,
And leave the world in darkness for to lie.
But thou art like a flame of holy fire,
That burns within the temple of my breast;
The sacred object of my soul’s desire,
In whom my weary spirit finds its rest.
No cloud can dim the brightness of thy grace,
Nor stormy wind put out thy steady glow;
I find my heaven in thy lovely face,
Whatever winds of fortune chance to blow.
    Then let the sun go down into the sea,
    My day remains while I am still with thee.
