IX

The heavy harvest bends the golden grain,
And tells the farmer that the year is old;
The summer’s warmth is followed by the rain,
And all the green is turned to brittle gold.
Thy beauty's bloom, which now is in its height,
Shall soon be tattered by the winter's hand;
Its petals falling in the sudden night,
To leave no trace upon the shifting sand.
Then sow thy beauty while the sun is high,
That when the frost comes with its biting breath,
A second spring within thy child may lie,
To cheat the cold and silent power of death.
    The fruit must fall so that the seed may live,
    This is the lesson that the seasons give.
