XVII

My eye and heart are joined in a revolt,
To see who shall possess thy image first;
The heart would lock thee in a secret vault,
The eye would see thee till it died of thirst.
The eye complains the heart is too confined,
To hold a beauty of such wide extent;
The heart replies the eye is nearly blind,
To all the virtues which the gods have lent.
But let them cease this vain and foolish strife,
And share the bounty of thy presence fair;
For thou art both the spirit and the life,
The visual glory and the inward prayer.
    Let eye see form, and heart the truth behold,
    To keep thy story from becoming old.
