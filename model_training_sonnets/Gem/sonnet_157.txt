III

Look in thy glass and tell the face thou seest,
That now is time it should another frame,
Unless thou wouldst be of thy self the least,
And leave the world no echo of thy name.
The earth is but a stage for fleeting shows,
Where every actor plays a weary part,
And even as the budding flower grows,
The canker worm is busy at its heart.
But thou art made of more enduring stuff,
If thou wouldst spend thy beauty as a loan,
For nature’s bounty is not large enough,
To let thee live for thy sweet self alone.
    Then make another self for love of me,
    That beauty still may live in thine and thee.
