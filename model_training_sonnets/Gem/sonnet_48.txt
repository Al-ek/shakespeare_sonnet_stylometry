XLVIII

Is it for fear of time’s consuming fire,
That thou dost keep thy beauty all alone?
To waste the object of the world’s desire,
Is surely sin that nothing can atone.
Thou art a steward of a precious gift,
Which nature lent to be employ'd with grace;
Then do not let the years with cruel thrift,
The glory of thy lineage efface.
Look on the field where golden harvest grows,
It dies to give the hungry earth increase;
So shouldst thou plant the beauty of thy rose,
That when thou fad’st, thy fame may never cease.
    Be not a miser of thy sweet self’s store,
    But give to love, and live for evermore.
