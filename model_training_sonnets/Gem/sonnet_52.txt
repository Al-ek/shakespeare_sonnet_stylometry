LII

When I consider how the lilies grow,
And weave their garments for the coming spring,
I think how swift the happy hours do go,
And all the sorrows that the years do bring.
For though the flower be clad in white and gold,
And breathe its fragrance on the morning air,
It soon must wither in the winter’s cold,
And leave the garden desolate and bare.
Thy beauty, too, must meet the hand of fate,
And yield its luster to the scythe of time;
But I shall keep thee in thy best of state,
Within the measure of my humble rhyme.
    Though nature’s pride must fall into the grave,
    My verse shall still thy lovely spirit save.
