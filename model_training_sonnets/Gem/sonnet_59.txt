LIX

If I could write the beauty of thine eyes,
And in fresh numbers tell of all thy grace,
The age to come would say the poet lies,
Such heavenly touches ne'er touch’d earthly face.
But since I am a man of humble wit,
And cannot reach the height of thy desert,
I leave my verse for other men to sit,
And judge the worth of my devouring heart.
Yet in this book thy memory shall stay,
Long after I am turn’d to dust and bone;
To show the world a glory that shall stay,
When all the monuments of pride are gone.
    Then let the future say that I was blest,
    To have my love within this heart expressed.
