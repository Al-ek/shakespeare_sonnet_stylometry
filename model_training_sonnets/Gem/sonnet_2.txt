II

Why dost thou waste the treasure of thy youth,
On selfish joys that leave no seed behind?
Thou ow'st to nature such a debt in truth,
As only through an heir can be resigned.
The rose that withers leaves a sweet perfume,
Distilled in vials to outlast the year,
But thou, if thou shouldst seek a lonely tomb,
Shalt leave no fragrance for the world to hear.
Thy beauty is a lease, not thine to keep,
A bounteous gift for which thou must account;
Before the scythe of Time begins to reap,
Return to earth a more than first amount.
    By giving life, thou dost thy life extend,
    And conquer death before thy days shall end.
