VI

How heavy is the journey I must take,
When every step doth lead me far from thee,
And every mile a deeper wound doth make,
Within the breast of my poor memory.
The world is but a desert, dark and wide,
Wherein I wander like a soul lost,
With nothing but my sorrow for a guide,
And nothing but my tears to pay the cost.
But when I think of thy sweet love again,
The heavy burden falls from off my back,
And all my grief is turned to sudden gain,
Like sunlight breaking through a cloudy rack.
    For though my body dwells in distant lands,
    My spirit rests within thy gentle hands.
