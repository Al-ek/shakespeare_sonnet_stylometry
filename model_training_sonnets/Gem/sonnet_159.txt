V

Mine eye hath played the painter to thy soul,
And drawn thy form in tables of my heart,
Where every line is under my control,
And every color is a work of art.
But though I strive to capture every grace,
And keep thy beauty from the touch of time,
I cannot find a way to save thy face,
Except within the temple of my rhyme.
For time is like a tyrant in his might,
Who seeks to level every noble thing,
And turn the glory of the morning light,
Into the shadow of a raven’s wing.
    Yet in my heart thy image shall remain,
    A sacred truth that time can never stain.
