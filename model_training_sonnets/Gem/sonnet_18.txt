XVIII

Let others build their monuments of stone,
To mock the passage of the flying years;
I build a temple for thy sake alone,
Out of my passion and my secret tears.
No wind can shake the pillars of my verse,
No rain can wash the colors from the page;
Though future tongues thy story shall rehearse,
They shall not find the wrinkles of thy age.
For here thou shalt remain forever young,
In all the freshness of thy early bloom;
Thy praises shall by every muse be sung,
Long after we are silent in the tomb.
    This is the miracle of ink and pen:
    To make thee live within the minds of men.
