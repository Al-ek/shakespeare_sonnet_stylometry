II

When I behold the dial’s shady stealth,
And see how fast the minutes haste away,
I tremble for the portion of thy wealth,
That time consumes with every passing day.
The rose that blooms so fair at break of dawn,
Must yield her petals to the evening air,
And all the grace that did her form adorn,
Is lost within the darkness of despair.
But in thy youth a greater power lies,
To stay the hand of ruin and decay,
If thou wouldst read the language of mine eyes,
And turn thy night into a glorious day.
    For love is not a slave to time’s decree,
    But lives eternal in my verse and thee.
