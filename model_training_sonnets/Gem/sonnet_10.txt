My heart hath found a language of its own,
That needs no sound to make its meaning clear;
A silent music, to the world unknown,
Which only thy attentive soul can hear.
When I am silent and I gaze on thee,
Think not my passion is a dying spark;
It is a flame that burns eternally,
A steady beacon in the lonely dark.
For words are but the shadows of the truth,
And often fail to speak the mind's intent;
They lose the vigor of their early youth,
And leave the speaker poor and discontent.
    But in my silence, read the perfect whole,
    The wordless testament of all my soul.
