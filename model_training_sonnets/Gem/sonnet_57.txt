LVII

When I behold the stars that shine above,
And count the numbers of the heavenly host,
I think how vast is the eternal love,
Of which the poets of the world do boast.
But in thy face I find a better light,
Than any candle in the sky can show;
To guide my spirit through the dark of night,
And make my heart with holy ardor glow.
For thou art made of such a heavenly mold,
That all the stars do seem but pale and dim;
A work of grace that never can grow old,
Nor lose the favor of the cherubim.
    Then let the heavens roll their spheres away,
    Thy beauty is the sun of all my day.
