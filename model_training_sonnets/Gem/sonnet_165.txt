XI

Why is my verse so barren of new pride,
So far from variation or quick change?
Why with the time do I not glance aside,
To new-found methods and to compounds strange?
It is because I always write of thee,
And thou and love are still my argument,
So all my best is dressing old words free,
Spending again what is already spent.
For as the sun is daily new and old,
So is my love still telling what is told,
And in the story of thy beauty bold,
I find a treasure that can ne’er grow cold.
    Then blame me not if I no more can sing,
    Than what I find within thy spirit’s spring.
