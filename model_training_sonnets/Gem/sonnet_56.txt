LVI

As some poor beggar at a palace gate,
Who lacks the words to tell his bitter need,
I stand before thee in my humble state,
And pray that thou my silent heart wilt read.
For though my tongue be tied by sudden fear,
And all my thoughts are scatter'd to the wind,
I have a love that is to me more dear,
Than any treasure that the world can find.
My looks shall be my only messenger,
To speak the passion of my longing soul;
To show the world I am a worshipper,
Of one who makes my broken spirit whole.
    Then let my silence be my best defense,
    And find in thee a loving recompense.
