XIII

What alchemy is hidden in thy breath,
That turns my base despair to shining gold?
Thy simple word can save a soul from death,
And make a coward suddenly be bold.
A single glance from thy majestic eye,
Can change the winter of my discontent,
To such a spring as never graced the sky,
With all the perfumes of the orient.
Thou art the master of the secret art,
That heals the wounds which life and fortune deal;
Thou find’st the broken pieces of my heart,
And bind’st them up with such a holy seal.
    No other doctor can my health restore,
    Save thou alone, whom I must still adore.
