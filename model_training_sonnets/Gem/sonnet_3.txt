III

When through the window of my weary sight,
I see the world in grey and sullen hue,
I crave the morning of thy spirit’s light,
To wash the earth in colors bright and new.
For thou art sun to my eclipsed soul,
The star by which my wandering bark is steered;
Thy presence makes my broken spirit whole,
And kills the shadows that my heart hath feared.
Let not the clouds of distance interpose,
To rob my vision of its only prize;
For in thy absence every pleasure goes,
And heavy sleep weighs down my longing eyes.
    Until I see thy face, I walk in night,
    For thou alone art source of all my light.
