VIII

Why art thou made of such a strange design,
Both kind as mercy and as cold as stone?
A heavenly spirit in a form divine,
Yet with a heart that beats for self alone.
One moment thou dost lift me to the skies,
With promises of everlasting grace;
The next, I see the lightning in thine eyes,
And find a stranger in thy lovely face.
Art thou a spirit sent to lead me home,
Or but a phantom born of my desire?
Must I forever through this desert roam,
To find the water that shall quench my fire?
    Be what thou art, and I shall still be thine,
    For in thy darkness I behold my shine.
