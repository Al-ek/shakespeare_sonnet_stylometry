IX

Accuse me thus: that I have scanted all,
Wherein I should thy great deserts repay,
Forgot upon thy dearest love to call,
Whereto all bonds do tie me day by day.
That I have frequent been with unknown minds,
And given to time thy own dear-purchased right,
That I have hoisted sail to all the winds,
Which should transport me farthest from thy sight.
But know, sweet love, my heart was never false,
Though I have wandered on a lonely way,
And if I danced to every idle pulse,
It was but to forget my soul’s decay.
    For in my dreams I ever turn to thee,
    The only star in my dark galaxy.
