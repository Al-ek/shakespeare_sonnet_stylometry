XLV

How like a thievish merchant is the year,
That steals the treasure of the summer’s day;
He takes the gold from fields both far and near,
And gives us only silver frost for pay.
So doth he deal with all thy beauty’s store,
Increasing debt with every hour that flies;
Until thy youthful wealth is seen no more,
And famine dwells within thy sunken eyes.
But I have made a contract with the Muse,
To keep thy riches from the grasp of time;
No legal art nor bond can I refuse,
To seal thy glory in my humble rhyme.
    Though nature’s lease must have a certain end,
    Thy worth shall live for every age to spend.
