VI

O, what a usury is found in love,
Where giving all doth make the giver rich!
No earthly treasure can so precious prove,
As that which bridges every sorrow’s ditch.
I lend my heart to thee with no receipt,
Content to wait for interest in a kiss;
And though the world should label me a cheat,
I find my profit in this holy bliss.
Let misers count their gold in iron chests,
And fear the thief that prowls the dark of night;
My wealth within thy gentle bosom rests,
Beyond the reach of envy or of spite.
    The more I give, the more I have to spend,
    In this sweet traffic which shall never end.
