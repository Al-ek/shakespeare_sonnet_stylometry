XX

If Time should come with his destructive blade,
To cut the thread of my existence here,
Think not that I shall vanish in the shade,
Or leave the world in trembling and in fear.
For I have lived within thy gentle thought,
And found a heaven in thy loving arms;
Such holy comfort has my spirit bought,
As shields me from the reach of all alarms.
Death can but take the vessel, not the wine,
The earthly shell, but not the inner light;
And since my spirit is entwined with thine,
I shall not fear the coming of the night.
    For love is victor over grave and stone,
    And finds a kingdom where it reigns alone.
