IV

If I should plead my case in love’s high court,
And sue for mercy from thy sovereign will,
What evidence could my poor soul report,
To prove I am thy faithful servant still?
My heart is witness to my secret sighs,
My eyes have wept a river for thy sake;
Yet in thy breast a cold indifference lies,
As if my bond thy cruelty would break.
Is not my service worth a kinder look?
Hath my devotion no reward in store?
Read all my longing in this silent book,
And judge me not by what I said before.
    For though my tongue be tied by fear and shame,
    My pulse doth beat the rhythm of thy name.
