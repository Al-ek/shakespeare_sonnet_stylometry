I

When in the glass I view my furrowed face,
And see the marks that envious Time hath made,
I seek in thee a more enduring grace,
Before my summer's bloom begins to fade.
For thou art spring where I am winter’s frost,
Thy golden light doth warm my frozen clay,
Restoring all the beauty I have lost,
And turning night into a boundless day.
Yet do not hoard what nature did bestow,
Lest like a miser thou shouldst die alone;
Let seeds of thy perfections start to grow,
In other soil when thy own strength is gone.
    Thus shall thy image live when thou art old,
    And keep thy story from the churchyard mold.
