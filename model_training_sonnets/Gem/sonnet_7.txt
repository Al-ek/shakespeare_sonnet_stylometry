VII

When marble tombs are leveled with the dust,
And brass inscriptions worn away by time,
When golden crowns are eaten o'er with rust,
And towers fall that once were thought sublime;
Then shall these lines, which now I humbly write,
Preserve the glory of thy matchless face;
They shall provide a lamp against the night,
And keep for thee a high and honored place.
For ink is stronger than the mason’s hand,
And paper outlasts stone and heavy clay;
Across the borders of a distant land,
Thy name shall travel to a future day.
    So long as men have eyes to read my verse,
    They shall thy beauty and thy truth rehearse.
