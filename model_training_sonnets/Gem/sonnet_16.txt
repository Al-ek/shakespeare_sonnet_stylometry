XVI

If I should make a list of every part,
That makes thy person so beyond compare,
I would begin with thy courageous heart,
And end with ringlets of thy golden hair.
But in between, what wonders would I find?
An eye that speaks, a lip that smiles in peace;
A noble spirit and a gentle mind,
Whose bounteous treasures never know decrease.
Yet even then, the inventory fails,
To catch the essence of thy inner light;
As when a painter tries to draw the gales,
Or capture all the glory of the night.
    Thou art a sum that numbers cannot reach,
    A sacred lesson that no man can teach.
