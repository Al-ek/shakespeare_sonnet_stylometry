XI

In sleep I see thy shadow haunt the room,
More bright than any creature of the day;
Thy presence breaks the power of the gloom,
And drives the heavy clouds of grief away.
But when I wake and find the bed is cold,
And see the grey dawn creeping through the pane,
I feel the sorrow that is ages old,
And wish to close my eyes and dream again.
For what is life but one long waking dream,
Where shadows pass and leave no trace behind?
Thou art the only truth, the only gleam,
That gives a purpose to my wandering mind.
    If I must sleep to have thee by my side,
    Then let the morning in the sea be tied.
