IV

Shall I compare thy grace to April’s prime,
When every bird doth sing upon the spray?
Thou art more constant than the changing time,
And brighter than the sun’s meridian ray.
But even as the seasons turn to gold,
And autumn leaves are scattered on the ground,
Thy youth must yield to winter’s icy cold,
And all thy glory in the grave be bound.
Yet in my verse thy summer shall not fade,
Nor shall the darkness hide thy lovely light,
For by my pen thy image is portrayed,
Beyond the reach of death’s eternal night.
    So long as men have eyes and hearts to feel,
    My love for thee shall time’s own ruin seal.
