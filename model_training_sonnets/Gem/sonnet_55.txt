LV

The thievish hours do steal the day’s delight,
And leave the world to mourn in shadows grey;
They take the gold of morning’s holy light,
And turn to ash the glory of the day.
So doth the time with all thy beauty deal,
And leaves his mark upon thy lovely brow;
No art of man can stop the turning wheel,
That brings to ruin all we honor now.
But in my heart thy lamp is burning bright,
A flame that time nor age can never tame;
To guide me through the darkness of the night,
And keep alive the honor of thy name.
    Though all the world in winter’s frost be lost,
    Thy love remains beyond the reach of frost.
