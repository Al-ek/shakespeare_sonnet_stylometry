XV

The rose is praised for being fair and sweet,
Yet on her stem a sharp-toothed thorn doth grow;
The silver stream that runs beneath our feet,
Hath hidden rocks where muddy waters flow.
So in thy nature, mixed with every grace,
Are those small flaws that make thee more than man;
A sudden cloud may darken thy fair face,
And mar the beauty of thy perfect plan.
Yet I do love the thorn as well as bloom,
The muddy bottom with the crystal wave;
For in thy spirit there is ample room,
For all the virtues that the angels crave.
    Thy very faults are dearer to my sight,
    Than others' virtues in their fullest light.
