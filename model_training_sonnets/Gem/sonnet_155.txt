I

From hollow earth where silent shadows sleep,
I summon up the ghosts of vanished days,
To see if time or tide can ever keep,
The golden light that in thy spirit plays.
But as the sun must sink into the night,
And leave the world to winter’s cold embrace,
So must thy beauty fade from mortal sight,
And leave no record of thy lovely face.
Yet if thou wouldst but plant a living seed,
To blossom when thy summer days are o’er,
Thy truth and beauty then would live indeed,
And flourish on this world's inconstant shore.
    Then be not wasteful of thy precious store,
    But give to life what death would else devour.
