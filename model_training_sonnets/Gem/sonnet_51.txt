LI

The heavy clouds that blot the summer sky,
And hide the golden glory of the sun,
Are like the doubts that in my bosom lie,
Before my work of loving thee is done.
For when the shadows of the world descend,
And all the beauty of the day is fled,
I fear that time our happy bond shall end,
And leave my heart as cold as are the dead.
But then thy kindness, like a gentle rain,
Doth fall upon the garden of my soul;
To wash away the bitterness of pain,
And make my weary spirit once more whole.
    So let the tempests of the world arise,
    I find my shelter in thy loving eyes.
