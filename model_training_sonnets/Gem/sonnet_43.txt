XLIII

Look in my heart and see thy image there,
More bright than any glass can e'er reflect;
For though thy face be passing sweet and fair,
Thy inward virtue claims my most respect.
The painter’s art can but the skin portray,
And show the world the beauty of thine eye;
But time shall wash those colors all away,
And leave the canvas cold and dull to lie.
My love doth draw a portrait of thy mind,
A work of grace that age can never mar;
In every thought some hidden worth I find,
That shines as constant as the evening star.
    Then fear not time, nor death’s eternal sleep,
    For what I love, the heavens still shall keep.
