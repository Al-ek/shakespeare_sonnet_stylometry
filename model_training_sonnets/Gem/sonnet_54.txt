LIV

How like the ocean is the human heart,
That ebbs and flows with every passing tide;
In every wave there is a hidden part,
Where all the secrets of the soul do hide.
My love for thee is like the deep and vast,
That knows no limit to its swelling might;
A power that shall for evermore outlast,
The fading shadows of the longest night.
No storm of doubt can stir the quiet deep,
Wherein thy image is for ever set;
A sacred treasure that I vow to keep,
Until the sun of all my days is met.
    Then let the surface of the waters roar,
    My love is anchor’d to the eternal shore.
