VII

When in the sessions of my silent thought,
I count the losses that my life hath known,
And see the ruins that the years have wrought,
Upon the fields where once my hopes were sown.
I weep for friends who sleep in death’s dark night,
And for the loves that withered in their bloom,
Until I lose the comfort of the light,
And find myself a prisoner in the tomb.
But then thy face appears before my sight,
Like some bright star that gilds the evening sky,
And all my darkness turns to golden light,
As all my sorrows in thy presence die.
    For thou art all the wealth that I possess,
    A sovereign cure for all my wretchedness.
