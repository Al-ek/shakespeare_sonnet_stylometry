LIII

If all the world were but a painted show,
And all our lives but dreams that pass away,
Where would the spirit of my loving go,
When night descends upon the busy day?
I find in thee a truth that cannot fail,
A solid rock within a shifting sea;
No phantom of the mind can e'er prevail,
To pull my heart away from honoring thee.
For though the shadows of the earth increase,
And all the glory of the sun be gone,
In thy sweet love I find a perfect peace,
That leads me safely to the coming dawn.
    Then let the world be nothing but a lie,
    Thy truth remains until the day I die.
