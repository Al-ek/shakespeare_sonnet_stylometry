XIX

The seasons pass in a perpetual round,
From budding spring to winter’s icy breath;
The flowers that were scattered on the ground,
Must meet the coming of a certain death.
So has our love its autumn and its spring,
Its summer heat and winter’s sudden chill;
One day the birds within the forest sing,
The next, the world is desolate and still.
But do not fear the changing of the year,
Nor think my heart shall alter with the sun;
For though the snow and biting frost appear,
My long devotion never shall be done.
    Rooted in truth, my love shall still endure,
    Through every season, constant and secure.
