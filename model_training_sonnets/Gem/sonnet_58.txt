LVIII

The silver brook that runs among the hills,
And sings its song to every passing breeze,
Is like the joy that all my spirit fills,
When I am resting in the shade of trees.
But thou art like the fountain of my life,
From whom the waters of my comfort flow;
To heal the wounds of all my worldly strife,
And make the garden of my heart to grow.
No drought of sorrow can thy spring dry up,
Nor winter’s frost thy gentle music stay;
I find a blessing in thy loving cup,
That turns to gold the silver of my day.
    So let the rivers run into the sea,
    My stream of love shall ever flow to thee.
