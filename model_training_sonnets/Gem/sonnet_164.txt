X

Let me not to the marriage of true minds,
Admit the shadows of a false report,
For love is not a thing that fortune finds,
Or loses in the changes of a court.
It is an ever-fixed mark that stands,
Against the tempests of the raging sea,
And reaches out with its eternal hands,
To touch the borders of eternity.
Love is not time’s fool, though the rosy cheek,
Must feel the edge of his devouring scythe,
And though the voice of youth may grow too weak,
To sing the songs that once were loud and blithe.
    If this be error and upon me proved,
    I never writ, nor no man ever loved.
