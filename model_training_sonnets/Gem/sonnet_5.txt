V

The golden sun that climbs the eastern hill,
And sheds his glory on the world below,
Doth work upon the earth with secret skill,
To make the hidden flowers start to grow.
Even so thy favor works upon my mind,
To wake the dormant music in my breast;
No other warmth so potent can I find,
To put my restless agonies to rest.
But if the sun should hide behind a cloud,
The world is left in shivering despair;
So when thy frown wraps me in sorrow’s shroud,
I find no comfort in the summer air.
    Thy smile is June, thy coldness winter’s bite,
    Thou art my seasons and my only light.
