LI

Thou took’st my pride, and left me gentler still,
A softer man than envy could design;
I thought my glory bound within my will,
But found my joy instead was bound in thine.
No crown so grand as modesty could yield,
No triumph sweet save grace without applause;
Thy calm unmasks the vanity conceal’d,
And humbles strength to serve its better cause.
So may I stand extinguish’d but content,
Unlaurel’d though in virtue’s fair degree;
For pride must burn that love may find its rent,
And only loss can teach such victory.
I lost myself, and won the greater grace,
To live but mirror’d in thy holy face.
