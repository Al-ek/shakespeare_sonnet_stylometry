XXX

The moon, that pale physician of the night,
Peers wan through clouds to heal the wounded sky;
Her silver draught restores the blinded light,
And soothes the stars when weary tears they cry.
So thou, fair spirit, to my darkness come,
And pour thy balm upon my sleepless care;
No sin so deep, but thou couldst pardon some,
No wound so grave, thy kindness fails it there.
I did not seek thee save through silent pain,
And found in prayer what prayer could not impart;
The more I call, the more I live again,
For thou art answer dwelling in my heart.
Thus night to day its borrowed grace may lend,
As sorrow learns by love itself to mend.
