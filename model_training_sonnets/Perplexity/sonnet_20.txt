XX

If I should lose the memory of thy face,
And time erase the lines I hold so dear,
Still would my soul retain thy hidden trace,
And feel thy presence constant, ever near.
For thou art written not in fragile thought,
Nor kept within the keeping of the mind,
But in that deeper place where truth is wrought,
Beyond the reach of time or fate unkind.
Thus even loss could not undo what’s made,
Nor absence break what presence first began;
For love, once formed, can never wholly fade,
Nor be unmade by any force of man.
So fear I not what future may remove:
For all I am is shaped and sealed by love.
