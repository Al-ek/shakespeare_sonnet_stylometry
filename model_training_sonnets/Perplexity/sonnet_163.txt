IX

I asked the rose what makes her blush so deep,
She answered not, yet fragrance told her name;
I asked the wind, and found no word to keep,
But in its sighing heard my own heart’s flame.
Thus nature speaks though never breath is drawn,
Her silence teems with loudest melodies;
For every dusk foretells another dawn,
And love still grows through winter’s boundaries.
Then shall I learn from her this patient way,
To bloom in beauty even when alone;
And when all words from mortal tongues decay,
To let my being speak in scent and tone.
For truth needs not the language of the wise,
When pure intent is seen through others’ eyes.
