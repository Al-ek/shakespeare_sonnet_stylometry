VI

The moon beholds what sun may never spy,
The tender dream that hides from waking eyes;
At night the truth and fancy closer lie,
And reason sleeps while faith in shadow flies.
Then ghostly thoughts, like wind through tender reeds,
Do stir the calm and rouse forgotten pain;
And lovers rise to mend what daylight bleeds,
Till dawn returns to part their hearts again.
But though the morning rob what night bestows,
And shame would bid our fevered vows recede,
Still in my breast that quiet lantern glows,
Fed not by sight, but endless, fervent need.
The sun may break and lay our secrets bare,
Yet love unseen still claims the midnight air.
