XIII

The stars above may guide the traveler’s way,
And mark the course that wandering feet should take,
Yet I require no distant light to stay
My steps from straying for thy gentle sake.
For thou art fixed within my inward sight,
A compass truer than the northern flame,
That points me ever toward what must be right,
And calls me softly by thy secret name.
Thus though the world grow dark and paths unsure,
I walk with confidence I cannot feign,
For love provides a guidance swift and sure,
That neither doubt nor danger can restrain.
And while that star within my heart remains,
No outward night can bind me in its chains.
