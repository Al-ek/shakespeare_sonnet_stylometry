XX

So ends this chain of thought in verse confined,
Yet not the thought that moves my hand to write;
For nothing stills the labor of the mind,
That seeks to turn our darkness into light.
What once was told in ink shall turn to air,
What once was pain shall sweet remembrance prove;
And though my days grow short beyond repair,
My words shall linger, ever teaching love.
Then if one soul, long hence, should find me true,
And read these sighs as though they still were warm,
I’ll know that time, defeated, fades from view,
And death undone forgets its mortal form.
Love lives in verse, and verse keeps love alive—
Thus art and heart through endless ages strive.
