I

When dawn first spills her pale and trembling gold,
And night withdraws her veil from waking sight,
My thoughts, like pilgrims timid yet made bold,
Set forth to seek thy presence in the light.
For thou art fixed within my restless mind,
A constant star no passing cloud can hide,
Though Fortune prove both fickle and unkind,
Still in thy grace my hopes and fears abide.
What art thou then, that thus my soul commands?
A dream made flesh, or truth in dream’s disguise?
I reach for thee with unavailing hands,
Yet find thee ever nearer than mine eyes.
So bound am I where reason cannot move,
A willing captive to the chains of love.
