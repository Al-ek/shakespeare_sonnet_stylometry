LVII

I love thee not as fools adore a scheme,
All changing colours glibly day by day;
But as the oak holds firm beside the stream,
Through storm and sun, and will not break or sway.
My heart, though plain, is steadfast as a star,
And giveth more the longer thou art near;
Its measure is not shown by loud afar,
But quiet hours that lengthen through the year.
Let fancies fade, let rumours mock the kind,
My faith is wrought of substance not of sound;
And where I stand thou shalt forever find,
That deep roots guard the blossoms spring hath crown’d.
Love’s proof is not in gesture or display,
But constancy that will not break nor stray.
