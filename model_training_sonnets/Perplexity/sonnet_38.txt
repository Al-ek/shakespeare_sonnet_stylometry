XXXVIII

What poet ever liv’d that lov’d so true,
That ink did bleed the pulse it dared commend?
Yet I, through thee, such privilege pursue,
To write what even silence might defend.
No muse could tune so sweet a human sound,
Nor dream so deep but thine exceeds its grace;
For heaven’s thought in mortal shape is bound,
When thou art near and smiling on this place.
So grant me leave to err with fervent tongue,
And praise beyond what sense or measure can;
For better bold in love than coldly sung,
And better truth in fire than ice of man.
Each word unworthy seeks thy name to raise,
Though failing art, it triumphs still in praise.
