VIII

When poets die, their voices linger still,
In syllables that dance through later years;
Though flesh returns to dust, the breath of will
Moves soft as hope between the mourner’s tears.
What other craft can capture time’s embrace,
Or teach the heart to echo endless sound?
The grave is deep, yet not so dark a place
That thought cannot its narrow bounds confound.
Then write, my soul, though none should read thy line,
Though fame neglect and fortune pass thee by;
For ink once spilt becomes a form divine,
A current flowing ever through the sky.
The dead may sleep, but through the written art,
Their restless dreams shall wake the living heart.
