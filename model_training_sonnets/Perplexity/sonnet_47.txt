XLVII

Thou art the reason sleep refused my care,
For dreams themselves grow jealous of my sight;
They know thy shape is more than visions bear,
And envy wakes my soul to real delight.
I close mine eyes, yet cannot bar thy face,
It glimmers soft upon the inward screen;
No phantasy could counterfeit that grace,
No dream could weigh such eyes or lips serene.
So rest departs that once was gentle peace,
And leaves me watchful, bound in longing’s net;
But though I tire, my joy shall not decrease,
Since love’s awake when slumber’s second set.
Sleep may withhold its comfort and its art,
Yet thou art more refreshing to my heart.
