II

Time’s subtle thief creeps softly through the years,
And steals the bloom from every fairest face,
Yet leaves untouched the memory it endears,
Preserved beyond his cold and grasping pace.
For in my heart thy image stands secure,
Unchanged by age, unwearied by decay,
Though flesh be frail and nothing can endure,
Love’s truer form shall never fade away.
Then fear not Time nor his devouring art,
Though mirrors show what once they could not tell,
For what he takes, he cannot claim the heart,
Where thou in endless youth dost ever dwell.
Let years advance; they do but prove this truth:
Love grants eternity to fleeting youth.
