XXXI

When envy stirs, and tongues of malice sing,
Their slander’d notes fall harmless on mine ear;
For truth, secure beneath thy sheltering wing,
Hears not the noise of falsehood or of fear.
What reck the crows that cry against the lark,
Whose music swells unseen above the field?
The spite of night can neither wound nor mark
The sun’s resolved and unpolluted shield.
So let the busy world its venom spend,
And rail at joy it never shall command;
Its poison dies where purity shall mend,
And even hate grows gentle by thy hand.
The more they chide, the better thou art proved,
For envy’s tongue gives witness to the loved.
