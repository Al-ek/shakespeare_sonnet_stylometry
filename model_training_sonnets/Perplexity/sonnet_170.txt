XVI

A maiden sat by twilight’s broken shore,
Her song unmoved the stars to silent tears;
Each note recalled what love had been before,
And mocked the silence gathered through the years.
She sang not loud, but truth gave tongue to sound,
And all the air grew tremulous with grace;
The sea, abashed, forgot its restless bound,
And stilled itself before her sorrowed face.
If grief can thus turn beauty into art,
Then suffer me to lose and yet create;
For from the ashes of a ruined heart,
The phoenix dreams and scorns the hand of fate.
So sing, sweet soul, though night deny return,
For love once sung is love that still will burn.
