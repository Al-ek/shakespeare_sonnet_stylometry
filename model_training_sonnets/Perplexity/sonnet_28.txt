XXVIII

The storm may batter roof and rend the plain,
And shake the cedars to their rooted core;
Yet love, like stone, abides the flood and rain,
And deeper lays its pledge beneath the roar.
No trial spent can raze its patient wall,
No winter frost can pale its living soul;
And though the mightiest towers totter, fall,
Its unseen flame preserves the ruin whole.
So let the tempests rage and kingdoms bend,
Let tyrants mock the meek their scepter wrong;
The faithful heart no season can offend,
It keeps its summer warm, its music strong.
For what can time or tempest do to me,
Whose peace is bound with thine eternally?
