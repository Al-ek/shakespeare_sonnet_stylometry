VII

No sweeter wrong than truth too soft to tell,
When kindness veils the wound that love would show;
For silence guards the places where we dwell,
And mercy hides what pride could overthrow.
Yet even gentlest lies must come to light,
And time will peel the gloss from painted grace;
The truest bond demands the naked sight,
The soul unveiled, the mask torn from the face.
Then let me love thee not for thine disguise,
Nor for the dream that others weave around,
But for the tears that glisten in thine eyes,
When earthly joy to sorrow’s chill is bound.
In truth lies love, in suffering proof is made,
And sweet deceit but blooms to gray and fade.
