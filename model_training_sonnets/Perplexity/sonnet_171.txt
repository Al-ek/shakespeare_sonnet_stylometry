XVII

When winter gnaws the marrow of the year,
And every branch stands black against the sky,
I turn within, where memories appear,
And find in thought what sun and leaf deny.
Though cold may rule the earth with frozen hands,
It cannot touch the embers in the chest;
For even in the bleakest, barren lands,
The heart creates its own eternal rest.
So let the snow erase the prints of men,
And silence reign where summer laughter grew;
For spring shall trace those vanished paths again,
And make the barren blossom bright and new.
In stillest frost, the soul has learned to see,
That death itself was born of memory.
