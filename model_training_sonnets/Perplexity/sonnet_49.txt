XLIX

The flame of youth, though proud and full of cheer,
Doth burn too bold, and swiftly finds its close;
It blazes high yet dies within a year,
While temp’r’d hearts like steady embers glow.
So let me love not quick nor rash, but deep;
Let constancy refine what passion blent;
The fire that lasts is that which learns to keep
Its warmth in peace, and not in riot spent.
Thus shall we grow not weary, though we age,
Nor lose the bloom that kindness makes survive;
For fondness turns each wrinkle to a page
Where patience writes the proof our souls derive.
Love is no stroke of flame through morning air,
But lamp that waits through midnight’s quiet care.
