XXIV

When fortune, blind, my little hopes deceived,
And fenced her smiles for those of grander birth,
I found in thee the grace my soul believed,
The equal mercy that redeems the earth.
No crown nor gold could purchase half thy glance,
Nor purple robes command so rich a look;
Thy worth, unminted, dims all circumstance,
And humbles kings as subjects to thy book.
Then let the world exalt its painted throne,
And bid its minions praise the false and proud;
Thou art my realm, my court, my fame alone—
Thy praise the only acclamation loud.
For if thy heart but keep me in its store,
I am no beggar, though I beg no more.
