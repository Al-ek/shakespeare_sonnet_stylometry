XL

I end where new beginnings softly start,
For love renews the circle never done;
Its birth is death, its death but birth’s counterpart,
Eternal rising underneath the sun.
The flame I kept hath kindled other days,
And lights the path that once I wander’d blind;
So may my verse enshrine thy living praise,
And lend the weary world an art refined.
O reader kind, though centuries divide,
Receive these hours distilled from beating breast;
If love endure, then none are crucified,
But live through time in one forever rest.
Thus let the heart’s immortal labour be,
To write our souls into eternity.
