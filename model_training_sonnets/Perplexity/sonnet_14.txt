XIV

If beauty were a kingdom to be won,
And hearts the fields where silent battles lie,
Then thou wouldst reign where countless wars are done,
And conquer all without a single cry.
No sword is drawn, no fortress stormed or scaled,
Yet still thy power subdues the strongest will;
Resistance fades, and reason’s voice is veiled,
While all submits, yet counts it triumph still.
Such is thy grace, that yielding feels like gain,
And loss itself is welcomed as a prize;
For in surrender none have cause to complain,
When victory shines so clear within thine eyes.
Thus art thou crowned where none can e’er compete:
A sovereign made by love’s most sweet defeat.
