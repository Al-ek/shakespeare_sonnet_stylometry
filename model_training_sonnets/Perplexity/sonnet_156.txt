II

Sweet thief of hours, that plunders ever on,
Thy subtle touch makes mirrors bend and sigh;
What once was morning fair, by noon is gone,
And twilight steals the blush from evening’s sky.
Yet in thy wake, thou leav’st both fear and grace,
For beauty ripens best when most it fades;
And wisdom walks behind thy shadowed face,
Where childish dream to sober truth persuades.
Then let me age if such decay be gain,
And count each wrinkle as a verse well writ;
For though I yield to time’s unending chain,
My soul warms still, while mortal fires quit.
He wins his crown who dares his loss embrace,
And smiles to find his glory in disgrace.
