LVI

When first despair did seize my wounded will,
And whisper, “All thy hope stands ruin’d deep,”
Thou cam’st like balm the loneliest hearts instill,
And taught the lost their vigil still to keep.
No power of night could bar thy gentle glow,
Nor weight of grief thy promise undermine;
Thy light was born to pierce the thickest woe,
And prove that stars through shade more brightly shine.
Now every sorrow I account as friend,
Whose face reminds how solace touched me first;
For pain fulfils the joy that truth shall lend,
And thirst makes sweet the water once immersed.
I bless the dark because thou wert the flame,
That found me cold and made my soul reclaim.
