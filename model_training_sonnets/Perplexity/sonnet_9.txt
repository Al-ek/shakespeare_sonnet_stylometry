IX

If ever doubt should cast its shadowed veil,
And bid me question what my soul holds dear,
Thy single glance would make that darkness pale,
And drive away the specter of all fear.
For truth resides not in the shifting mind,
Nor in the logic reason would impose,
But in the certainty that love can find
When heart to heart in silent concord goes.
No argument can better prove thy worth
Than that which springs unbidden from within,
A knowing older than the mind’s own birth,
Untouched by change, untroubled by all sin.
Thus doubt dissolves like mist before the sun,
And leaves me certain thou and I are one.
