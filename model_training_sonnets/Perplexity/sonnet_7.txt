VII

Let others boast of wealth or titles rare,
Of power that bends the will of lesser men;
Such fleeting triumphs fade to empty air,
And leave no mark beyond the present ken.
But I possess a treasure none can claim,
A quiet joy no envy can destroy,
For in thy love I find a steadier flame
Than all the world could give or take from me.
What care I then if fortune turn her wheel,
Or chance bestow or snatch her passing grace?
So long as thou art constant, firm, and real,
No loss can rob me of my rightful place.
For richest kings are beggars in this part,
If love like thine dwell not within the heart.
