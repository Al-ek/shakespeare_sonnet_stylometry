IV

The painter’s hand may catch thy lovely grace,
But never soul that sparks behind thine eyes;
No shade nor hue shall mimic that fair face,
Though canvas lives while human beauty dies.
For art doth plead with time, yet time prevails,
And marble cracks beneath the wind of truth;
But love once voiced in verse no storm assails,
For wit outsteps the tender graves of youth.
So let the sculptor labor as he must,
And let the poet tire his inky pen;
Our dust shall mix when all is turned to dust,
But thoughts shall breathe and make thee new again.
Though death may claim what fingers cannot save,
My words shall kiss thee past the silent grave.
