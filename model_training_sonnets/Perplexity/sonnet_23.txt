XXIII

The dawn in envy rises from the bay,
To rival blush that lights thy gentle cheek;
Yet fails, though dressed in all her proud array,
For none but hearts in love thy warmth may seek.
Each glance of thine rebukes the jealous sun,
Who burns in shame to see himself out-shone;
While I, poor mortal, live when day is done,
For thee my heaven, when the world is gone.
O stay, sweet light, nor yield to eve’s decline,
Let night grow meek before thy splendid reign;
My faith is fixed where glories fade in thine,
And seeks no dawn that brings thee not again.
For every morning born that finds thee nigh,
Becomes eternal, though the hours die.
