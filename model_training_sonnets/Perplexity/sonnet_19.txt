XIX

The seasons turn with unrelenting pace,
And bring their gifts only to take them back;
Each leaves its fleeting imprint on the face,
Then yields to change upon another track.
Yet in this cycle one thing stands apart,
Unmoved by winter’s chill or summer’s blaze:
The steady warmth that lives within the heart,
And glows unchanged through all the passing days.
For love is not the servant of the year,
Nor subject to the rule of sun or rain;
It keeps its course though all else disappear,
And thrives where other beauties wane and wane.
Thus stands it firm while all the world must bend:
A season without start, a time without end.
