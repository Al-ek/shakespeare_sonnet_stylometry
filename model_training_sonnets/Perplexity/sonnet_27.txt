XXVII

I dream’d last night thy spirit walk’d the skies,
And strew’d with stars the darkling veil of sleep;
Thy hand, unworldly, touch’d mine waking eyes,
And drew from them such tears the heavens keep.
O vision fair! too brief for mortal stay,
Thou vanish’d’st ere I knew what grace I held;
As mist before the blush of newborn day,
Thy smile dissolved, my solace unimpell’d.
Yet still that dream a realer truth imparts
Than all the waking hours that call it vain;
For though unseen, thou livest in my heart’s
Unfinished psalm that death cannot attain.
Thus do I count each dream a holy trust,
Where love outlives the boundary of dust.
