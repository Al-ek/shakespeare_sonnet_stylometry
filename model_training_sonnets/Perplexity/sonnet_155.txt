I

When morning breaks through curtains made of gold,
The night withdraws in shame to darker halls;
Her tender silence leaves the world grown old,
While youth awakes and answers daylight’s calls.
Yet every dawn, though fair, must fade to dusk,
And every rose to earth return once more;
So beauty’s scent is bound in time’s cold husk,
And love must fight what dying hours implore.
But if thy eyes keep vigil through the years,
And memory serves to guard their early fire,
Then shall my verse outlast the tide of tears,
And give new breath to what we still desire.
Love’s fleeting bloom shall never fully die,
While words endure to teach the heart to fly.
