XXXIV

When first I lov’d, I knew not what I did,
But play’d with arrows fashion’d out of sighs;
Each dart unmeant did strike where reason hid,
And bound my wit beneath thy conquering eyes.
Since then, I serve in willing tyranny,
A cheerful slave that thanks his own constraint;
No chain so strong as love’s identity,
No law so sweet as rule that feels no taint.
If this be folly, wise men err in vain,
Their sober truths half-starved of living fire;
For what is prudence but a pale disdain,
That leaves the soul unwater’d of desire?
I lose my sense, yet gain a heart entire,
And count that loss a fortune to admire.
