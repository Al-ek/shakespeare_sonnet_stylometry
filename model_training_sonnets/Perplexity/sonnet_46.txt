XLVI

I fear the word “forever,” loud yet frail,
For men have sworn and broken through its clay;
Their oaths, though bright, must tarnish, fade, and pale,
When fleeting hearts grow wearied of their stay.
Yet thou hast taught me “ever” means no end,
But deep continuance unseen, yet true;
It lives not bound by time or how we bend,
But breathes as spirit stitched between the two.
So though we die, our bond shall not decay,
For death but trims the lamp to brighter glow;
And when the world forgets the light of day,
Our flame shall burn where only heavens know.
I trust that truth beyond the reach of flesh,
Whose mighty calm makes “ever” new and fresh.
