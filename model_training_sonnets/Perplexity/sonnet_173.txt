XIX

When first I wrote, I thought to seize the muse,
To bind her flight and wear her voice for mine;
But she, unchained by flattery or ruse,
Escaped, and left me longing for her sign.
Yet from that want a truer song was born,
Not learnt from art, but whispered from despair;
The best of words are those by sorrow worn,
Their polish dull, their sweetness rough and rare.
So shall I write, though grace may never bless,
Though silence mark the margin of my page;
For pain itself knows deepest tenderness,
And tempers youth with unexpected age.
The poet’s gain is born of what he lacks,
A shining truth that every heart attacks.
