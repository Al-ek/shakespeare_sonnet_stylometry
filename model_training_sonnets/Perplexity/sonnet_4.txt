IV

Mine eyes accuse my heart of reckless choice,
While heart protests that sight was first deceived;
Each blames the other’s all-persuading voice,
And neither grants the wrong it has conceived.
For when I saw thee, reason took to flight,
And judgment faltered at thy sovereign grace,
Yet heart declares it followed but the sight,
That first proclaimed thee worthy of such place.
So stand they both in argument unresolved,
Two stubborn judges none can reconcile,
Till love, the cause, by love itself absolved,
Turns all their strife to laughter and a smile.
Thus error thrives where beauty holds its throne,
And gladly errs, content to be o’erthrown.
