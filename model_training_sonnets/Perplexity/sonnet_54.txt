LIV

Not marble cold nor brass defiant strong,
Can match the softness wrought in mercy’s chain;
For pity rightens wrong more deep than song,
And turns all sorrow into sweeter gain.
Thy kindness, like the dawn on troubled bay,
Transforms the tempest to a golden field;
And every wound that pride had made its way,
Is healed by one fair smile thy eyes revealed.
Let kings decree and nations war again,
Their might is smoke compared to gentle grace;
The hard shall fall, the cruel break in vain,
But love alone shall crown the human race.
In every heart where mercy is the guest,
The world itself is reconciled and blest.
