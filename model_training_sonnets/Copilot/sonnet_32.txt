XXXII

The painter strives with colors rich and rare
To catch the beauty nature freely gives;
Yet though his canvas glows with skillful care,
It lacks the breath by which true splendor lives.
So too my heart, though eager to portray
The wonder of your spirit pure and bright,
Finds every effort faltering in its way,
For mortal craft falls short of such a sight.
Yet still I try, compelled by love’s command,
To shape in verse the grace I cannot show;
Though words may fail, they reach with trembling hand
Toward truths too deep for any art to know.
Accept these lines, though flawed in every part,
For love, not skill, has guided all my art.
