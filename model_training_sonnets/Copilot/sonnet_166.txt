XII

How strange that love, which once I held in doubt,
Should now command my thoughts with sovereign sway;
For long I kept its gentle power out,
And feared the wounds it left in others’ way.
But thou, with patient hand and steady light,
Didst show me love not as a fleeting flame,
But as a star that guides through darkest night,
And leads the wandering heart to peace again.
Thus have I learned, through thee, what love may be—
Not tempest wild, nor passion’s brief delight,
But quiet strength that sets the spirit free,
And holds its course through every changing plight.
So let the world speak lightly of love’s art—
I know its truth, for thou hast taught my heart.
