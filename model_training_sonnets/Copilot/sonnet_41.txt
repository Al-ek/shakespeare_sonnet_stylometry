XLI

The tender hush that crowns the break of day
Falls soft upon the fields where shadows part;
And in that quiet glow my thoughts convey
The secret longings dwelling in my heart.
For though the world awakes with bustling sound,
My spirit lingers where your memory lies;
No sweeter solace have I ever found
Than dreaming still beneath your gentle eyes.
Yet dawn must call me forth to toil and care,
And bid me leave the warmth of reverie;
But though I go, my heart remains still there,
Held fast by bonds no distance sets free.
Thus every dawn, though bright with promise new,
Begins and ends in thoughts that turn to you.
