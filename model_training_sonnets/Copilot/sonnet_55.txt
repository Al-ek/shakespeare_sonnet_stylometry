LV

The quiet brook that murmurs through the glen
Sings soft of peace the world too soon forgets;
It soothes the weary hearts of troubled men,
And washes clean their sorrows and regrets.
Yet sweeter still than any brook’s refrain
Is that soft comfort found within your voice;
It calms the storm, relieves the deepest pain,
And bids the fainting spirit to rejoice.
What music could surpass such gentle sound?
What melody could rival such a grace?
Your words restore what grief has overthrown,
And lift the soul to its appointed place.
Thus though the brook may charm the listening ear,
Your voice alone brings peace that lingers near.
