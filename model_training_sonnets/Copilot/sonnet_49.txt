XLIX

The winter wind that sweeps across the plain
May chill the bone and freeze the trembling air;
Yet though it howls with bitter, harsh disdain,
It cannot touch the warmth that you prepare.
For in your love there lies a steady flame
That burns against the coldest night of fear;
It guards the heart from sorrow, guilt, and shame,
And keeps the weary spirit ever near.
What frost could dim the fire your kindness gives?
What storm could quell the comfort of your voice?
In you my truest consolation lives,
And in your presence all my fears rejoice.
Thus winter’s wrath may rage without control,
Your love remains the hearth that warms my soul.
