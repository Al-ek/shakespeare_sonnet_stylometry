XV

When evening spreads her cloak of fading gold,
And shadows stretch across the quiet land,
I feel the weight of all the hours untold
That long to rest within thy gentle hand.
For every day that keeps us far apart
Grows heavy with the silence of my ache;
Yet still I guard the hope within my heart
That time, at last, our parted hours will break.
And when that day arrives, as come it must,
The night itself shall tremble at our joy;
For love, long patient in its tempered trust,
Shall rise renewed, no distance to destroy.
Till then I wait, as steadfast as the sea—
My longing deep, yet anchored still in thee.
