XXIV

The seasons shift, yet still my thoughts remain
Fixed fast upon the memory of your face;
Though winter storms may lash the earth with rain,
Your smile restores the world to gentle grace.
When spring returns with blossoms newly born,
I see your beauty in each budding flower;
And when the summer gilds the ripened corn,
Your warmth returns in every golden hour.
But autumn, with its quiet, dying light,
Reminds me most of how our time has flown;
For though the day gives way to creeping night,
My heart still keeps the glow it once had known.
Thus through the year your presence does not part,
For every season lives within my heart.
