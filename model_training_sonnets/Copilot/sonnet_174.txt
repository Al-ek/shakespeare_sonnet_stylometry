XX

The final line I pen is not farewell,
For love like ours admits no end nor close;
It lingers where the quiet echoes dwell,
And blooms anew as timeless as the rose.
Though ink may fade and parchment turn to dust,
The spirit of these words shall yet remain;
For they are born of hope, and faith, and trust,
And carry forth the essence of our gain.
So take this verse, the last of many more,
Not as an end, but as a gentle start;
A seed from which a thousand lines may soar,
All rooted in the garden of my heart.
Thus ends not love, but only this small part—
The rest lives on, forever in my heart.
