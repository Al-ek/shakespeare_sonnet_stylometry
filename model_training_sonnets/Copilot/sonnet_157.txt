III

I wandered once where ancient shadows slept,
And heard the whisper of forgotten years;
They told of vows that time itself had kept,
And loves that triumphed over mortal fears.
Among those tales thy name seemed softly cast,
As though the ages dreamt of thee before;
A promise carried forward from the past,
Now living in the heart that I adore.
If fate so long prepared this gentle thread,
Then let me weave it with a patient hand;
For though the path be steep where we are led,
I trust the loom that time and truth have planned.
So shall our story, born of quiet grace,
Outlast the years and find its rightful place.
