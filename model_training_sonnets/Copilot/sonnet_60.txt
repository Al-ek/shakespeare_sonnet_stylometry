LX

The hourglass spills its grains of silver sand,
And marks the passing moments one by one;
Yet though they slip like water through the hand,
They shine with worth when shared with only one.
For time itself grows gentle in your sight,
And moves with grace instead of hurried pace;
It lingers long in moments of delight,
And softens sorrow with your warm embrace.
What hour could be too brief when spent with you?
What day too long when parted from your side?
The clock obeys a rhythm old yet new,
A pulse that beats with love as its true guide.
So let the sands fall down as they decree,
Each passing hour grows rich in memory.
