XXVI

The moon, that pale and solitary queen,
Ascends her throne to watch the dreaming earth;
She casts her silver gaze on every scene,
Yet finds no sight to match your radiant worth.
For though her beams enchant the quiet night,
And stir the tides with soft, commanding grace,
They cannot rival your resplendent light,
Nor steal the wonder shining in your face.
I wander through the dusk in reverent awe,
As though the heavens whispered of your name;
And though the stars obey their ancient law,
Your brilliance sets their trembling hearts to shame.
So let the moon reign on her lofty height,
I worship you, the truer source of light.
