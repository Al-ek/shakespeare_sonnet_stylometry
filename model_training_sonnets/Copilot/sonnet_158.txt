IV

Why should I fear the turning of the hour,
When every moment spent with thee is sweet?
The clock may boast its cold, relentless power,
Yet cannot steal the joy when our eyes meet.
For time, though proud, is but a passing guest,
A traveler who lingers but a day;
While love, once kindled in a faithful breast,
Outlives the sun and will not fade away.
Let others chase the minutes as they flee,
And tremble at the shadows on the wall;
I measure life by what thou art to me,
And find no dread in time’s advancing call.
So long as thou art near, my heart is strong—
With thee, the briefest moment grows lifelong.
