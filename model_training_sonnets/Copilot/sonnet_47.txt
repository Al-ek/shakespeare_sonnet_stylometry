XLVII

The sculptor shapes the marble with his hand,
And seeks within the stone a form divine;
He chips away at what he cannot stand,
To free the beauty hidden in its line.
So too does love reshape the human soul,
And carve away the faults that weigh it down;
It smooths the rough, restores the fractured whole,
And lifts the heart to claim its rightful crown.
For in your gaze I find a gentler art
Than any mortal craft could hope to show;
It molds the very substance of my heart,
And teaches it the tenderness you know.
Thus love becomes the sculptor of my breast,
And you the form that gives its labor rest.
