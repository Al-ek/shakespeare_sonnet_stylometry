XLIII

The poet seeks a muse of rarest kind,
A spark to set his slumbering thoughts aflame;
Yet though he roams the chambers of his mind,
He finds no fire until he speaks your name.
For in that sound lies music yet unheard,
A harmony that stirs the deepest part;
It wakes the soul more swiftly than a word,
And bids new life to rise within the heart.
No other muse could grant such sweet command,
Nor shape such visions with a single breath;
Your presence guides the trembling poet’s hand,
And lifts his verse from silence into death.
So long as you inspire my humble art,
My pen shall serve the kingdom of your heart.
