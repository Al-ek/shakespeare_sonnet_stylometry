XI

The river winds through valleys green and wide,
Its waters murmuring secrets as they flow;
It carves its path with patient, gentle pride
And shapes the land with movements soft and slow.
So too hath love reshaped my wandering soul,
Not with the force of storms that rend and tear,
But with a quiet strength that makes me whole
And smooths the jagged edges buried there.
I marvel at the changes thou hast wrought,
The peace that settles where my fears once lay;
Thy kindness bends the currents of my thought
And guides my drifting heart along its way.
Thus like the river, love flows calm and true,
And all its sweetest waters spring from you.
