LIV

The summer noon that burns with golden fire
May dazzle eyes and warm the ripened field;
Yet though its heat fulfills the earth’s desire,
It fades at dusk and to the night must yield.
But your bright warmth endures beyond the day,
And lingers long when all the world grows cold;
It lights my path in every darkened way,
And grants me courage when my heart grows old.
What sun could boast a radiance so pure?
What flame could match the kindness you bestow?
Your love alone remains forever sure,
A steady glow no shadow can overthrow.
So let the sun descend in crimson hue,
My brightest warmth is found alone in you.
