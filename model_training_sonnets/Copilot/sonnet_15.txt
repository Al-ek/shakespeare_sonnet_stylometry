XV

The traveler seeks a road that leads him home,
A path familiar to his weary feet;
He wanders far through forest, field, and loam
Until at last his longing finds retreat.
So have I roamed through many a restless year,
My heart uncertain where its rest might be;
But all my searching ended when, my dear,
I found my refuge safe within thee.
For home is not a roof of stone or wood,
Nor hearth that burns with ever‑glowing flame;
It is the place where one is understood,
Where love stands guard and calls us by our name.
Thus though the world be wide and full of strife,
My home is thee, the shelter of my life.
