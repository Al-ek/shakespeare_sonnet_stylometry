XLVI

The distant bells that toll at eventide
Call weary souls to rest from daily care;
Yet though the world grows still on every side,
My thoughts find no such peace when you’re not there.
For in your absence, silence speaks too loud,
And shadows lengthen where the light once shone;
My heart, once bold, grows timid, faint, and bowed,
And wanders lost when left to stand alone.
But when you come, the bells ring bright again,
Their chimes restored to harmony and grace;
The night grows warm, the world forgets its pain,
And joy returns to fill the empty space.
So let the bells toll on in solemn hue,
Their sweetest song is heard when played for you.
