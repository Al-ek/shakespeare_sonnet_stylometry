XXII

The winds that wander through the silent glade
Repeat your name in whispers soft and low;
They stir the leaves as though your hand had made
A gentle sign to set their dance in flow.
I follow where they lead, though paths are dim,
For love compels me farther than my will;
And though the forest grows with shadows grim,
Your presence bids my trembling soul be still.
What power has fear when love commands the way?
What gloom can stand against such sovereign fire?
Though night may press and steal the light of day,
Your memory lifts my fainting heart higher.
Thus through the dark I walk with steady mind,
For where love leads, no soul is left behind.
