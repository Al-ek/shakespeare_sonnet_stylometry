XXXV

The roses bloom with fragrance rich and sweet,
Their petals soft as whispers in the air;
Yet though their beauty stands in full complete,
It fades too soon, undone by time’s despair.
But your fair grace no withering can claim,
No passing season dims your gentle light;
For though the world may change and shift its frame,
Your constancy remains both firm and bright.
What flower boasts a charm that does not die?
What blossom keeps its splendor through the years?
Yet you, whose worth no mortal can deny,
Outshine all blooms that earth or heaven bears.
So let the roses fall to dust and rain,
Your beauty lives where time cannot attain.
