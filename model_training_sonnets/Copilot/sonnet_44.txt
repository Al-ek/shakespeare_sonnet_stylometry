XLIV

The fading light that crowns the western hill
Reminds me how our moments slip away;
For though the sun returns by nature’s will,
No hour once lived can greet another day.
Yet in that truth I find no cause for grief,
For every fleeting moment spent with you
Becomes a treasure rich beyond belief,
A jewel time itself cannot undo.
Though days may pass and seasons rise and fall,
And youth retreat before the march of age,
Your memory stands firm above them all,
A constant star no darkness can engage.
So let the sun descend in crimson hue,
My brightest light remains the thought of you.
