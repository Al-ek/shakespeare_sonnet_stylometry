VII

When fortune smiles, men boast of what they own,
And count their treasures with a greedy hand;
But I, though poor in riches, stand alone
More blessed than kings who rule a wealthy land.
For thou art mine, and in thy gentle gaze
I find a wealth no coin could ever buy;
A fortune bright enough to set ablaze
The darkest corners where my sorrows lie.
Let others chase the glitter of the gold,
Or seek the praise of crowds that roar and cheer;
I ask but this: that I may still behold
Thy face, and know thy heart remains sincere.
For in thy love I hold a kingdom fair,
And reign content, though worldly goods be spare.
