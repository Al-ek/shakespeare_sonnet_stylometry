VII

The world may boast of treasures rare and fine,
Of jewels wrested from the ocean’s hold;
But none so bright as that pure light of thine,
Which shames the proudest gem with living gold.
For stones, though fair, are cold to mortal touch,
And glitter only where the sun is cast;
But thou dost warm the soul with gentle much,
And shine within the heart when day is past.
Let merchants praise the wealth their ships have borne,
And kings parade their crowns with haughty air;
I envy not their riches, though well-worn,
For thou art wealth beyond all worldly care.
Thus am I rich, though fortune pass me by—
My treasure lives wherever thou art nigh.
