II

The winds that wander through the distant hills
Bear whispers of a peace I seldom find;
They cool the heat of all my mortal ills
And soothe the restless corners of my mind.
But stronger still than any roaming breeze
Thy voice commands my storms to quiet lie;
It bends my grief as branches bend to trees
And lifts my hope as wings lift birds to sky.
If fate should scatter us on foreign ground
And cast our paths apart by cruel design,
I’d seek thee still, though none but ghosts be found,
And trust the stars to guide my steps to thine.
For though the world may shift like drifting sand,
My love remains, unmoved by time’s command.
