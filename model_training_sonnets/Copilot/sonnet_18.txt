XVIII

I’ve heard the tales of heroes long since gone,
Of battles fought and victories hard‑won;
Their names endure in story, verse, and song,
Their deeds remembered though their days are done.
Yet none of these, in all their vaunted fame,
Could match the quiet courage thou dost show;
For thou hast faced thy trials without claim
To glory’s crown or praise from those who know.
Thy strength is not the strength of sword or shield,
But that which rises from a gentle soul;
A power born of kindness, softly sealed,
That lifts the weary and the broken whole.
So let the bards their ancient heroes praise;
I honor thee, whose grace outshines their days.
