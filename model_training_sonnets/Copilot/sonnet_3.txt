III

O let my verse be humble in its aim,
For words are frail when weighed against thy grace;
Yet still I strive to shape thy noble name
In lines that time itself cannot erase.
The painter’s brush may falter in its stroke,
The sculptor’s hand may tremble at the stone,
But love, once kindled, never can be broke,
Nor can its fire be quenched by flesh or bone.
So grant my pen a moment’s borrowed flame
To trace the truth my heart would fain impart:
That none but thee could set my soul aflame
Or claim the throne that rests within my heart.
And though my verse be simple, plain, or few,
It lives for one sweet purpose: praising you.
