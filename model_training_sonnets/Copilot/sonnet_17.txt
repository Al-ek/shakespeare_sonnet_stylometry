XVII

The oak stands firm against the winter’s breath,
Its branches bare, yet resolute and strong;
It weathers frost, and though it seems near death,
It waits for spring to right the season’s wrong.
So stands my love when trials gather near,
Unshaken by the winds of doubt or fear;
Though storms may rise and shadows linger here,
It holds its ground, enduring year by year.
For love is not a fragile, fleeting thing
That wilts when faced with sorrow’s heavy hand;
It is a root that clings through suffering,
A steadfast force no grief can countermand.
Thus let the winter rage with icy art;
My love stands firm, a fortress of the heart.
