I

When dawn first breaks and spills her molten gold,
The earth, half‑waking, stirs beneath her light;
So stirs my heart, long quiet, grown cold,
Revived anew by thy returning sight.
For though the night may claim its sable throne
And press its silence on my weary breast,
Thy presence lifts the dark from flesh and bone
And grants my troubled spirit gentle rest.
Thus every morn becomes a sweeter birth,
And every hour a gift I dare not waste;
For time, that thief, steals all we love on earth,
And leaves behind but shadows faintly traced.
Yet while thou walk’st beside me, bright and true,
No dusk can dim the world I share with you.
