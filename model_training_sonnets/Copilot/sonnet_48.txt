XLVIII

The traveler who counts his worldly gains
May boast of riches gathered far and wide;
Yet though he stores his gold in countless chains,
He lacks the wealth that in your heart resides.
For treasure kept in vaults will fade with rust,
And jewels lose their luster in the night;
But love, once given freely in its trust,
Outshines all gems with everlasting light.
What fortune could I seek beyond your grace?
What riches rival what your smile bestows?
The proud may chase the world from place to place,
But I hold dear the love that ever grows.
So let them boast of wealth and golden store,
I claim a treasure richer than their ore.
