XXXVII

The traveler who roams from land to land
Seeks wonders vast and treasures yet unseen;
He scales the mountains, crosses desert sand,
And marvels at the world in shifting sheen.
Yet I, who wander not from place to place,
Have found a richer world within your eyes;
For there I see the truth of love and grace,
A realm more fair than earth or distant skies.
What need have I of journeys far and wide,
When all I seek resides within your heart?
The world may boast of wonders in its pride,
But none can match the beauty you impart.
So let the traveler chase horizons new,
My greatest voyage ends and starts with you.
