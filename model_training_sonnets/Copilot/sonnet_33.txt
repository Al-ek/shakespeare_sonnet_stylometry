XXXIII

The morning sun climbs slowly through the sky,
And gilds the world with soft, forgiving light;
It warms the fields where dewy shadows lie,
And drives away the remnants of the night.
Yet though its beams bring comfort to the land,
They cannot soothe the ache within my breast;
For only you possess the gentle hand
That calms my fears and grants my spirit rest.
The sun may rise and set with faithful grace,
But you alone can lift my weary soul;
No earthly light can match your shining face,
Nor grant the peace that makes my being whole.
So let the sun adorn the waking day,
My heart finds dawn in you and not in ray.
