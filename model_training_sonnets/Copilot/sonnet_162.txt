VIII

When storms arise and shake the trembling ground,
And all the sky is torn with thunder’s cry,
I seek no shelter but where thou art found,
For in thy presence tempests learn to die.
Thy voice, more soothing than the softest rain,
Can calm the winds that rage within my breast;
Thy touch can banish every inward pain,
And lull my restless fears to gentle rest.
What need have I of fortress, shield, or wall,
When thou, my steadfast refuge, standest near?
Though all the world in ruin round me fall,
I hold my peace, for thou hast stilled my fear.
Thus in thy love I find my surest ground—
A harbor safe when storms are all around.
