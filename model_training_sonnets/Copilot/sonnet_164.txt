X

I once believed my heart a guarded keep,
A fortress none could enter or command;
But thou didst come with footsteps soft and deep,
And laid thy gentle claim with open hand.
No siege was laid, no battle cry was heard,
No force employed to win my trembling will;
Thy kindness was the only conquering word,
Thy patience wrought the victory soft and still.
Thus am I thine, not by compulsion bound,
But by the quiet wonder of thy grace;
For in thy gaze a sweeter truth I found
Than any pride my heart had sought to chase.
So let the world proclaim its conquests wide—
My greatest triumph is to stand at thy side.
