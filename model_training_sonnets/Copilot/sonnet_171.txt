XVII

I marvel oft how thou, with simple grace,
Canst turn my heaviest sorrow into song;
For where despair once held its bitter place,
Thy presence bids it cease its grieving wrong.
No counsel learned, nor wisdom deep and old,
Could soothe my troubled mind with such sweet art;
Yet thou, with but a glance, a touch, a hold,
Dost quiet all the storms within my heart.
What magic this, that needs no spell nor charm,
But flows from thee as rivers from the spring?
A gentle power, free of threat or harm,
That lifts my soul as though on unseen wing.
Thus am I healed by thee, in ways unknown—
Thy love the balm that makes my spirit whole.
