XXXVI

The quiet hours that linger after dusk
Bring thoughts that wander where they will not rest;
They drift like motes of light through shadows husk,
And settle softly in my troubled breast.
For in the stillness, memory takes its throne,
And summons visions of our brighter days;
Though joy and sorrow mingle in its tone,
It warms my heart in strangely tender ways.
I do not flee these recollections dear,
Though some bring ache and others gentle peace;
For every thought of you, though edged with fear,
Becomes a balm that bids my longing cease.
Thus in the hush of night I find anew
That all my solace lives in thoughts of you.
