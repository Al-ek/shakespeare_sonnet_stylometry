XX

If ever words could capture all I feel,
Or bind in verse the truth my heart would speak,
I’d write a song no time could e’er unseal,
A testament for future souls to seek.
But language fails, as mortal things must do,
And leaves me grasping shadows of the real;
For nothing wrought by pen can equal you,
Nor can my craft the depth of love reveal.
Yet still I try, compelled by hope alone,
To shape in rhyme the passion I confess;
Though flawed, these lines are seeds that I have sown,
A garden grown from longing’s tenderness.
And if they bloom in some small, humble part,
Let them bear witness to my faithful heart.
