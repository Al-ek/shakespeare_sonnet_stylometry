X

The poet seeks a muse to stir his pen,
A spark to light the forge of living art;
He wanders far through crowds of mortal men
Yet finds no flame to warm his cooling heart.
But I, though humble in my craft and voice,
Have found in thee the fire my verse requires;
Thy presence bids my weary lines rejoice
And fills my barren thoughts with sudden fires.
If ever I should lose this sacred spark
And find my words grown silent, cold, or few,
I’d look to thee to banish all the dark
And breathe again the breath my poems knew.
For thou alone canst rouse my slumbering mind
And teach my tongue the grace it longs to find.
