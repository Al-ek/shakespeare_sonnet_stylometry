XXVII

When weariness has chained my limbs to rest,
My mind escapes and swiftly flies to you;
It seeks the place where once my heart was blessed,
And paints again the scenes it fondly knew.
Though darkness veils the world from mortal sight,
My inward eye beholds you clear and bright;
And though the hours stretch long throughout the night,
Your presence turns my solitude to light.
But when the dawn returns with weary pace,
My thoughts retreat, as dreams are wont to do;
Yet still I rise, sustained by fleeting grace,
For even dreams are sweet when shaped by you.
Thus night and day in equal measure prove
That all my rest is bound within your love.
