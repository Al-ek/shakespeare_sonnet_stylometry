XI

The morning breeze that stirs the waking field
Seems charged with whispers carried from thy lips;
And every flower its dewy fragrance yields
As though it drank thy sweetness in soft sips.
The birds that trill their songs at break of day
Repeat the music of thy gentle tone;
The brook that winds along its silver way
Reflects the calm that in thy eyes is shown.
Thus nature, in her quiet, earnest art,
Doth echo thee in every tender part;
And I, who walk among her works divine,
Find all her beauty but a shade of thine.
For though the world in loveliness is dressed,
It is thy love that makes my spirit blessed.
