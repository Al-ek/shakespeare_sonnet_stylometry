VI

If I could borrow from the poet’s art
A phrase to crown thee with immortal praise,
I’d search the boundless chambers of my heart
Where thou hast walked in unimagined ways.
But language falters where affection grows,
And stumbles at the threshold of thy worth;
No mortal tongue the full of thee bestows,
Nor captures all thy grace in lines of earth.
Yet still I try, compelled by love’s command,
To shape my awe in humble, trembling rhyme;
For though my craft be weak, my purpose stands—
To honor thee beyond the reach of time.
So take these lines, imperfect though they be,
And read in them the truth thou art to me.
