XL

The final word I speak before the night
Is always shaped by memory of you;
It lingers softly in the fading light,
A vow renewed with every dusk anew.
For though the day may bring its cares and woes,
And weigh my spirit down with weary sighs,
Your thought returns like fragrance from a rose,
And lifts my heart until the sorrow dies.
No darkness holds a terror for my soul,
No shadow dares to whisper doubt or fear;
For love, once kindled, keeps the spirit whole,
And burns with strength whenever you are near.
So ends the day, yet not my love’s refrain,
For dawn will wake and find it whole again.
