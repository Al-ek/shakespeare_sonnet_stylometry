XVIII

The day may boast its bright and cloudless sky,
And claim perfection in its golden hue;
Yet even then my thoughts to thee will fly,
For thou art fairer than the fairest view.
No landscape, though adorned with nature’s pride,
Can match the wonder living in thy gaze;
No sunlit field, no river deep and wide,
Can stir my heart as thou in simplest ways.
For beauty born of earth is bound to fade,
And yields at last to time’s relentless hand;
But thou, whose grace no mortal craft has made,
Dost shine with light no years can countermand.
Thus all the world, though lovely it may be,
Is but a shadow when compared to thee.
