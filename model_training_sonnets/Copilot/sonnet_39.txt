XXXIX

The twilight hour, when day and night embrace,
Holds secrets whispered only to the few;
It paints the sky with hues of tender grace,
A fleeting vision born of dusk and dew.
In such a moment, standing still and lone,
I feel your presence nearer than before;
Though distance keeps us parted, flesh and bone,
My spirit walks beside you evermore.
For love defies the boundaries of space,
And binds two hearts though miles may intervene;
It finds its way through every time and place,
And weaves a bond no mortal eye has seen.
So in the hush of twilight’s gentle hue,
I feel the world grow small between us two.
