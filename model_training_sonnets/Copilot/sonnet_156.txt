II

The seasons wheel as if in eager chase,
Each striving to outshine the one before;
Yet none can match the glory of thy face,
Nor paint such warmth upon my spirit’s door.
Spring strews her blossoms with a lavish hand,
But thou dost bloom with grace no frost can kill;
Summer may rule with heat across the land,
Yet thou alone can calm my fevered will.
Autumn, in robes of fire, struts the field,
But lacks the flame that in thine eyes is set;
Winter, though stern, to thee must humbly yield,
For thou canst melt the coldest mortal threat.
Thus nature’s pride, though vast her rich display,
Bows low to thee, who outshines all her sway.
