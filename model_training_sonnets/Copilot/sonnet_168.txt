XIV

The poet’s quill may dance with nimble grace,
And weave a thousand tales of sweet desire;
Yet none can match the warmth of thy embrace,
Nor set my trembling spirit more afire.
For words, though fair, are but a fleeting breath,
A moment’s echo cast upon the air;
But thou art life itself, defying death,
And in thy truth my soul finds solace rare.
So let the poets chase their lofty themes,
And fill the world with verses bold and bright;
I seek no glory in their woven dreams—
My muse is thee, my ever‑constant light.
Thus all my lines, however humbly spun,
Are lit by thee, my heart’s unsetting sun.
