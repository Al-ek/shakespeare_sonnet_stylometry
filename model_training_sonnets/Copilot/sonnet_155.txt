I

When dawn unbars the gates of weary night,
And spills her gold upon the drowsing land,
My thoughts, long wandering, take sudden flight
To where thy gentle virtues silent stand.
For though the world with clamor fills the air,
And every hour demands its noisy due,
One breath of thee dissolves my darkest care,
And shapes my scattered hopes to purpose new.
Thus am I bound, though distance stretch between,
By cords no mortal hand can cut apart;
Thy memory walks where I have never been,
And plants its quiet garden in my heart.
So let the day grow proud with rising sun—
My truest light is thee, and thee alone.
