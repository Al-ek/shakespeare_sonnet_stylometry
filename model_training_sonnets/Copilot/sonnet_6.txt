VI

The nightingale may pour her silver song
Across the quiet hush of twilight’s veil,
And though her notes in sweetest measure throng,
They fade like mist before the morning gale.
But thou, whose voice outshines the bird’s refrain,
Dost plant thy music deep within my soul;
It lingers there when silence falls again
And makes my broken spirit newly whole.
No melody of earth or sky above
Could match the tune that rises when thou’rt near;
For every word thou speak’st is shaped by love,
And every breath thou draw’st is sweet to hear.
Thus let the nightingale her praises keep;
Thy voice alone can charm my heart to sleep.
