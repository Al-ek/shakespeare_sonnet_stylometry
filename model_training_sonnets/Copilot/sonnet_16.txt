XVI

The painter dreams of colors rich and rare,
Of hues that mortal eyes have never seen;
He seeks to catch the light that fills the air
And fix it fast upon a canvas clean.
Yet though his palette holds a thousand shades,
None match the glow that lives within thy gaze;
For there the warmth of dawn in silence fades,
And there the fire of summer gently plays.
If I could mix the pigments of the sun
With all the silvered tones of midnight’s gleam,
Still would my work fall short when it was done,
A pale reflection of a brighter dream.
For art may strive, but never can impart
The living beauty written in thy heart.
