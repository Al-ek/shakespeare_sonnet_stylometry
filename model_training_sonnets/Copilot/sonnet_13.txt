XIII

I walked alone beneath the twilight sky
And felt the hush of evening settle near;
The fading light bade all the day’s cares die,
And left my thoughts to wander soft and clear.
In that still hour, thy memory arose
And filled the quiet air with gentle grace;
It bloomed within my mind like some rare rose
Whose fragrance lingers long in empty space.
Though thou wert far, I felt thee close at hand,
As if thy shadow walked beside my own;
And in that moment did I understand
That love needs not the flesh to make it known.
For though the miles between us stretch and bend,
Thy presence lives where thought and longing blend.
