XXVIII

The world grows harsh with cares that press the mind,
And burdens heavy weigh upon the soul;
Yet in your voice a gentleness I find
That lifts my heart and makes the broken whole.
No storm can shake the calm your presence brings,
No sorrow dim the comfort of your smile;
For though the world may bruise with cruel stings,
Your kindness heals and strengthens all the while.
What greater gift could fortune ever send
Than one whose love restores the weary heart?
Though time may steal the treasures others spend,
Your grace remains, untouched by fate or art.
So let the world bring trials as it will,
Your love alone can make my spirit still.
