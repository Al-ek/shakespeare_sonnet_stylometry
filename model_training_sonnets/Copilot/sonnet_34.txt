XXXIV

The ocean’s roar resounds upon the shore,
A ceaseless hymn of longing and release;
It speaks of journeys ended, battles o’er,
And restless hearts that yearn for quiet peace.
I stand beside its ever‑moving tide,
And feel its pulse beat steady with my own;
For though the waves may wander far and wide,
They always find their way again to home.
So too my heart, though tossed by storm and strife,
Returns to you with every rising swell;
For you remain the harbor of my life,
The refuge where my truest feelings dwell.
Thus like the sea, my love forever flows,
And evermore toward you it comes and goes.
