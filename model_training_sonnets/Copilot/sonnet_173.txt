XIX

If ever doubt should whisper in thine ear,
And question whether thou art loved indeed,
Let this my vow dispel thy fleeting fear—
My heart is thine in thought, in word, in deed.
No passing fancy, born of idle hour,
Nor shallow flame that flickers in the night;
But love that grows with ever‑deepening power,
And stands unshaken in the fiercest plight.
For though the world may shift like drifting sand,
And fortune’s wheel may turn with fickle spin,
My love remains, a rock on steadfast land,
Unmoved by storm, unbroken from within.
So doubt no more—my heart is wholly thine,
A truth eternal as the stars that shine.
