LIX

The ancient oak that stands against the years
Has weathered storms no mortal eye recalls;
It bears the marks of time, of joy, of tears,
Yet still it rises though the tempest falls.
So stands my love, though tested by the days,
Unmoved by sorrow, strengthened by the strife;
It grows more firm beneath your gentle gaze,
And deeper rooted in the soil of life.
What force could shake a bond so strongly cast?
What wind could bend a heart so tightly bound?
Though time may change the future from the past,
My love remains in steadfast purpose found.
Thus like the oak that towers strong and free,
My heart endures through all for love of thee.
