LIII

The river winds through valleys deep and wide,
And carves its path with patient, steady flow;
It moves with purpose none can turn aside,
And shapes the land wherever it may go.
So does my love, though quiet in its course,
Move ever toward the refuge of your heart;
No barrier can halt its gentle force,
Nor keep its faithful waters far apart.
Though mountains rise and storms may intervene,
It finds its way through every trial and test;
For love, once set upon its course serene,
Will not be stayed until it finds its rest.
Thus like the river flowing to the sea,
My heart moves ever closer unto thee.
