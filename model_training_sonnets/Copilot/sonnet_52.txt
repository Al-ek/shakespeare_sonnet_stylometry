LII

The painter’s brush may capture fleeting light,
And fix a moment on the waiting frame;
Yet though his colors glow with beauty bright,
They cannot hold the warmth from whence they came.
So too my verse, though crafted with intent,
Cannot contain the fullness of your grace;
For every line, however eloquent,
Falls short of what I see within your face.
Yet still I write, compelled by love’s command,
To honor what no mortal art can show;
My trembling pen obeys your gentle hand,
And seeks to praise the truth I long to know.
Thus though my words may falter in their aim,
They rise in hope to celebrate your name.
