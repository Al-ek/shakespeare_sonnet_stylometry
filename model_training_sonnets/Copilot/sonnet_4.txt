IV

The seasons turn with ever‑faithful pace,
And each repeats the tale of life anew;
The spring with blossoms, summer with its grace,
The autumn’s fire, the winter’s frosted hue.
Yet none of these, in all their shifting guise,
Can match the changes wrought within my breast
When thou art near, for then my spirits rise
And find in thee their solace and their rest.
But when thou’rt gone, the world grows pale and thin,
Its colors drained, its music faint and low;
A barren field where once my joys had been,
A silent path where once my hopes did grow.
Thus nature’s wheel may turn both fair and true,
But all my seasons bloom or fade with you.
