LVI

The autumn leaves that drift upon the breeze
Fall softly as the year begins to fade;
They whisper tales of summers spent at ease,
And speak of time’s unyielding, gentle blade.
Yet though the world grows older day by day,
My love for you grows younger in its bloom;
For every hour spent in your sweet sway
Dispels the creeping touch of age and gloom.
What season could diminish such a flame?
What winter chill could cool its fervent glow?
Your love remains untouched by time or shame,
A fire no frost or shadow can overthrow.
So let the leaves fall down in rustling hue,
My heart stays evergreen in thoughts of you.
