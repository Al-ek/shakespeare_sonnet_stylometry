XXXVIII

The poet’s craft depends on borrowed fire,
A spark that leaps from heart to trembling hand;
Yet though his verses rise with bold desire,
They falter when true greatness they withstand.
So I, who strive to praise your noble worth,
Find every line too frail to bear the weight;
For though my thoughts soar high above the earth,
My words fall short and stumble at the gate.
Yet still I write, compelled by love’s decree,
For silence is a burden hard to bear;
And though my verse may lack in mastery,
It speaks the truth no ornament can spare.
Thus take these lines, imperfect as they are,
For love alone has shaped them from afar.
