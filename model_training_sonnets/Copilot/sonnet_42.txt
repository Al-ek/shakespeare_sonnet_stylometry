XLII

The storm that breaks upon the trembling land
May shake the oak and scatter leaves abroad;
Yet still it bends to some unseen command,
And yields its fury to the will of God.
So too my heart, though tossed by grief and fear,
Submits at last to love’s enduring sway;
For when your voice speaks soft, the skies grow clear,
And all my doubts like fleeing clouds give way.
What tempest dares to challenge such a power,
Or stand against the calm your presence brings?
Your grace transforms the wildest, darkest hour,
And turns the thunder’s roar to gentle wings.
Thus though the storm may rage with might untold,
Your love remains my refuge strong and bold.
