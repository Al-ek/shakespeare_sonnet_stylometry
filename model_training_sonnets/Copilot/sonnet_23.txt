XXIII

My pen rebels when I would write your praise,
As though it knew its reach could never climb
To match the splendor of your gentle ways
Or catch your grace in meter, rhyme, or time.
For words are but the shadows of the truth,
And truth itself too vast for mortal art;
Yet still I try, as once I did in youth,
To shape a verse that mirrors half your heart.
But every line falls short of what I seek,
And every phrase betrays my bold intent;
Still I persist, though craft and skill are weak,
For silence is a heavier punishment.
So take these lines, imperfect though they be,
And read in them the love that governs me.
