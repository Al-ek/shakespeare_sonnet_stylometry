XIII

The path we walk is neither smooth nor straight,
But winds through valleys shadowed deep with care;
Yet still I trust the hand that shapes our fate,
For thou art with me, and thy presence fair.
What need have I of certainty or chart,
When thou, my compass, pointest ever true?
Though doubts may rise to trouble mind and heart,
They fade like mist when I take hold of you.
For in thy steadiness my fears grow small,
And in thy courage mine is born anew;
Though mountains loom and heavy shadows fall,
Thy light breaks through and shows the way we’ll do.
Thus hand in hand we walk where fate may lead—
With love our guide, we want for nothing need.
