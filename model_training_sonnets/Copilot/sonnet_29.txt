XXIX

The distant hills grow blue with evening haze,
And shadows lengthen on the quiet ground;
Yet still my heart recalls our brighter days,
When joy was near and sorrow seldom found.
Though now the path grows steep with toil and care,
And hope seems lost beneath the weight of years,
Your memory remains a lantern fair
That guides my steps and dries my silent tears.
For though the world may change with passing time,
And youth give way to age’s sober truth,
Your love endures in every gentle rhyme
My heart repeats from days of tender youth.
Thus even now, when all seems worn and old,
Your love returns to warm me from the cold.
