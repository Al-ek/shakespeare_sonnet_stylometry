VIII

The moon, half‑cloaked in drifting veils of cloud,
Hangs silent o’er the sleeping fields below;
Her silver beams, though gentle, still are proud,
And lend the night a soft, enchanted glow.
Yet even she, in all her borrowed light,
Must bow before the radiance thou dost bear;
For thou outshin’st the sovereign of the night
And mak’st her pale in envy of thy fair.
If heaven’s host should gather in array
And cast their brilliance down upon the land,
Still would their splendor falter, fade, and sway
Before the glow that rests within thy hand.
Thus let the moon her quiet vigil keep;
Thy light alone commands my heart’s deep sleep.
