XXI

When dawn first breaks and spills her trembling gold,
I wake to thoughts that wander where you are;
For though the night leaves all the world so cold,
Your memory burns brighter than a star.
The hours move on with slow, reluctant feet,
As if they fear to tread where joy has been;
Yet still I chase the ghost of moments sweet,
And search the day for traces left unseen.
But time, that thief, denies my earnest plea,
And steals the warmth that once adorned my breast;
Still I persist, for hope remains in me,
A stubborn guest that never will find rest.
So let the morning rise or fade away,
My heart keeps vigil for your light each day.
