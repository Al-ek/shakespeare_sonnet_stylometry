IX

I’ve seen the storms that lash the trembling sea
And watched the waves rise up in wild despair;
Yet none of these could frighten or unfree
My steadfast love, which stands beyond compare.
For though the winds may howl with bitter might
And tear the sails from ships that brave the deep,
My heart holds firm, unshaken in its plight,
And guards the vow it silently must keep.
No tempest born of sky or earthly strife
Could break the bond that binds my soul to thine;
For thou art anchor, compass, chart, and life,
The star that guides me when the night grows fine.
So let the storms unleash their cruelest art;
They cannot move the harbor of my heart.
