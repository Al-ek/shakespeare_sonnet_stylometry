XVI

The world may change with every passing breath,
And all we know may crumble into dust;
But love, once sworn, outlasts the hand of death,
And stands when all else falters in its trust.
For though the years may carve their lines in stone,
And time may steal the vigor of our youth,
The heart that loves with constancy alone
Preserves its strength in honesty and truth.
Thus let the world grow old and worn with care,
And let the seasons shift from bloom to bare;
For I shall hold thee still, though all things fade,
And cherish thee though every star degrade.
So long as breath remains within my breast,
My love for thee shall never be at rest.
