XXXI

The nightingale that sings in twilight’s shade
Pours forth a tune that stirs my soul to ache;
For in her notes a longing is conveyed
That only hearts in love can truly wake.
She mourns the fading of the dying day,
Yet finds her solace in the rising moon;
So I, though parted from your side, must stay
Content in hope that we shall meet full soon.
Her song becomes the echo of my grief,
A melody that mirrors my desire;
Yet in its sorrow lies a sweet relief,
A promise whispered through her trembling choir.
For though we part, the nightingale reminds
That love endures in hearts and in their minds.
