XXX

If I could bargain with the fleeting hour,
And hold it still when you are by my side,
I’d trade the world for such a precious power,
And let all else be lost, or cast aside.
For time, that tyrant, envies every bliss,
And steals the moments lovers hold most dear;
It mocks the sweetness of a fleeting kiss,
And leaves behind the bitter taste of fear.
Yet though it robs us of our dearest days,
It cannot touch the truth our hearts have known;
For love, once kindled, never more decays,
But grows in strength though all the world has flown.
So let time rage and tear the hours apart,
It cannot break the bond within the heart.
