XIV

The morning dew upon the meadow lies,
A thousand jewels scattered on the green;
They glimmer bright beneath the waking skies
And vanish swift, as though they’d never been.
So fleeting are the joys that life bestows,
So quick to fade, so easily undone;
Yet one remains, though all the others close—
The steadfast warmth of love that thou hast won.
No passing hour can steal it from my breast,
No sudden grief can dim its constant flame;
It stands unmoved when all the world’s at rest
And whispers still the comfort of thy name.
Thus though the dew may melt with morning’s hue,
My love endures, and ever blooms for you.
