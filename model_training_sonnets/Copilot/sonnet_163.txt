IX

The painter strives with color, brush, and frame
To catch the fleeting beauty of the day;
Yet fails to seize the essence of thy name,
Which lives in realms no mortal art can sway.
For beauty such as thine is not of form,
Nor bound by line, nor held in shade or light;
It moves like music through the gathering storm,
And turns the darkest hour to pure delight.
Let artists chase the visions that depart,
And sculptors carve their dreams in stone and clay;
But I, who hold thy image in my heart,
Need no such craft to keep thy grace in play.
For thou art art itself, in living guise—
A masterpiece that time cannot disguise.
