XIX

The twilight hour, when day and night embrace,
Holds secrets whispered only to the few;
It paints the sky with colors soft as lace
And bids the weary world begin anew.
In such an hour I find my thoughts of thee
Grow tender as the fading of the light;
They drift like petals on a quiet sea
And settle deep within the heart of night.
For love is sweetest when the world grows still,
When noise and haste give way to gentle peace;
It blooms in silence, shaped by human will,
And finds its strength when all distractions cease.
Thus in the hush where dusk and darkness meet,
Thy memory comes, and makes the moment sweet.
