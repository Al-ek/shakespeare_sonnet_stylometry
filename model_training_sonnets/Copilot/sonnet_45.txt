XLV

The gentle breeze that wanders through the vale
Carries the scent of blossoms newly born;
It whispers tales the springtime winds unveil,
Of life renewed and winter’s sorrow shorn.
Yet sweeter still than any breath of spring
Is that soft grace your presence brings to me;
For though the flowers bloom and robins sing,
Their beauty pales beside your constancy.
What season boasts a charm that does not fade?
What bloom endures beyond its destined hour?
Yet you, whose worth no passing time can shade,
Remain more steadfast than the fairest flower.
Thus spring may come with all its fragrant art,
But none can match the bloom within your heart.
