LI

The weary steed that gallops through the field
Finds strength renewed when nearing stable door;
For though the road demands he never yield,
He knows his rest awaits forevermore.
So too my heart, though burdened by the day,
Finds solace when it turns its thoughts to you;
For in your love all weariness gives way,
And every sorrow fades from mortal view.
What refuge could I seek beyond your grace?
What comfort rivals what your smile imparts?
Your presence is the home I long to face,
The haven built within our kindred hearts.
Thus though the world may press with ceaseless strife,
Your love remains the rest that steadies life.
