V

The night, in velvet silence, veils the sky,
And bids the weary world to gentle rest;
Yet still my thoughts to thee unbidden fly,
As though thy name were written in my breast.
No star so constant in its shining course,
No moon so faithful in her silver round,
As is my heart, compelled by unseen force
To seek thee out where peace and truth are found.
Though darkness spreads her mantle far and wide,
She cannot dim the flame that thou hast lit;
For in that glow my wandering hopes abide,
And all my doubts in quiet patience sit.
Thus even night, though deep her silent sea,
Is brightened by the thought that drifts to thee.
