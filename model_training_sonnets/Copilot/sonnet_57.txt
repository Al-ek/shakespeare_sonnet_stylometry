LVII

The distant choir that sings at break of morn
Lifts up its voice to greet the waking skies;
Its notes, though soft, with purity are born,
And stir the soul to wonder as they rise.
Yet though their song ascends with beauty rare,
It cannot match the music of your grace;
For in your presence all my doubts repair,
And every sorrow finds its resting place.
What choir could sing a hymn so sweet and true?
What chorus match the comfort you impart?
Your love alone composes something new,
A melody that lives within my heart.
Thus though the morning choir may charm the air,
Your song alone dispels my deepest care.
