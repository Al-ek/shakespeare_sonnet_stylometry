XXV

Let others chase the fleeting wings of fame,
And carve their names on monuments of stone;
Such brittle honors perish all the same,
And leave the seeker desolate, alone.
But I, content with humbler joys than these,
Find richer treasure in your gentle gaze;
For though the world may shift like restless seas,
Your constancy outshines a thousand days.
What need have I of crowns or laurel leaves,
When love bestows a kingdom of its own?
The proud may boast of triumphs time retrieves,
But I hold fast to joys they’ve never known.
For in your heart I find my noblest part,
A realm more vast than glory can impart.
