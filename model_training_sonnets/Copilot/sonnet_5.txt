V

I wandered once through gardens rich and wide,
Where roses climbed and lilies bent with grace;
Yet none among them, though in beauty dyed,
Could rival thee in form or gentle face.
For flowers live but briefly in the sun,
Their petals falling with the evening dew;
But thou endurest when their days are done,
And hold’st a charm no season can undo.
If I could pluck the stars from heaven’s dome
And weave them in a garland for thy hair,
Still would my gift fall short, for light alone
Cannot reflect the glow that thou dost bear.
So let the garden keep its fleeting bloom;
Thy beauty needs no borrowed sweet perfume.
