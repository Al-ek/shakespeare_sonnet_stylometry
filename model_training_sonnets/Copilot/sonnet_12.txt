XII

Time’s hand is stern, and none may stay its pace;
It steals the bloom from every youthful cheek,
And leaves behind the lines that years encase,
A map of joys and sorrows we must speak.
Yet though the world may bow to time’s command,
And beauty fade as seasons rise and fall,
Thy worth stands firm, untouched by shifting sand,
For virtue needs no youth to shine at all.
If age should claim the brightness of thine eyes
Or dim the glow that once adorned thy face,
Still would my heart, untroubled, recognize
The deeper charms no years can e’er erase.
For love, once true, grows richer with the years,
And finds its strength through laughter, grief, and tears.
