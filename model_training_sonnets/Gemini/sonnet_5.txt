V

The violet that bows its purple head,
Doth lose its scent when summer days are done,
And though the garden thinks the flower dead,
Its essence lives beneath the golden sun.
So let thy spirit find a vessel new,
To hold the perfume of thy gracious mind,
Before the chilling frost and morning dew
Shall leave no trace of what thou art behind.
For time is like a thief within the night,
That steals the treasure from the locked chest,
And turns the morning’s glory into blight,
To put the weary traveler to rest.
But in thy verse, thy fragrance shall endure,
To keep thy memory forever pure.
