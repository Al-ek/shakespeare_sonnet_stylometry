LV

Though brass should crumble and the iron rust,
And granite mountains wear away to sand,
Though all the works of men return to dust,
And silence covers all the busy land;
This verse shall stand as a defiant tower,
Against the ravages of wind and rain,
Preserving through the long and heavy hour,
The beauty that the world would seek to drain.
For ink is stronger than the mason's stone,
When it is guided by a heart that's true;
It builds a kingdom that is thine alone,
And keeps the morning ever fresh for you.
Till the last fire shall consume the earth,
These words shall tell the story of thy worth.
