XXIII

As music swells within the hollow wood,
To touch the air with trembling, sweet delight,
So would I sing thy praises if I could,
And wake the silence of the lonely night.
But words are heavy, laden with the clay,
While thy perfections soar on wings of light;
The brightest verb is but a cloudy day,
When set against the sun of thy dear sight.
Then let my pulse become the beating drum,
That marks the rhythm of a love unspoken;
For when the voice of art is rendered dumb,
The heart’s own music remains yet unbroken.
If speech should fail to tell what I adore,
Believe my silence, for it tells thee more.
