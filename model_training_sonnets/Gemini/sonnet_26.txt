XXVI

My pen I take, a soldier’s rusted blade,
To fight the battle for thy memory’s sake,
Against the legions that the years have made,
And all the toll that greedy Time will take.
With every stroke I carve a lasting line,
To stand as rampart for thy gentle name,
That through the ages thy bright light may shine,
With an undying and a holy flame.
Though mortal flesh must wither and decay,
And monuments of stone to dust must fall,
These ink-black verses shall not pass away,
But rise triumphant o'er the funeral pall.
Let kings be buried in their tombs of lead,
In these few words thou shalt not join the dead.
