IV

How like a ship upon a restless sea,
My heart is tossed by every wind of doubt,
Seeking a shore that leads me home to thee,
While all the storms of passion rage without.
The stars are hidden by the veils of night,
And no familiar landmark meets my gaze,
I have no compass but thy constant light,
To lead me through this dark and weary maze.
Should tempest break the mast and rend the sail,
And leave me drifting on the barren deep,
Thy memory shall o’er the waves prevail,
And guard the lonely vigil that I keep.
For thou art both my anchor and my port,
My only refuge in this earthly court.
