XIX

If I could write the music of thy voice,
In notes that never could be lost or changed,
The very heavens would at last rejoice,
To see the harmony so well arranged.
But human songs are fleeting as a breath,
And music dies as soon as it is heard,
Falling at last into the hush of death,
Without a single or a lasting word.
Yet in these lines a melody remains,
A silent song for those who have the ear,
To catch the rhythm of the heart’s sweet strains,
And feel the presence of the one so dear.
Though all the instruments should break and fail,
Thy music lives within this simple tale.
