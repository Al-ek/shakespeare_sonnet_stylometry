VIII

The painter takes his brush to catch the light,
And fix the color of a fleeting day,
To save the vision from the coming night,
And keep the shadows of the world at bay.
But paint will crack and canvas start to fray,
And all the colors fade into the gloom,
Until the masterpiece has passed away,
Into the silence of a dusty room.
But I do paint thy portrait with my pen,
In words that colors never could express,
To show the world, and all the sons of men,
The secret of thy hidden loveliness.
No brush could ever capture half thy grace,
As these few lines that mirror back thy face.
