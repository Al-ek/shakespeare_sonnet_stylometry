XII

The clock doth strike the hours of the night,
With heavy tongue that tells of passing time,
And mocks the day with all its golden light,
In every slow and melancholy chime.
I count the beats that lead me to the grave,
And see the shadows lengthen on the floor,
Knowing that nothing in this world can save,
The weary soul from death’s eternal door.
But then I think of thee, and time doth stand,
As if the sun had paused within his race,
And I am held by thy gentle hand,
Within the harbor of thy sweet embrace.
The clock may tick, but love knows no such thing,
For in thy heart, it is forever spring.
