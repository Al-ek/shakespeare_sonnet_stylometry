XXIX

When Fortune turns her fickle wheel away,
And leaves me stranded on a barren shore,
When all the promises of yesterday,
Are like the echoes of a closing door;
I look within to find a hidden wealth,
More precious than the bullion of a king,
Which grants the spirit a perpetual health,
And makes the heavy, laden heart to sing.
For though the world may strip me of my pride,
And cast me down into the common dust,
With thy affection safely kept inside,
I hold a treasure that can never rust.
Let others scramble for the golden prize,
I find my riches in thy loving eyes.
