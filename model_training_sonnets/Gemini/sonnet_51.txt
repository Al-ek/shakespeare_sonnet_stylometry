LI

But when I turn my face toward home again,
What horse can match the speed of my desire?
The fastest steed would labor then in vain,
To keep the pace of my internal fire.
Then would I wish for wings of eagles' might,
To soar above the valleys and the hills,
And vanish like a flash of morning light,
To cure the spirit of its thousand ills.
The slow return is agony to bear,
While every second seems a weary year;
I'd pierce the very bosom of the air,
To reach the harbor that I hold so dear.
Until I see the threshold of thy door,
My soul shall be a traveler evermore.
