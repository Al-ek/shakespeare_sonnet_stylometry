VII

If I should leave this weary world behind,
And sleep within the dark and silent earth,
Let not thy soul to sorrow be inclined,
Nor dwell upon the moment of my birth.
For what is man but dust and fleeting breath,
A shadow passing o’er a sunlit wall?
There is no victory in the sting of death,
If love can rise above the final fall.
Remember only that I loved thee well,
With all the strength a mortal heart can hold,
More than the chronicles of time can tell,
Or all the stories that were ever told.
And if thou find’st a flower on my grave,
‘Tis but the ghost of love I freely gave.
