LIII

The world is but a gallery of glass,
Reflecting various aspects of thy grace;
The summer flowers and the waving grass,
Are but the shadows of thy lovely face.
In every beauty that the seasons bring,
I find a fragment of thy perfect soul;
In every melody the poets sing,
Thy name is the beginning and the goal.
Speak of the spring, and I will show thy youth;
Speak of the harvest, and I see thy wealth;
In every ancient tale of love and truth,
I find the spirit of thy inner health.
Though many shapes the busy world may wear,
It is thy image that makes them all fair.
