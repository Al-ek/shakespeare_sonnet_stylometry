XIII

The mountain peak is crowned with silver snow,
That never melts beneath the summer sun,
And looks upon the valleys far below,
Where all the little rivers leap and run.
Thou art to me that high and holy height,
A place of peace above the worldly noise,
A beacon in the middle of the night,
To keep the balance of my spirit’s poise.
And though the clouds may gather ‘round thy head,
And lightning strike against thy stony face,
Thy strength is like the earth’s eternal bed,
A firm foundation for my soul’s own grace.
Let storms arise and let the heavens shake,
My faith in thee is one they cannot break.
