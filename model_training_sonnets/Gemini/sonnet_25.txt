XXV

When winter’s frost has turned the world to white,
And chilled the marrow of the naked tree,
I find a summer in the softest light,
That radiates from thoughts of only thee.
The rose may wither in the biting air,
And brooks be bridled by the ice’s chain,
But in my heart the garden remains fair,
Untouched by snow or by the freezing rain.
For love provides a fire for the soul,
To warm the spirit through the darkest cold,
Making the fractured elements a whole,
And turning iron hours into gold.
Let Boreas rage and all the tempests blow,
Thy love’s the only warmth I need to know.
