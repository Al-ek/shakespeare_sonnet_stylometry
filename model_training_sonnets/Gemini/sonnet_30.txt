XXX

In hours of silence when the world is still,
And memory summons up the ghosts of old,
I climb the summit of a weary hill,
To count the losses that the years have told.
I mourn for friends who vanished in the mist,
And weep for loves that withered in the bud,
For every opportunity I missed,
That left me drowning in a bitter flood.
But then the thought of thee, like morning sun,
Disperses every shadow from the brain,
And tells me that the race is not yet run,
And gives a solace to the sharpest pain.
All losses vanish and all sorrows flee,
When I but turn my quiet thoughts to thee.
