II

When winter’s frost shall bite the tender leaf,
And silver age doth crown thy brow with white,
Think not that beauty is a common thief,
Who steals away the visions of delight.
For though the mirror tells a tale of woe,
And maps the lines where youth was wont to dwell,
Within thy heart a deeper grace shall grow,
More fair than any tongue of man can tell.
The outward frame is but a house of clay,
Subject to ruin and the worm’s decree,
But love doth hold a more enduring sway,
And builds a temple for eternity.
Look not upon the glass to find thy worth,
But in the love that gave thy spirit birth.
