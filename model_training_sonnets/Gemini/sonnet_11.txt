XI

Upon the shore I saw a child at play,
Building a castle in the shifting sand,
But soon the tide did wash the walls away,
And left a level waste upon the strand.
So do we build our lives with pride and care,
As if the walls could stand against the sea,
But time is like a tide that’s always there,
To sweep away the things that used to be.
What then remains of all our toil and strife,
When we are gone into the great unknown?
The only mark we leave upon this life,
Is in the seeds of love that we have sown.
For walls will fall and castles turn to dust,
But love survives, because it always must.
