IX

When in the glass of memory I view,
The many faces I have known before,
I find that none are fashioned quite like you,
Though I have searched the world from shore to shore.
Some had the grace of lilies in the spring,
And some the fire of the summer rose,
But thou art every fair and lovely thing,
That in the garden of the spirit grows.
Thou art the seed, the blossom, and the fruit,
The very sap that feeds the thirsty vein,
And though the winter strike the branch and root,
Thy beauty in my garden shall remain.
For other blooms must wither in the cold,
While thy perfection never shall grow old.
