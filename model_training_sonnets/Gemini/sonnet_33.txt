XXXIII

I saw the sun arise in crimson state,
To kiss the meadows with his golden breath,
And for a moment, in a joy so great,
I thought the world had conquered every death.
But then the clouds, in grey and somber line,
Did wrap the glory in a damp embrace,
And forced the heavenly light to cease to shine,
And hid the beauty of his glowing face.
Even so my love, which blossomed in thy smile,
Now finds itself beneath a stormy sky,
Since thou hast turned away for a brief while,
To let the promise of our union die.
Yet if the sun can bear the clouds of night,
I’ll wait in darkness for thy returning light.
