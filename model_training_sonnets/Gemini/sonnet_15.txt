XV

When I behold the moon in silver dress,
Walking the heavens like a lonely queen,
I see a mirror of thy loveliness,
The purest vision that was ever seen.
But as the moon doth change her subtle phase,
And hide her face within the veil of night,
So do the fortunes of our earthly days,
Wax and then wane within our weary sight.
Yet thou art constant as the northern star,
That guides the sailor through the stormy deep,
And shines upon the traveler from afar,
While all the rest of heaven is asleep.
No change of fortune can thy soul displace,
Or mar the beauty of thy holy face.
