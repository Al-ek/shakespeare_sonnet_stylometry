XXII

When in the glass I view my weathered brow,
And see the lines that sorrow’s plow has made,
I wonder how the love I proffer now
Can seek a dwelling in so deep a shade.
For thou art morning in its freshest gold,
While I am twilight, fading fast to grey;
Thou art the story yet to be unrolled,
And I the scrap the wind would cast away.
Yet love is not a tenant of the skin,
Nor does it build its house on fleeting youth,
But finds a sanctuary deep within,
In the foundations of a steady truth.
Though mirrors mock what Time has done to me,
My soul remains a mirror, holding thee.
