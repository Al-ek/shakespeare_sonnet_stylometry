XLVII

A truce is signed between the eye and heart,
That each may prosper in the other's gain;
The eye shall play the messenger's brief part,
To ease the burden of the heart's deep pain.
When eye is pining for a glimpse of thee,
The heart shall offer up its store of thought;
When heart is drowned in sorrow's heavy sea,
The eye shall bring the image it has caught.
So in the absence of thy living face,
Thy picture or thy memory serves them both,
Filling the empty and the silent space,
And fostering the spirit's quiet growth.
Though thou art far, thy presence is so near,
The eye can see, the heart can always hear.
