XVI

The eagle soars above the rocky height,
With wings that beat against the thin blue air,
And watches with a keen and steady sight,
The world that lies in all its beauty there.
My spirit seeks to fly to where thou art,
To rise above the heavy mist of earth,
And find the quiet center of thy heart,
Where all my better thoughts have had their birth.
But I am bound by chains of mortal clay,
And cannot reach the heavens of thy mind,
But must in silence wait the coming day,
When all these earthly bonds are left behind.
Until that time, I send my love on high,
To meet thy spirit in the open sky.
