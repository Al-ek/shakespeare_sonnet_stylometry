XIV

The rose is queen of all the garden fair,
With petals soft as velvet to the touch,
And sends a fragrance through the morning air,
That makes the weary heart to love too much.
But thorns are hidden deep within the green,
To guard the beauty of the crimson flower,
A reminder of the pain that lies between,
The joyful moment and the final hour.
So is thy love a mixture of the two,
A sweet delight that carries still a sting,
A sky of gold that turns to sudden blue,
The heavy burden that the seasons bring.
But I would take the thorn to have the rose,
As every lover in the kingdom knows.
