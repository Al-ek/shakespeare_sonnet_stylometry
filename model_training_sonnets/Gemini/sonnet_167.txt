XIII

No more be grieved at what thou hast forgot,
For memory is but a heavy chain,
That binds the spirit to a weary lot,
And feeds the ancient appetite of pain.
The past is but a ghost that haunts the hall,
A voice that whispers from a shuttered room,
It has no power to make the kingdom fall,
Nor any right to dictate now thy doom.
The present is the only truth we know,
A golden moment in the stream of time,
Where love has room to flourish and to grow,
Without the tarnish of a former crime.
Let yesterday be buried with its dead,
And look upon the path that lies ahead.
