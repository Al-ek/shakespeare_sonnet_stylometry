XLII

If thou shouldst love another for my sake,
And think to pay me with a double debt,
It is a hollow comfort that I take,
A sun that rises only for to set.
For love divided is a love decreased,
No matter how the sophistry is spun;
The famine grows whene'er the soul has feasted,
On fragments when it hungers for the one.
But since my soul and thine are so entwined,
That what thou lovest, I must love as well,
I seek a peace that I can never find,
Within the borders of this double spell.
My friend and thee I love with equal fire,
The twin-born objects of my soul's desire.
