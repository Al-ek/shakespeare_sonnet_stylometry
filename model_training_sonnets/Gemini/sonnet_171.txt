XVII

Thou art the book wherein my life is writ,
In characters of fire and of gold,
And though I lack the scholar’s ready wit,
Thy story in my spirit shall unfold.
Let others read the chronicles of kings,
Or study maps of lands beyond the sea,
I find the meaning of all earthly things,
Within the pages dedicated to thee.
Each word is like a promise well-bestowed,
Each line a path that leads me to thy grace,
And though the journey be a heavy road,
I find my heaven in thy quiet face.
If I should lose the power of my tongue,
Thy praises in my heart shall still be sung.
