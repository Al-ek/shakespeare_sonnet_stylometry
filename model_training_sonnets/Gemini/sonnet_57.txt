LVII

I am a servant waiting at thy gate,
To do the bidding of thy slightest whim;
I do not murmur at my lowly state,
Nor let the candle of my faith grow dim.
While thou art seeking pleasure where thou wilt,
And spending hours in some other's sight,
I do not question if there be a guilt,
But watch the slow approach of lonely night.
For love has made me a submissive slave,
Who has no will except to see thee blest;
I'd follow thee into the very grave,
And find in thy command a perfect rest.
Think what thou wilt, and go where'er thou please,
I am the shore, and thou the restless seas.
