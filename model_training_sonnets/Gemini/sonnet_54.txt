LIV

The rose is lovely for its outward show,
The velvet petal and the crimson hue,
But in its heart a deeper grace does grow,
A sweetness that is ever fresh and new.
The wild rose has a beauty just as bright,
And hangs its head upon the thorny spray,
But when its petals fall into the night,
Its scentless memory withers quite away.
But thy sweet virtue is a lasting scent,
That shall survive when all thy bloom is gone;
A holy fragrance that the heavens lent,
To linger when the earthly race is won.
When youth has faded and thy colors die,
Thy truth shall live within this poetry.
