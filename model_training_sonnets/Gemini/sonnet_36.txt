XXXVI

As some poor sailor on a shattered mast,
Looks o'er the waste of the devouring sea,
And sees the harbor where he anchored last,
Fading away into the memory;
So do I watch the distance grow between,
Our separate paths that once were joined as one,
And mourn the beauty that might well have been,
Before the setting of our little sun.
The winds of fate have blown us far astray,
To different latitudes of grief and pain,
And though I labor through the weary day,
I fear we shall not meet on earth again.
Yet on the map of heaven, I shall find,
Thy soul still anchored in my lonely mind.
