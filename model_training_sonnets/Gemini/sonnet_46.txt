XLVI

A civil strife is waged within my breast,
Between my hungry eye and pining heart,
For each would claim the prize it loves the best,
And keep the other from the smallest part.
The eye would feast upon thy outward frame,
The symmetry and color of thy skin;
The heart would whisper thy beloved name,
And find its portion in the soul within.
They sit in judgment on their separate claims,
Like rival kings who seek a single throne,
Calling upon the gods by various names,
To make the victory of their passion known.
But let them cease their quarrel and agree,
To share the glorious banquet found in thee.
