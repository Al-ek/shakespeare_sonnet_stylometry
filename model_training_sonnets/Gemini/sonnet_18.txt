XVIII

The harvest field is ripe with golden grain,
The fruit of all the summer’s heat and toil,
And waits the coming of the heavy swain,
To gather in the riches of the soil.
Our love is like a harvest rich and deep,
The slow result of many patient years,
A treasure that we both shall strive to keep,
Through all the season of our joy and tears.
And though the winter comes with icy breath,
To lay the garden and the meadow low,
We have a store that triumphs over death,
A fire to warm us in the falling snow.
The barns are full, the work of life is done,
And we shall rest beneath the setting sun.
