XXXII

I strive to paint thy portrait with my rhyme,
To catch the essence of thy living grace,
And hold it captive from the thief of Time,
Who seeks to ruin every lovely face.
But colors fade upon the mental cloth,
And brushes stumble in the shaking hand,
For art is fragile as a fluttering moth,
While thy perfections like the mountains stand.
The best I offer is a shadow’s trace,
A pale reflection of the fire I feel,
Which cannot mirror the majestic pace,
Of a devotion that is true and real.
Yet take this tribute, though it be but slight,
A candle’s flicker in thy blinding light.
