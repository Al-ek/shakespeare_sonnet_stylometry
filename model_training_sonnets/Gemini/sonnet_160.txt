VI

Against the tyranny of cruel time,
Who mows the meadow with a rusted blade,
I build this fortress with a humble rhyme,
That thy perfection never may be decayed.
He robs the rose of all its crimson pride,
And turns the emerald field to dusty brown,
With nowhere for the frightened soul to hide,
When he doth cast the lofty towers down.
But he shall find no entrance to this verse,
Which keeps the essence of thy spirit still,
And though he may the living world disperse,
He cannot bend my passion to his will.
So long as men can read and eyes can see,
This song shall give a lasting life to thee.
