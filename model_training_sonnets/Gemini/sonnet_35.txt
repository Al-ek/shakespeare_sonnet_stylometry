XXXV

The weaver sits before the tangled loom,
To cross the threads of sorrow and of joy,
To make a pattern in the quiet room,
Which neither age nor malice can destroy.
The dark threads are the days when we’re apart,
The gold are those when I am by thy side,
And all are woven in my aching heart,
To form a tapestry both deep and wide.
Though some may see but knots and ragged ends,
When viewed from behind the heavy frame,
The Master’s hand a hidden beauty lends,
To every trial that we cannot name.
Our life’s a fabric that the heavens spin,
Where love is both the needle and the pin.
