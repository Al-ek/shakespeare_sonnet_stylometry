LX

Like waves that beat upon the pebbled shore,
The minutes hasten to their final end;
Each taking what the other gave before,
While all toward the vast oblivion tend.
Youth flourishes and then is mowed away,
By the sharp scythe of a relentless hand;
The golden tresses turn to silver grey,
As time's cold shadow covers all the land.
Yet in the middle of this vast decay,
Thy beauty stands like a resilient flower,
Defying the progression of the day,
And the dark menace of the final hour.
My verse shall be a wall against the tide,
Wherein thy memory shall safely hide.
