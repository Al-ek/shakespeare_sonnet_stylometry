XL

If I have wronged thee by a word or deed,
Or failed to render what was justly due,
I pray thee, love, to plant a mercy seed,
And let thy kindness start our bond anew.
For even saints may stumble on the road,
And even mirrors show a distorted face,
And every traveler bears a heavy load,
That makes him weary in the frantic race.
Forgiveness is the jewel of the mind,
The brightest star in all the human sky,
Which leaves the bitterness of earth behind,
And lifts the spirit where the angels fly.
So take my hand and let the past be gone,
And let us walk together toward the dawn.
