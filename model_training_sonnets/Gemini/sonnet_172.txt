XVIII

The weaver plies his trade with nimble hand,
To make a tapestry of rich design,
With threads of silk from many a distant land,
Wherein the colors of the rainbow shine.
But all his skill can never hope to match,
The subtle weaving of thy gentle soul,
Wherein the light of heaven seems to catch,
And make the broken parts of nature whole.
Thou art the warp and woof of my desire,
The very fabric of my every thought,
And though the world should set the loom on fire,
The pattern of our love cannot be caught.
For we are woven with a thread so strong,
It lasts as long as virtue conquers wrong.
