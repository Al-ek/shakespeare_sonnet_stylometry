VII

When I consider how the stars do shine,
In silent watch above the sleeping world,
I see a pattern in their grand design,
Like banners of a mighty king unfurled.
But stars themselves must flicker and go out,
As aeons pass and galaxies grow cold,
Leaving the universe in lonely doubt,
Of stories that the ancient heavens told.
Yet thou, my love, dost hold a brighter flame,
Than any spark that lights the midnight sky,
And though the dark may try to claim thy name,
The light of truth shall never, ever die.
For love is not a slave to space or time,
But lives forever in a simple rhyme.
