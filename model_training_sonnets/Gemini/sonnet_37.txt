XXXVII

The glutton sits before a laden board,
And stuffs his belly with the richest fare,
Yet finds his spirit is no more restored,
Than if he feasted on the empty air.
For hunger of the soul is not appeased,
By wine or meat or any earthly thing,
Nor is the heavy, burdened conscience eased,
By all the pleasures that the world can bring.
But I, who lack the comforts of the great,
And dine on crusts within a humble stall,
Am more contented in my low estate,
Because thy love is greater than them all.
Thy kindness is the bread on which I live,
The only blessing that the heavens give.
