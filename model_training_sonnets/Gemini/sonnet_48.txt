XLVIII

I locked my jewels within a chest of oak,
To keep them safe from every prying hand,
And hid my garments from the common folk,
Lest they should soil what I had carefully planned.
But thee, the only treasure that I prize,
The jewel that outshines the purest gold,
I leave exposed to all the world's sharp eyes,
In the wide field where common things are sold.
For how can I contain thy spirit's light,
Or build a wall to keep thy beauty in?
Thou art as public as the stars of night,
Yet free from any touch of earthly sin.
I fear the thief who wanders through the street,
To steal the only thing that makes me sweet.
