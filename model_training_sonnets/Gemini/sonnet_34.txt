XXXIV

The prisoner who dwells in iron chains,
Does count the moments by the falling tear,
While in his breast a heavy sorrow reigns,
And every heartbeat is a pulse of fear.
So is my soul a captive to thy will,
Locked in a dungeon where no light can stray,
Waiting for thee the emptiness to fill,
And turn the midnight to a joyful day.
For thou dost hold the key to my release,
And keep the pardon in thy gentle hand;
One word from thee could bring a perfect peace,
And lead me safely to a better land.
Then be not cruel to a heart so bound,
But let the music of thy grace resound.
