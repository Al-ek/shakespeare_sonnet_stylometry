XLIV

If thought were flesh and bone as heavy as this,
It could not leap the valleys and the seas,
To find the sanctuary of thy kiss,
And find a respite in a moment's ease.
But thought is swift and lighter than the wind,
Unchecked by distance or by iron bars,
Leaving the sluggish body far behind,
To seek its station 'mongst the distant stars.
Yet herein lies the torment of my state:
That while my mind is anchored in thy grace,
My heavy limbs must bear the leaden weight,
Of miles that keep me from thy lovely face.
I am a spirit trapped in walls of clay,
That longs for wings to fly the miles away.
