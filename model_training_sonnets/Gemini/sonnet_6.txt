VI

The sea doth beat upon the rugged shore,
With waves that follow waves in endless line,
And though the waters crash with heavy roar,
They cannot break the spirit of the brine.
My love for thee is like the restless sea,
That knows no boundary and knows no end,
But flows from out my weary soul to thee,
A gift that only heaven’s grace could send.
And though the years may wash the sands away,
And change the features of the coastal land,
My heart shall find a place wherein to stay,
Safe from the reach of Time’s destructive hand.
For in this song, thy name is carved in stone,
A kingdom where thou reignest all alone.
