XLIII

In sleep my eyes are opened to the truth,
And see thy image in the darkest night,
Restoring all the vigor of my youth,
By the mere presence of thy phantom light.
The day is dull and weary to my view,
A grey procession of unmeaning things,
Unless it brings me tidings that are new,
Upon the beating of the morning’s wings.
But night is kind and yields a golden prize,
When in the theatre of my dreaming brain,
I gaze upon the heaven of thine eyes,
And feel the pulse of ecstasy again.
If shadows have the power to make me blest,
Then let me find a permanent unrest.
