L

The horse plods on with heavy, leaden feet,
Bearing me further from the thing I love;
The very air is bitter, once so sweet,
And grey the sky that stretches out above.
The beast does groan as if he felt my grief,
And measures out the miles with weary pace,
Knowing that every step is but a thief,
That steals the sight of thy beloved face.
The spur can goad him to a faster trot,
But cannot move the burden in my breast,
For I am anchored to a single spot,
Wherein my spirit finds its only rest.
My journey leads me to a barren land,
While all my joy remains within thy hand.
