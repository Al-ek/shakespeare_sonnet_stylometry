XXVIII

Within the garden of my inward mind,
I plant the seeds of every word thou spoke,
In hopes that there some blossom I may find,
To heal the spirit that the world has broke.
The weeds of worry try to choke the vine,
And thorns of doubt arise to pierce the skin,
But still I labor at this task of mine,
To keep the beauty of the love within.
For what is life but a brief growing season,
A flash of green before the autumn’s brown?
Unless we find a high and holy reason,
To wear the laurel or the flowered crown.
My heart’s a soil that’s enriched by thee,
To bear the fruit of immortality.
