LVIII

I have no right to question where thou art,
Or ask the reason for thy long delay;
It is enough that I have given my heart,
To be the silent witness of thy way.
If thou shouldst choose to wander far and wide,
And leave me waiting in this quiet room,
I'll keep my patience and my love inside,
And find a comfort in the gathering gloom.
For who am I to bind a spirit free,
Or cast a net to hold a soaring bird?
I'll wait in silence till thou come to me,
Without a bitter or a jealous word.
Thy time is thine, to spend as thou shalt choose,
I am but one who has no heart to lose.
