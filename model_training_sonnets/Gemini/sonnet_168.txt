XIV

As through a forest dark and thick with briar,
I wandered long in search of some relief,
Consumed by all the coals of vain desire,
And heavy with the luggage of my grief.
The thorns did tear the fabric of my soul,
And every step was weary and forlorn,
With no clear vision of a distant goal,
Before the breaking of the quiet morn.
But then I found a clearing in the wood,
Where sunlight fell in patterns on the grass,
And there in silent majesty thou stood,
To watch the heavy clouds of sorrow pass.
Now let the forest close behind my back,
For I have found the only path I lack.
