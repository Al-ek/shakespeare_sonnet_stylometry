XII

The stars are jealous of thy shining eyes,
And hide their heads behind the clouds of night,
For they were born within the lonely skies,
While thou art born of pure and inward light.
They burn with fire that is cold and far,
And care not for the sorrows of the earth,
But thou art like a warm and guiding star,
That watched the very moment of my birth.
Let astronomers track the planets' course,
And name the constellations as they fly,
I seek the fountain at its holy source,
Within the heaven of thy quiet eye.
For there I see a universe more grand,
Than any mapped by man’s uncertain hand.
