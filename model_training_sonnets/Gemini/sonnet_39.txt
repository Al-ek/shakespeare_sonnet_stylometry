XXXIX

How like a desert is the time we spend,
When miles of silence stretch between us two,
And every hour seems it will never end,
Because I cannot share it, love, with you.
The parched earth gapes beneath a burning sun,
And prays for moisture from the heavy cloud,
Waiting for the day that separation’s done,
And love may speak its happiness aloud.
Absence is the rain that makes the flower grow,
Or so the poets in their wisdom say,
Testing the roots that hidden dwell below,
To see if they can survive the dusty day.
If love be strong, it thrives on distance too,
And keeps its colors of a vibrant hue.
