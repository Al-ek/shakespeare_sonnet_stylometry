III

Look in the mirror of the quiet lake,
And see the ripples mar thy perfect face;
The image there no permanence can take,
But vanishes without a lingering trace.
So Time doth play a cruel and mocking part,
To steal the luster from the brightest eye,
And plant a winter in the warmest heart,
Beneath the vast and unrelenting sky.
But if thou weave thy soul in lines of gold,
And lock thy beauty in a poet’s rhyme,
Thy story shall a thousand times be told,
To mock the scythe of all-consuming Time.
Though marble tombs shall crumble into dust,
Thy love shall live, as every spirit must.
