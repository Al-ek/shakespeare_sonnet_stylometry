LII

I am like one who keeps a hidden hoard,
Of rare and precious stones in secret deep,
Which are not on the common table poured,
But for the highest festivals I keep.
For if the feast were held on every day,
The appetite would soon begin to tire,
And all the golden joy would turn to grey,
Extinguishing the furnace of desire.
Therefore thy absence is a blessed thing,
That makes the meeting a much richer prize;
It gives the winter promise of the spring,
And adds a luster to thy searching eyes.
Blessed are those who wait for what they love,
Like saints who watch for manna from above.
