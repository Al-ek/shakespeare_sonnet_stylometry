XV

Mine eye and heart are at a mortal war,
How to divide the conquest of thy sight;
Mine eye my heart thy picture's sight would bar,
My heart mine eye the freedom of 그 light.
The eye contends it hath the greater part,
Since it doth catch the glory of thy face,
But then the heart replies, within the heart,
There is a more enduring dwelling place.
To side with eye is but to love the skin,
To side with heart is but to love the thought,
And yet I find a harmony within,
By which this civil bitter strife is brought.
For when thou art away, the eye doth sleep,
And then the heart thy image safe doth keep.
