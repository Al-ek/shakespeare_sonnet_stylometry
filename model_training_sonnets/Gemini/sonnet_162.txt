VIII

The merchant seeks his fortune on the tide,
And risks his life for bags of heavy gold,
But I have found a treasure to abide,
More precious than the wealth of kings of old.
His riches may be lost to fire or wave,
Or stolen by the cunning of a knave,
But thou art all the fortune that I crave,
And all the legacy I wish to save.
Thy smile is more than rubies in the sun,
Thy voice a sweeter sound than silver coin,
And when the race of mortal life is run,
Our souls in one rich treasury shall join.
Let others hoard the dross of worldly gain,
While I the kingdom of thy heart attain.
