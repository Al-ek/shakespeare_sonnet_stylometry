XXVII

The stars that jewel the velvet robe of night,
Are but the embers of a dying fire,
Compared unto the clear and steady light,
That feeds the furnace of my soul’s desire.
They wink and flicker in the distant sky,
Indifferent to the cares of mortal men,
But the sweet brilliance of thy searching eye,
Gives life and purpose to my tired pen.
For eyes are windows where the spirit dwells,
Revealing secrets that no tongue can name,
And in their depths a liquid music swells,
To put the heavenly constellations to shame.
Let astronomers map the paths of space,
I find my heaven only in thy face.
