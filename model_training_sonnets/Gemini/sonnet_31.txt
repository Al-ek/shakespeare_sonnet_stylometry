XXXI

The architect may build a house of stone,
With lofty pillars and a vaulted roof,
To stand against the winds that wildly moan,
And give of human skill a lasting proof.
But Time, the master of the wrecking ball,
Shall bring the highest turret to the ground,
Until the carvings from the cornice fall,
And not a single trace of it is found.
But I have built a temple in my breast,
With walls of faith and rafters of pure love,
Wherein thy spirit may forever rest,
Below the stars and in the light above.
No storm can shake this dwelling of the soul,
While thy dear hand maintains the chief control.
