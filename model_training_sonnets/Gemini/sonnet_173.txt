XIX

Is it thy will thy image should keep open,
My heavy eyelids to the weary night?
Or is it that the vows which we have spoken,
Are haunting me with visions of delight?
I cannot sleep while thou art far away,
For every pulse is beating out thy name,
And longing for the coming of the day,
To set the frozen world once more aflame.
But sleep is but a shadow of the grave,
A temporary truce with mortal care,
And I would rather be a wakeful slave,
Than miss the smallest answer to my prayer.
For in my dreams I only find a ghost,
But waking, I possess what I love most.
