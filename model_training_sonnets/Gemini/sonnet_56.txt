LVI

Awake, my love, and shake the winter's sleep,
Let not thy spirit fall into a doze;
The fires of passion we must ever keep,
Lest they should flicker as the daylight goes.
For hunger comes and goes with every day,
Satisfied now, but sharp again by noon;
So let not love be cast in such a way,
That it should wither like a dying tune.
Let every meeting be a new-born feast,
To whet the longing of the pining soul,
Until the shadows in the furthest east,
Declare the victory of the spirit's goal.
Love is a flame that needs a constant fuel,
Lest time should prove a master stern and cruel.
