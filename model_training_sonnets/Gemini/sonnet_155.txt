I

The golden sun doth climb the morning sky,
And wakes the world with fingers tipped in light,
While shadows from the valley swiftly fly,
To hide their faces from the coming sight.
So does thy beauty rise upon my soul,
And chase the heavy vapors of my grief,
Making my broken spirit once more whole,
With promises of love, though they be brief.
But even as the sun must seek the west,
And leave the earth to cold and silent stars,
So must thy glory find its final rest,
Beyond the reach of time’s eternal bars.
Yet in these lines thy summer shall not fade,
Though all the world be lost in evening shade.
