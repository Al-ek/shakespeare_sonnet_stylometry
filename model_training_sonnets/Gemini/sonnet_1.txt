I

When golden dawn doth brush the silvered dew,
And wakes the lark to sing his morning prayer,
Thy youthful face reflects a morning new,
Beyond the reach of sorrow or despair.
Yet even as the sun ascends his height,
He hastens toward the shadows of the west,
And all that glows within the morning light
Must find at last a quiet place of rest.
So shall thy bloom be gathered by the frost,
Unless thou plant a seed in fertile soil,
Lest all thy summer’s treasury be lost
To winter’s dark and unremitting spoil.
Bequeath thy grace before thy glass shall break,
And live again for thy dear offspring’s sake.
