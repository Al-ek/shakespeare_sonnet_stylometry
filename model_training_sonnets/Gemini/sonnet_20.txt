XX

The sun has set behind the western hill,
And left the world in shadows soft and gray,
The busy voices of the earth are still,
As night descends to end the weary day.
So let this book of sonnets find its end,
A quiet closing to a long desire,
A message that a lover’s heart did send,
To keep alive a small and sacred fire.
For though these words are only ink and breath,
They hold a truth that time can never kill,
A love that dares to look in face of death,
And keep its holy promise even still.
Sleep now, my love, the stars are in the sky,
And in this verse, our love shall never die.
