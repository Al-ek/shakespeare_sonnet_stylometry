V

Why should I deck thy name with borrowed praise,
Or seek for jewels in a foreign mine?
The simple truth of all thy works and ways,
Is more than any poet can define.
Let those who lack the substance of their theme,
Paint hollow pictures with a gilded pen,
And weave a fabric of a fevered dream,
To catch the eyes of vain and foolish men.
I need no art to tell how fair thou art,
Nor any wit to prove thy soul is true,
I only need the language of the heart,
To say the old things in a fashion new.
For beauty that is perfect in its frame,
Doth lose its luster by a fawning name.
