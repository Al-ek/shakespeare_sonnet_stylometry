XXXVIII

A pilgrim travels to a distant shrine,
Through desert sands and over mountains steep,
To find a comfort that is half divine,
And pay the vows he promised him to keep.
He cares not for the blisters on his feet,
Nor for the hunger that his body feels,
For in his heart he finds a holy heat,
That grows the stronger as he humbly kneels.
So have I journeyed through the wilderness,
Of doubt and sorrow to thy presence fair,
In hopes that thou mightst find the power to bless,
A heart that’s heavy with a long despair.
Thou art the temple where my soul finds rest,
The only sanctuary of my breast.
