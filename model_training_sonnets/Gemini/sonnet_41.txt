XLI

Thy beauty is a lure for every eye,
A honeyed trap where many wings are caught,
And though I watch the foolish suitors die,
I fear the lesson that their end hath taught.
For thou art sovereign in thy youthful might,
And laws of men are light as summer air,
When set against the brilliance of thy light,
Or the soft promise of a face so fair.
Yet if thou strayest from the narrow way,
To taste the fruit that grows in other fields,
Remember that the brightest summer day,
To autumn’s cold and bitter harvest yields.
Be true to me, though many seek thy grace,
Lest time should mar the virtue of thy face.
