XXIV

The restless tide beats on the shifting sand,
To claim the treasures that the shore would keep,
With every wave it reaches for the land,
Then drags the pebbles to the briny deep.
So do my thoughts, in never-ending surge,
Roll toward the harbor of thy quiet grace,
Only to feel the bitter, outward urge,
That pulls me back from seeing of thy face.
For love is like the ocean, vast and wide,
With currents hidden from the landsman’s eye,
Possessing both the power and the pride,
To lift the spirit or to let it die.
Yet like the moon that governs every sea,
My every motion is controlled by thee.
