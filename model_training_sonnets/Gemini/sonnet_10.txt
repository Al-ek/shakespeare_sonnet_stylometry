X

The winter wind doth howl across the plain,
And strips the forest of its summer dress,
Bringing a season of a cold disdain,
To leave the earth in weary nakedness.
But in the root, the spark of life remains,
To wait the calling of the April rain,
When sap shall rise within the frozen veins,
To break the power of the winter’s chain.
So does my heart, when thou art far away,
Endure the cold of sorrow’s heavy night,
And wait the dawning of a better day,
When I shall stand within thy blessed light.
For thou art spring to all my wintry thought,
The only joy that ever could be bought.
