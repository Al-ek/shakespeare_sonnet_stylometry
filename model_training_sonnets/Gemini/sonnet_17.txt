XVII

The river flows into the boundless sea,
And loses there its name and single course,
To find at last a grander destiny,
Far from the quiet of its mountain source.
So does my life into thy spirit flow,
To lose the narrow limits of my soul,
And in the union of our loving grow,
Until the broken parts are made a whole.
No longer am I wandering alone,
A single drop upon a thirsty land,
But in thy heart I find a world my own,
Guided and guarded by thy loving hand.
The river ends, but ocean has no bound,
And in thy love, my eternity is found.
