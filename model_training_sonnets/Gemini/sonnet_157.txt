III

Thou art the music that the silence sings,
A melody that lingers in the air,
Borne up on heavy and invisible wings,
To soothe the heavy burden of despair.
When discord strikes the chords of my unrest,
And all the world is out of tune with me,
I find a quiet harbor in thy breast,
And drown my sorrows in thy harmony.
Let others seek the trumpet’s hollow sound,
Or chase the fleeting echoes of the crowd,
In thee a truer resonance is found,
Beyond the reach of voices harsh and loud.
If music be the food of love’s design,
Then let me feast upon those notes of thine.
