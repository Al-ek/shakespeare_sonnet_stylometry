LIX

If there be nothing new under the sun,
And all that is has been a thousand times,
Then is our race already nearly run,
And we are echoes of some ancient rhymes.
But I believe that in thy living face,
A new-born wonder has at last appeared,
A touch of glory and a special grace,
That even the oldest poets would have feared.
They saw the shadows, but I see the light;
They felt the winter, I have found the spring;
And through the watches of the darkest night,
I hear a melody they could not sing.
Let history boast of beauties past and gone,
Thou art the first true herald of the dawn.
