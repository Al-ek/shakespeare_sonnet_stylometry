XLIX

Against that time—if ever such a time should come—
When thou shalt look upon my faults with scorn,
And find the voice of thy affection dumb,
Leaving me wretched, naked, and forlorn;
I now prepare my soul for that dark day,
By listing every weakness that I own,
That I may have no bitter word to say,
When I am left to face the world alone.
Thou hast the right to leave this humble door,
For what can I provide to keep thee here?
My garden is a patch of weeds, no more,
Watered by nothing but a silent tear.
I'll side with thee against my own self's plea,
For thou art right to turn away from me.
