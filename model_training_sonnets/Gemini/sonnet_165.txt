XI

Look in thy heart and tell me what thou seest,
Is there a corner kept for me alone?
Of all the burdens that thou now releasest,
Am I the only one thou wilt not own?
I fear the changing seasons of thy mind,
And dread the turning of the fickle tide,
Lest in thy soul I should no longer find,
The secret place where I was wont to hide.
But if the world should prove an unkind host,
And turn its back upon thy weary head,
I’ll be the one who loves thee still the most,
When all the other fair-weather friends have fled.
For love is not a garment for a day,
But a thick cloak to keep the cold away.
