XXI

Upon the dial of my heavy heart,
The shadow creeps with silent, steady pace,
To tear the joy of present hours apart,
And steal the light from thy beloved face.
But though the iron hand of Time may reap
The golden harvest of thy youthful prime,
And sow the seeds of winter’s icy sleep,
Deep in the furrows of a weary rhyme,
Yet shall thy beauty find a second breath,
Where ink and paper defy the dusty grave,
To snatch a victory from the maw of death,
And keep the glory that no king could save.
For while men breathe and eyes have power to see,
This verse shall live, and living, honor thee.
