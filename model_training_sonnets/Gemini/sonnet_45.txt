XLV

The air and fire within my breast reside,
The lighter elements of life and breath,
That on the currents of the spirit ride,
To snatch a victory from the hand of death.
The fire is my desire, the air my thought,
And both are sent as envoys unto thee,
To tell the wonders that thy love hath wrought,
And bring a message of thy constancy.
But when these two are gone, I sink to earth,
Possessed by water and the heavy stone,
Lacking the vital spark that gives me worth,
And left to languish in the dark alone.
Until my scouts return from thy dear sight,
To bring me back the fire and the light.
