X

O, let me be the shadow at thy feet,
That follows where thy gentle footsteps go,
To share the burden of the noon-day heat,
And watch the silver waters softly flow.
I ask no place of honor at thy side,
Nor any title that the world bestows,
Content within thy presence to abide,
As one who reaps the harvest that he sows.
For in thy light I find my only day,
And in thy breath the air I need to live,
I throw the trappings of the world away,
For all the simple comfort thou canst give.
The sun may fail to rise upon the sea,
But I shall never fail in following thee.
