XI

The winter wind may strip the trees of green,
And frost may bite the tender fields of earth;
Yet in thy image, all the world is seen
A warmer place, a source of endless mirth.
Though shadows lengthen and the daylight fades,
Thy presence fills the void where darkness reigns;
And even as the sun its course invades,
Thy light remains, unbroken by these chains.
So let me carve thy virtues in my song,
A record of a beauty none can claim;
That all who read may feel the joy along,
And honor thee beyond a mortal name.
Though fleeting life may vanish like the mist,
Thy memory by verse shall still exist.
