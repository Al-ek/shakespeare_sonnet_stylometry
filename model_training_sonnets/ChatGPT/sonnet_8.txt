VIII

When storms arise upon the restless sea,
The trembling mast must bend, yet not be broken;
So too must hearts endure adversity,
And speak with courage what cannot be spoken.
Though fortune flings her harsh and cruel darts,
And fate denies the tender wish of men,
The soul, though battered, keeps its secret arts,
And rises stronger than it once had been.
O let my verse record this quiet might,
And bear the witness of enduring grace;
Though shadows fall and darken all the night,
The heart shall hold a steadfast, luminous place.
For even in despair, the soul may gleam,
A fleeting spark amid the troubled stream.
