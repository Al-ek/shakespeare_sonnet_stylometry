XXIX

When in disgrace with fortune and men’s eyes
I all alone beweep my outcast state,
And trouble heaven with my bootless cries,
And look upon myself, and curse my fate;
Yet in these depths, when hope seems wholly fled,
Thy thought arises like the break of morn,
And lifts my spirit from its heavy bed,
As if new wings within my soul were born.
For then I scorn the riches others prize,
And count myself more blessed than kings may be;
No change of state could tempt me otherwise,
So long as I possess the thought of thee.
Thus sorrow turns to joy at thy recall,
And makes me rich though I possess but all.
