LII

So am I like the keeper of a chest
Wherein a jewel of rare worth is laid;
I open it but seldom, counting best
To keep its treasure safe, though long delayed.
So do I spare the sight of thee, most dear,
Not from neglect, but from a careful love;
For constant use might make thy worth appear
Less wondrous than thy rarity should prove.
But when I choose to feast upon that sight,
It brings a joy beyond all common fare;
And makes each meeting richer in delight,
By being held so precious and so rare.
Thus absence serves to heighten what is sweet,
And gives new savor to each time we meet.
