LVII

Being thy slave, what should I do but wait
Upon the hours and times that thou dost choose?
I have no will but what attends thy state,
Nor liberty to act as I might use.
No moment is my own while thou command’st,
Nor thought but serves the purpose of thy will;
I stand obedient where thy pleasure stands,
And find my freedom in submission still.
Yet in this bond there lies no bitter chain,
For love makes service sweeter than delight;
And what seems loss is turned to hidden gain,
When all is yielded to thy gracious sight.
Thus am I bound, yet count my bondage free,
Since all I lose returns in loving thee.
