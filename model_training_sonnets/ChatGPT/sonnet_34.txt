XXXIV

Why didst thou promise me a cloudless sky,
And lead me forth without a cloak of care,
When sudden storms were gathering on high,
And rain prepared to catch me unaware?
Thy gentle words did tempt my trust too far,
And made me venture where I stood unarmed;
Now drenched in grief, I see what false hopes are,
And learn too late how fondly I was charmed.
Yet though thou caused this tempest in my breast,
Thy pity comes to dry the tears it made;
And in that grace I find a second rest,
Though first thy fault my confidence betrayed.
So wounds thou giv’st are healed by thee again,
And sorrow yields to thy restoring rain.
