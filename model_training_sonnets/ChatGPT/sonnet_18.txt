XVIII

The autumn leaves fall softly on the ground,
A fiery carpet wrought by passing time;
And in their rustling there is gentle sound,
A warning hidden in their fleeting rhyme.
For all that blooms must wither, all that thrives
Shall bend beneath the weight of unseen hand;
Yet in the turning of our mortal lives
There lies a beauty none may yet command.
Let then my pen preserve the fleeting flame,
And capture all that passes from the eye;
Though seasons change and life shall not be same,
The soul shall speak, and mortal hearts reply.
So write, O hand, though leaves fall as they must,
And hold in ink the bloom that turns to dust.
