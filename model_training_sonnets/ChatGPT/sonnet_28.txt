XXVIII

How can I find repose when day and night
Conspire to keep me captive to thy thought?
The day doth tire me with its glaring light,
The night with dreams wherein thy form is sought.
By day, thy absence makes the hours seem long,
Each minute stretched beyond its natural span;
By night, thy presence doth my rest prolong,
As sleep dissolves before love’s watchful plan.
Thus both extremes, though opposite they seem,
Do join in one to vex my weary state;
The light denies me ease, the dark a dream,
And both alike upon thy absence wait.
O cruel time, that grants no hour of ease,
But binds my soul to love’s unresting seas.
