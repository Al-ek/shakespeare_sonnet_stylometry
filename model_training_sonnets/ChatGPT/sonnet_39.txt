XXXIX

O how thy worth with mine may be compared,
When thou alone dost hold all excellence?
What praise can I give thee, unless I shared
In some small part thy shining eminence?
Yet if I stand apart, I see thee best,
And give thee honor as thy due requires;
For love made humble seeks not to be blest
With equal claim, but lifts what it admires.
Thus separation grants me clearer sight,
To view thy grace without conceited claim;
And makes my verse more honest in its right,
Since it exalts thee free from self-born aim.
So do I lose myself to praise thee more,
And gain in loss what I resigned before.
