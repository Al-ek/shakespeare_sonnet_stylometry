XLIX

Against that time, if ever it shall fall
That thou wouldst frown and cast my love aside,
When in thy judgment I seem nothing at all,
And faults long hid are suddenly espied;
Against that day I arm my patient heart,
And learn to live without thy cherished sight;
I teach my soul to bear the bitter part,
And make of loss a reasoned kind of right.
For thou wert never bound to love me still,
Nor I to claim what time may justly take;
Yet though I yield submission to thy will,
My grief shall know the measure of its ache.
Thus I prepare for what I cannot stay,
And grieve beforehand for that coming day.
