XXIV

Mine eye hath drawn thy image in my breast,
And there it hangs in living portraiture;
No painter’s art such cunning hath expressed,
Nor time can make so fair a form obscure.
Each lineament is graven in my thought,
Each gentle grace in memory doth dwell;
No thief of years can rob what love hath wrought,
Nor fortune’s spite this inward truth expel.
Yet is mine eye both artist and the frame,
For through its sight thy beauty first I knew;
And in that act it gained immortal fame,
To house a heaven that ever seemeth new.
Thus art thou fixed where change can never be,
In heart’s deep gallery, reserved for thee.
