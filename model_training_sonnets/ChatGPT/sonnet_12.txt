XII

The river winds beneath the mountain’s shade,
Its waters singing softly to the stone;
And I, observing, see how life is made
Of currents hidden, yet in motion known.
Though men may boast of deeds in public square,
The quiet acts of kindness bear the weight;
The gentle heart, though small and unaware,
May shape the world beyond the reach of fate.
So let my verse like river winding flow,
And carry all that lies within the soul;
For in the subtle currents men may know
The greater truth, beyond their fleeting goal.
And though the banks may crumble to the tide,
The song of waters in the heart shall bide.
