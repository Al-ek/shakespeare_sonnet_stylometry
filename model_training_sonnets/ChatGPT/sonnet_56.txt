LVI

Sweet love, renew thy strength; be not so dull
That appetite should languish in excess;
For surfeit dulls the edge that once was full,
And makes abundance seem like nothingness.
Let absence be the pause that sharpens taste,
And longing keep desire in constant flame;
For too much ease doth make affection waste,
And dims the brightness of its eager aim.
As tides retreat to gather greater force,
So let thy ebb prepare a stronger flow;
And make thy hunger take a fuller course,
That joy may rise where once it seemed to go.
Thus love, though stretched, shall never wholly break,
But grow more keen for every pause it take.
