IX

When autumn leaves fall softly on the ground,
And whisper tales of summers past and gone,
My thoughts of thee make every silence sound,
And turn the fleeting moment into song.
For though the seasons change, thy beauty stays,
A constant star amid the shifting sky;
And in the night’s reflective, tender gaze,
I see the light that will not fade nor die.
So let me write thee in each measured line,
A tribute made of syllables and care;
That all who read may feel the glow divine,
And know that love like thine is rich and rare.
Though time may try to claim the earth and sea,
Thy worth shall live in immortality.
