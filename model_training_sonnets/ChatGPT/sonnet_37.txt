XXXVII

As a poor sire, made glad by thriving child,
Takes pride in strength he knows he cannot claim,
So I, though age and weakness render mild,
Rejoice to see thy youth’s advancing flame.
Thy beauty is the triumph of my sight,
Thy grace the echo of my fond desire;
And though I fade beneath time’s withering might,
Thou stand’st renewed, a monument of fire.
In thee I live what I have left behind,
And find new worth in what I cannot be;
For all my loss is balanced in my mind
By what is gained in gazing upon thee.
Thus do I thrive by thy increasing worth,
And take new life from thy unfolding birth.
