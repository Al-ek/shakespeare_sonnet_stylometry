X

The dawn appears, yet in its early glow
I find no beauty like the thought of thee;
The skies may blush, the morning winds may blow,
Yet none outshine the vision my heart sees.
Each word I write, though humble, bears thy name,
Each line a testament to love’s pure fire;
And though the world may scorn, or hearts may blame,
It cannot dim my soul’s most deep desire.
O let my verse, though small, attain the height
Of grandeur found within thy gentle ways;
And let my pen, by day and quiet night,
Preserve the marvel of thy endless days.
Thus shall my spirit, bound in ink and thought,
Immortalize the treasure love hath wrought.
