LX

Like as the waves upon the pebbled shore
Do chase each other in a ceaseless race,
So do our minutes hasten evermore,
Each taking up the other’s fleeting place.
No moment stands, but yields unto the next,
And all move forward in time’s swift decay;
The present fades, the future stands perplexed,
Till both are swallowed in the past away.
Yet thou, though subject to this mortal flow,
Shalt find in verse a harbor from such tide;
For words can fix what time would overthrow,
And give a second life where forms have died.
Thus though the waves consume all things they find,
Thou shalt endure within the poet’s mind.
