V

The ocean’s tide doth rise and break at will,
Yet none may measure what its depths contain;
So too the heart, untrammeled, dark and still,
Holds love and sorrow in a boundless reign.
Though men may chart the stars in careful line,
And map the heavens with their learned hand,
The secret currents of the soul divine
Shall flow where none may grasp, nor understand.
O restless mind, attend to subtle art,
And let thy verse be mirror of the sea;
Reflect the passions beating in the heart,
The hidden depth, the storm, and tranquil lea.
In writing thus, the soul achieves its flight,
Beyond the reach of mortal sense or sight.
