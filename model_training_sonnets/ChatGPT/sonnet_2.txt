II

The silver moon doth hang in solemn sky,
Its beams like whispers on the slumb’ring stream;
And I, alone, with tender, watchful eye,
Do chase the phantoms of a weary dream.
Though men may boast of gold and earthly power,
The heart’s true wealth lies not in mortal gain;
’Tis found within the silent, tender hour,
Where love doth bloom untouched by grief or pain.
O teach me, night, thy patient, endless grace,
That I may see the beauty in decay;
For time doth strike all faces, every face,
And none may from his harsh commandment stray.
Yet in these lines, a soul may find delight,
A star unfallen in the arms of night.
