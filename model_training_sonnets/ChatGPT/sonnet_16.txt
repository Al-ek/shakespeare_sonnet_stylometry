XVI

The falcon soars above the jagged cliff,
Its wings outstretched against the vault of sky;
And I, in thought, perceive the secret whiff
Of freedom found where none may question why.
Though men are bound by duty, law, and time,
The spirit claims its sovereign, quiet reign;
And those who dare may rise in silent rhyme,
And find their kingdom far from mortal chain.
O let my verse ascend as falcon’s flight,
And pierce the clouds that shade the mortal view;
For in each line there lies a quiet might,
A place where thought may wander, pure and true.
And though the earth may press with heavy hand,
The soul shall reign where words alone command.
