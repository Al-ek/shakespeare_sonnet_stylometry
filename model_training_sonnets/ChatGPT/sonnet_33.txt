XXXIII

Full many a glorious morning have I seen
Flatter the hills with beams of golden light,
Kiss silent streams and crown the meadows green,
And gild the world with majesty of sight;
Yet oft the clouds arise to dim that grace,
And hide the sun that seemed so bright before;
So hath thy favor shone upon my face,
Then veiled itself and left me wanting more.
No blame I lay upon thy changing will,
For nature’s course doth shift from fair to foul;
Yet do I feel the absence lingering still,
That clouds the brightness once within my soul.
Thus like the sun thou giv’st and tak’st away,
And make’st my night from what was once my day.
