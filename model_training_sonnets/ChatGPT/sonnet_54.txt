LIV

O how much fairer seems the truth of grace
When inward virtue doth adorn the show;
For beauty’s outward form may lose its place,
If no true worth beneath its bloom doth grow.
The rose, though sweet, is prized for scent as well,
Not only for the charm its petals bear;
But weeds, though like in outward hue may dwell,
Lack that perfume which makes the rose so rare.
So thou, whose beauty outwardly delights,
Art doubly fair if truth within thee lies;
For then thy worth exceeds mere mortal sights,
And lives beyond what fleeting form denies.
Thus virtue gives to beauty lasting breath,
And guards it safe against the touch of death.
