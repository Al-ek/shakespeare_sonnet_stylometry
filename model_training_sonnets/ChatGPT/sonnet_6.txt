VI

When youth doth paint the cheek with ruddy grace,
And laughter dances lightly in the eye,
Yet time doth chase the mirror of the face,
And beauty fades as clouds obscure the sky.
The mind, however, may retain its bloom,
In letters, thought, and memory enshrined;
No frost of age, nor death, nor lover’s gloom
Shall rob the treasure written by the mind.
So wield the pen as sword against decay,
And let each line defy the passing years;
Though mortal flesh may perish and give way,
The heart survives, immortal through its tears.
Then write, O soul, though shadows cross thy path,
And in thy verse, outlast the tempest’s wrath.
