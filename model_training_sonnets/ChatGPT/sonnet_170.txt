XVI

O fleeting youth, that dances on the wind,
And leaves behind the shadowed trace of age,
Remembered not in vain, nor merely pinned
To pages pale, but treasured as a sage.
For in thy likeness, beauty takes its stand,
And makes the mortal world seem less severe;
No sculptor’s hand, nor painter’s careful hand,
Could match the vision that my soul holds dear.
Thus shall my verse, though modest, do its part,
To guard thy grace from time’s relentless tide;
A lasting echo of a gentle heart,
A place where mortal care cannot abide.
Then let these words, though simple, testify
To thee, whose light no darkness can belie.
