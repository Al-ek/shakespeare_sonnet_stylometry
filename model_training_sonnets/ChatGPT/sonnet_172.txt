XVIII

When autumn winds toss leaves along the lane,
And evening falls with quiet, gentle grace,
I think of thee, and in that thought remain,
A steadfast heart, unmoved by fleeting pace.
Though all the world may shift and alter still,
The truth of thee abides beyond control;
No trial nor fear, nor fortune’s fiercest will,
Can mar the wonder rooted in the soul.
Then let this verse, though small and brief in frame,
Preserve thy image for the future days;
A lasting token of a constant flame,
A tribute wrought in softly measured lays.
Thus shall my pen, though humble, serve its part,
To bind thy glory ever to my heart.
