XIII

The waves may break against the stubborn shore,
And winds may howl their ceaseless, bitter cry;
Yet steadfast as the ocean’s ancient roar
Remains the love that lifts my spirit high.
Though tempests shake the fragile bark of man,
The anchor of thy presence holds it fast;
No trial, nor fear, nor treacherous plan
Can tear the bonds that time has never passed.
So let my verse proclaim thy gentle might,
A shield that guards the memory of thee;
And let its words, like lighthouses of light,
Guide all who wander on life’s stormy sea.
Though mortal years may fade, the truth remains:
Love’s steadfast course outlasts all earthly chains.
