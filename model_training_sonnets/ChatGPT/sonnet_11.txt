XI

O winter’s frost, that bites the tender rose,
And strips the orchard of its crimson fruit,
Yet in thy chill a quiet beauty shows,
A lesson hidden in thy rigid root.
So too the heart, when wounded by despair,
May grow in secret, stronger than before;
The bitter winds may pierce the stagnant air,
But courage blooms from sorrow’s secret store.
Let not the fleeting warmth of spring deceive,
Nor summer’s golden hand allure the mind;
For virtue shines when winter bids us grieve,
And truth is tempered by the harshest kind.
Then write, O heart, when frost surrounds thy way,
And find in ice the promise of the day.
