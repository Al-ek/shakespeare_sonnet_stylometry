XIX

The moon ascends upon the velvet night,
And bathes the earth in silvered, gentle gleam;
Yet pales its light before thy radiant sight,
A brilliance born of every cherished dream.
Though time may pass, and mortal hours decay,
The essence of thy being shall remain;
A steady lamp to guide both night and day,
A solace through both sorrow and through pain.
So let my verse, in faithful cadence, stand,
A marker of the beauty thou impart;
No age, nor fate, nor ever-changing land,
Shall shake the glory rooted in thy heart.
Though fleeting life may vanish with the sun,
Thy memory shall dwell when all is done.
