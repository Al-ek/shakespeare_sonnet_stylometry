IV

O gentle night, that drapes the world in black,
And veils the stars in shadows soft and still,
Let not my thoughts of thee one moment lack,
Nor let my yearning heart thy absence fill.
The moon, in silver majesty, may shine,
Yet pales before the radiance of thy eyes;
The heavens bend to mark a love divine,
That neither fate nor mortal art denies.
And though my pen may tremble as I write,
Its trembling serves to honor what is true;
For only in the hand of careful light
Can shadows turn to something born anew.
So let this night, though dark, reflect thy grace,
And find its form within my verse’s space.
