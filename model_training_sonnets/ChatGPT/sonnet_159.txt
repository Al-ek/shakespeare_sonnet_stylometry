V

When laughter rings along the summer’s field,
And echoes dance through every gilded tree,
I think on thee, whose presence doth not yield
To fleeting joy, but stands eternally.
The river flows, and yet it cannot wash
The image of thy love from mortal mind;
No tempest fierce, no thunder’s sudden crash,
Can make my memory of thee less kind.
O teach me, heart, to keep such treasure safe,
To honor it with care beyond the hours;
And in my verse to carve thy perfect shape,
A lasting monument of mortal powers.
Then shall the world, through verse, remember thee,
Though all else fades as shadow from the sea.
