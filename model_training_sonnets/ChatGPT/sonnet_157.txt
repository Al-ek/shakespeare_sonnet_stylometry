III

The mirror shows a visage worn with care,
Yet in that face the soul’s bright glimmer shines;
Though age shall strip the cheek of youthful air,
It cannot steal the light that love defines.
For beauty rooted in the mind’s delight
Outlasts the fleeting bloom of outward hue;
And hearts that feel the tenderest of sight
Know joys that time nor sorrow can subdue.
O let me write thy glory in my rhyme,
That men yet unborn may gaze and find
The living essence captured out of time,
A treasure stored within immortal mind.
So shall my verse, though humble in its frame,
Preserve the wealth of thine eternal name.
