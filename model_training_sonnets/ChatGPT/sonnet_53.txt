LIII

What substance is in thee that shadows form
In every shape where beauty finds a place?
Each graceful thing, though varied in its norm,
Seems but a partial echo of thy grace.
In flowers, I see the pattern of thy bloom;
In skies, the brightness of thy shining eye;
In all fair forms that nature doth assume,
Some hint of thee doth secretly comply.
Yet none can fully match thy perfect whole,
For each reflects but fragments of thy art;
Thou art the sum that satisfies the soul,
Whilst others serve as copies set apart.
Thus art thou all that beauty can express,
The single source of manifold excess.
