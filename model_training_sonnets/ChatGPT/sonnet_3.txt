III

When I behold the morning’s early hue,
And hear the birds proclaim the waking day,
I think how swiftly all things bid adieu,
And none may halt the sun’s relentless way.
The rose that blossoms in the garden fair
Shall lose its charm beneath the summer heat;
Yet in my verse, its beauty lingers there,
A tender record none may see defeat.
So let the world pursue its fleeting strife,
Its glory, sorrow, triumph, and disdain;
I write a legacy of tender life,
Unmoved by time’s relentless, cruel chain.
Though all the world may crumble and decay,
My words, like seed, shall bloom beyond its day.
