IV

O gentle winds that wander through the glade,
Bear whispers of the hearts long left behind;
The silent trees in shadowed order laid
Do guard the secrets of a mortal mind.
Though kings may sit upon their gilded thrones,
And laws enforce the will of fleeting men,
The soul’s true power is as yet unknown,
It whispers softly through the darkened glen.
Attend, my pen, and capture fleeting truth,
Before the hour of reckoning doth come;
For youth is but a vanishing, sweet sooth,
And death’s cold breath shall beat a quiet drum.
Then let my words endure where flesh shall fall,
A steadfast tower, though kingdoms heed its call.
