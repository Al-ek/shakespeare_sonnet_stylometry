XLIII

When most I close mine eyes, then best I see,
For all the day they view but empty show;
But in the night thy image comes to me,
And makes the dark with living splendor glow.
Then shadows turn to substance at thy grace,
And dreams present what daylight cannot give;
For in that inward sight I see thy face,
And in that vision more than waking live.
O blessed sleep, that grants such gentle art,
To bring thee near when thou art far away;
And paint thee perfect in my longing heart,
Where truth outshines the errors of the day.
Thus night excels the day in love’s delight,
Since thou art absent then, yet present sight.
