XLVI

Mine eye and heart are at a mortal war
For conquest of thy image rich and rare;
Mine eye would claim it as a prize from far,
My heart insists it holds thee hidden there.
The eye doth plead that sight first gave thee place,
And therefore it deserves thy outward show;
The heart replies that love, not fleeting grace,
Hath made thy dwelling in its depths to grow.
To end this strife, a truce at last is sworn:
Thy outward form the eye may still possess,
Whilst inward love, by deeper truth adorned,
Shall in the heart thy secret self express.
Thus both are pleased, though neither wholly reign,
And peace is made where once was jealous pain.
