XLVII

Betwixt mine eye and heart a league is made,
That each shall share what either doth obtain;
When one is starved, the other sends it aid,
And so their mutual comfort doth remain.
If eye be filled with sight of thee so fair,
The heart rejoices in that borrowed feast;
If heart in thought thy absent form repair,
The eye is pleased though outward joys have ceased.
Thus each to other lends a kindly hand,
And makes thy presence felt though thou be gone;
No want is known while both in concord stand,
But feed on thee, divided yet as one.
So thrive I still, though parted from thy grace,
By inward sight that fills thy outward place.
