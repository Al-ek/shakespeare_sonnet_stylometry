XLII

That thou hast won another’s heart, I know,
And she, perhaps, doth cherish thee as dear;
Yet in that loss strange comfort still doth grow,
Since what I loved in both is joined there.
If thou dost love her, then thou lov’st my part,
For she was mine ere thou didst claim her sight;
And if she love thee, she hath still my heart,
Though turned now to another’s sweet delight.
Thus I in both do find a doubled gain,
Though seeming loss doth outwardly appear;
For what I gave returns to me again,
In love that binds you both, and holds me near.
So am I wronged, yet strangely satisfied,
For love divides, yet keeps me still allied.
