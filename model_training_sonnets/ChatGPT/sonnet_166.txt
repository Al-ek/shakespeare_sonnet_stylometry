XII

O gentle thought, that lingers in my mind,
And wanders through the corridors of night,
Thou art the solace that my heart doth find,
A steady lamp against the shadowed fright.
Though men may seek to conquer and erase,
The truth of thee abides beyond their reach;
No law, nor force, nor time may thus deface
The lesson that thy living virtues teach.
And so my pen, obedient to thy flame,
Records the wonder of thy constant grace;
A faithful servant of an endless name,
A testament no mortal hand can place.
Then let this verse endure when I am gone,
A marker of thy life, and of my own.
