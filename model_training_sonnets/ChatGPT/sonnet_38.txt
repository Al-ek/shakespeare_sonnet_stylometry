XXXVIII

How can my verse be barren of fresh praise
When thou dost give the matter and the art?
Thy presence feeds the fountain of my lays,
And pours invention freely from my heart.
Thou art the muse that guides my humble pen,
The source from which all worthy thoughts proceed;
And what I write, though shaped by mortal ken,
Is but thy beauty clothed in verbal seed.
Then if my lines seem rich in any part,
The praise is thine, not mine who only frame;
For thou dost lend both subject and the start,
And give to verse its substance and its name.
Thus I but serve to show what thou hast wrought,
A mirror faint of thy unbounded thought.
