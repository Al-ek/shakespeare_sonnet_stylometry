XX

The evening star doth tremble in the sky,
Its light a jewel set in violet deep;
And I, reflecting, find a gentle sigh,
A thought that stirs the heart from quiet sleep.
Though night may press upon the weary eye,
And clouds obscure the firmament’s embrace,
Yet in this glow, the soul doth not deny
The tender hand of beauty and of grace.
O let my verse, like star, endure the night,
And shine where mortal gaze may scarce perceive;
For words, though small, contain a lasting light,
A spark of life that time can not bereave.
And in this flame, the heart shall find its way,
To guide it safely through the coming day.
