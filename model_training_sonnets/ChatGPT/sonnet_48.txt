XLVIII

How careful was I, when I first did part,
To lock away each trifle I held dear,
Lest careless loss might wound my guarded heart,
Or time should make what once was precious, mere.
Yet thee, most rich, I left unguarded quite,
Exposed to every thief of wandering eye;
No key I found to keep thy beauty tight,
Nor bars could hold what nature made so free.
For thou art treasure that cannot be bound,
Whose worth exceeds all custody of mine;
And though I seek thee, thou art ever found
Where others gaze upon thy form divine.
Thus must I trust what cannot be confined,
And guard thee only in my faithful mind.
