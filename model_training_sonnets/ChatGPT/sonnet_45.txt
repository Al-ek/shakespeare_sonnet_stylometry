XLV

The elements that build this mortal frame,
Of earth and water form the heavier part;
But fire and air, more subtle in their aim,
Do make the motion of my loving heart.
These lighter parts do travel where thou art,
And keep me living by their quick return;
But earth and water linger still apart,
And with their absence make me sigh and yearn.
Thus when thy presence feeds my inward fire,
I feel a balance in my parted state;
But when thou’rt gone, both air and flame retire,
And leave me dull, oppressed, and desolate.
Till they return, I live in sad decay,
Half of myself removed and far away.
