XXXII

If thou survive me when my breath is spent,
And read these lines when I am turned to dust,
Think not my verse with later age content,
Nor judge it harsh by art more fine and just.
For in my time, such words were all I knew,
And love gave strength where skill was yet untried;
If others write with sweeter phrase than true,
Yet mine were born with thee their only guide.
O, then compare me not with pens more rare,
But read with kindness what I meant to show;
For had I lived to see thee still so fair,
My verse had grown as rich as love could grow.
Thus let my lines, though humble, find thy grace,
As shadows faint that yet reflect thy face.
