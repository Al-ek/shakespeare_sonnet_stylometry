VII

The lamp that burns within the scholar’s room
Doth cast its feeble light upon the page;
Yet in that glow there lies a quiet bloom,
A whispered challenge to the hand of age.
For wisdom is a fire that none can quench,
Though all the world may frown upon its flame;
And those who seek it in the silent trench
Shall find a life that bears no fleeting name.
O let my thought, however small and weak,
Take wing and soar above the common throng;
For truth, however humble, dares to speak,
And echoes loudly in the poet’s song.
Though time may try to drown the voice of men,
Their words shall rise and breathe again and then.
