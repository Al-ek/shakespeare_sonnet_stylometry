XVII

When shadows stretch across the garden wall,
And evening whispers of the dying day,
The fleeting light reminds us all of thrall,
And bids the heart consider its own way.
Though youth may tempt with pleasures unrefined,
And fleeting passions stir the restless breast,
Yet wisdom lies in quiet, patient mind,
And teaches which pursuits deserve our rest.
O gentle verse, attend to mortal care,
And hold the fleeting moments in thy gaze;
For those who write may find their solace there,
A sanctuary through the troubled maze.
And when the night descends with quiet power,
The written word shall bloom like evening flower.
