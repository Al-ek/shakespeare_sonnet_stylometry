XXXV

No fault is pure, nor free from mixture found,
For even virtue leans to some excess;
The sweetest rose doth harbor thorny ground,
And beauty walks with some unworthiness.
Thus I excuse what reason might condemn,
And find in fault a shadow of thy grace;
For if perfection were denied to them,
How could I hope to see it in thy face?
Yet in this pardon lies my deeper grief,
That love must plead where judgment would accuse;
For though I grant thy error some relief,
My heart still feels the wrong it would refuse.
Thus stand I torn between what love allows,
And what my wounded truth no more avows.
