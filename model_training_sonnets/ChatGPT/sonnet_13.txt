XIII

When evening falls and paints the sky with fire,
The fleeting colors whisper of decay;
Yet in that glow there lies a strange desire,
To hold the moment, though it slips away.
For life is but a shadow, swift and fleet,
And joy a candle in the gusting wind;
Yet verse endures where mortal hearts may meet,
A quiet harbor where the soul is pinned.
O gentle hand, let not this hour escape,
But fix its image in enduring line;
Though sun shall set, and stars their watch reshape,
The beauty caught in writing shall be thine.
Then read, and see how mortal life may gleam,
Preserved within the ever-constant dream.
