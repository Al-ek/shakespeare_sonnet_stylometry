XIV

When night’s soft curtain hides the world from view,
And silence reigns upon the sleeping land,
My thoughts of thee shine clearer, pure and true,
A flame no shadow can make less or grand.
Though all around may crumble into dust,
The heart preserves what time cannot destroy;
And in this truth, my mind places its trust,
A witness to unyielding, quiet joy.
Then let this sonnet hold thy image near,
A fragile glass where lasting beauty dwells;
And all who read may feel thy essence clear,
As though thy voice in tender cadence swells.
Thus shall my words, in faithful measure, bind
The light of thee within the mortal mind.
