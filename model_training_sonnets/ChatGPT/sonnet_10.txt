X

The sparrow flits across the trembling air,
Its shadow dancing lightly on the ground;
And I, observing, find a secret care
That beauty lies in motion, not in bound.
For stillness steals the grace of fleeting things,
And binds the spirit in a narrow cage;
But life is caught upon unending wings,
And every moment marks a turning page.
Let then my verse like wings extend its reach,
And lift the mind above the common throng;
For words may soar where mortal feet beseech,
And teach the heart to recognize its song.
Though time may seize the fleeting, fragile frame,
The thought survives, enduring in its name.
