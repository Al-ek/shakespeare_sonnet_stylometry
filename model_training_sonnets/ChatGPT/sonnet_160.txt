VI.

The fleeting hours, like birds, take wing and flee,
Yet thou remain’st, a constant in the storm;
The eye may wander, but the heart shall see
That in thy light all darkness takes new form.
Each moment spent in thought of thee is gold,
A wealth that none may steal or counterfeit;
And age, though cruel, shall never make thee old,
For in my verse thy virtues shall be writ.
So let me bind thee in immortal rhyme,
And place thy glory where no time can reach;
A jewel set beyond the grasp of time,
Beyond the teaching any tongue can teach.
Thus shall my soul, though mortal in its frame,
Preserve thy being in undying flame.
