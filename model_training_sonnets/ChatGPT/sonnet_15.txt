XV

O gentle rain, that falls upon the plain,
And wakes the flowers from their silent sleep;
Thy tender drops, though mournful in their strain,
Do wash the heart, and make the soul more deep.
For sorrow, though it strikes the mortal frame,
Shall teach the mind to see beyond its grief;
And in its shadow burns a subtle flame,
A hidden balm, a small but certain relief.
Let then my verse, like rain, descend and pour,
And cleanse the mind of all its earthly stain;
For words, like drops, may nourish evermore,
And bring a harvest from the field of pain.
So write, O hand, and let thy labor fall,
A gentle mercy, tender to us all.
