I

Upon the morning’s gentle, golden rise,
The dew doth cling to tender blade and leaf;
Yet fleeting light doth fade in mortal eyes,
And joy, like shade, departs with sudden grief.
The heart that holds ambition’s soaring flame
Finds oft the chill of doubt in silent night;
Yet love, though fickle, still doth stake its claim,
And turns the darkest sorrow into light.
So let me write what time cannot erase,
A verse to mock the fleeting grasp of breath;
Though all the world shall vanish from its place,
These lines shall linger, conquering even death.
O fleeting world, thy beauty is my scorn,
For only in thy loss is art reborn.
