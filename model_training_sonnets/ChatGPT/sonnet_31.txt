XXXI

Thy bosom is the treasure-house of all
The loves and friendships I have held most dear;
Each absent grace obeys thy gentle call,
And lives again, revived and gathered here.
Those whom I thought were lost to time’s decay
Do in thy presence seem restored to sight;
For in thy worth their virtues find a stay,
And shine anew within thy living light.
Thou art the grave where buried loves arise,
The shrine where memory keeps her sacred part;
And what was scattered far from mortal eyes
Is brought to rest within thy faithful heart.
Thus all I lost in thee is rendered whole,
And thou contain’st the riches of my soul.
