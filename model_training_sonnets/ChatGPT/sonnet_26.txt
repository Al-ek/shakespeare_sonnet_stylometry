XXVI

Lord of my thoughts, to whom in vassalage
My spirit yields obedience full and true,
I send these lines as duty’s humble page,
To show the boundless service I do you.
No worth have they, but what thy grace imparts,
For in thy favor lies their only pride;
And as thy eye gives value to my arts,
So do I stand or fall as thou decide.
O, if thy glance but deign to read them o’er,
They shall be graced beyond their poor desert;
But if thou turn away and seek no more,
They die, unblessed, like hopes within my heart.
Thus wait I still upon thy gentle will,
To raise me high, or else to cast me still.
