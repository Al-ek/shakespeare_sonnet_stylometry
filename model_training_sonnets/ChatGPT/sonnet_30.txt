XXX

When to the sessions of sweet silent thought
I summon up remembrance of things past,
I sigh the lack of many a thing I sought,
And grieve anew old woes too long since cast.
Yet in this court of memory’s review,
Where losses plead and sorrows take their part,
There comes a witness, ever fair and true,
Whose presence doth absolve my burdened heart.
For thinking on thy love, all debts are paid,
And griefs are balanced by thy gentle grace;
The darkest clouds by thy bright beams are stayed,
And leave no shadow in their former place.
Thus memory, though rich in pain before,
Finds peace in thee and grieves for naught once more.
