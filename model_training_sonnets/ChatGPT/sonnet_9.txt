IX

The garden lies in morning’s tender light,
Its blossoms shivering in the gentle breeze;
Yet I, in thought, perceive the silent night
Where time doth strip the branches of their ease.
The sun shall rise and set, as it hath done,
And men shall chase the pleasures of the day;
Yet words remain, enduring as the sun,
To guide the wandering heart along its way.
O gentle muse, let memory’s touch be keen,
And paint the fleeting hour with lasting art;
For all that fades in mortal sight and scene
May live immortal in the written heart.
Then shall the hand that pens resist decay,
And turn the night of loss to golden day.
