LV

Not marble, nor the gilded works of kings,
Shall outlive this poor verse that holds thy name;
For monuments decay with passing things,
But words, once fixed, may still preserve thy fame.
The wars of time shall topple towers of pride,
And storms shall wear the hardest stones away;
Yet in these lines thy memory shall abide,
Unshaken by the ruin of the day.
When all that breathes is subject unto change,
And nothing stands secure from age’s hand,
My verse shall travel where all else seems strange,
And make thee known in every distant land.
So long as men can read or hearts can feel,
Thy life in these enduring lines shall dwell.
