VII

The rose doth fade, the lily droops in sun,
Yet beauty rooted in the mind doth stay;
Thy grace, unmarked by mortal hours begun,
Shall shine when earth itself has passed away.
For all that lives must bow to time’s decree,
Yet love, unmeasured, knows no finite end;
Its light survives where eyes can never see,
Its voice eternal, neither bend nor bend.
And I, a poet, labor at this task,
To hold thy image in the sculpted word;
Though humble be my verse, yet none may ask
For truer witness than my faithful sword.
O let this labor, simple though it be,
Proclaim thy life for all eternity.
