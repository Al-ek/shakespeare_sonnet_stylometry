XXII

The glass that shows me age I cast aside,
For in thy youth my better self I see;
My wrinkled hours in thy fair morn abide,
And time grows young in gazing upon thee.
If thou but thrive, I flourish in thy spring,
Though winter lodge upon my outward frame;
Thy budding days new sap of life do bring,
And warm the ashes of my fading flame.
But should thy beauty fall to nature’s due,
And yield its glory to devouring night,
My borrowed youth would perish then with you,
And leave me bare to age’s cruel spite.
Thus am I bound, thy fortune is mine own,
In thee I live, or else I die alone.
