XX

O gentle thought, that wanders through the mind,
And lingers where the soul is wont to dwell,
Thy presence leaves all fear and doubt behind,
A silent strength that none may break or quell.
Though mortal days may fade like fleeting mist,
And time may steal the bloom of youthful years,
The heart retains what cannot be dismissed,
A treasure rich beyond the reach of fears.
Thus shall my verse enshrine thy lasting grace,
A testament that outlives every breath;
No shadow may deface thy gentle face,
Nor silence steal the music from thy death.
Forever in these lines thou shalt remain,
A light undimmed, untouched by earthly pain.
