LVIII

That god who made me first thy willing thrall
Forbade me question how thou spend’st thy time;
Whether with others thou dost feast or call,
Or wander where new pleasures rise in prime.
It is enough that I remain apart,
Content to serve though absent from thy grace;
And though suspicion trouble oft my heart,
I dare not challenge what I must embrace.
For love that questions risks its own undoing,
And doubt may break what trust alone sustains;
Thus do I suffer all thy careless doing,
And turn my grief to patience in its pains.
So wait I still, though wronged I may appear,
And make thy will the law I hold most dear.
