XXXVI

Let me confess that we are two apart,
Though bound by love that makes us seem as one;
For difference stands between thy state and heart,
And bids us hide what we have freely done.
Thy name must walk in honor’s open view,
While mine remains in shadowed paths of thought;
And though my love is no less strong or true,
It must be veiled, lest scandal come of aught.
So do I lose the glory of thy praise,
And cannot boast the treasure that I hold;
Yet in that loss, love finds more secret ways
To cherish what no outward speech has told.
Thus we are twain in show, though one in truth,
Divided by the world, yet joined in youth.
