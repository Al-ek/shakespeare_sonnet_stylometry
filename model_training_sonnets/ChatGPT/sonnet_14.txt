XIV

The lark ascends upon the morning air,
Its song a herald of the waking earth;
And I, inspired, cast off all dull despair,
To measure fleeting pleasure’s tender worth.
Though grief may come as shadow in the heart,
And darken every bright and shining day,
Yet love and hope may never wholly part,
But guide the spirit through its thorny way.
Let not the fleeting trials dim the mind,
Nor earthly cares obscure the noble sight;
For those who seek, a steadfast truth shall find,
A beacon glowing in the endless night.
And though the dawn may fade, the song remains,
Unbroken by the hand of earthly pains.
