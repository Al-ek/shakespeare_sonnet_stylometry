XXVII

Wearied with toil, I haste me to my bed,
The dear repose for limbs with labor spent;
Yet there begins a journey in my head,
When sleep denies the rest it should present.
For then my thoughts do travel far to thee,
And keep my waking spirit still in play;
My body lies in shadow quietly,
Whilst mind pursues the image of thy day.
Thus night, that should bring comfort to my frame,
Becomes a second stage for love’s unrest;
And darkness only feeds the inward flame,
That robs both night and day of equal rest.
O, when shall peace these wandering thoughts reclaim,
And grant me sleep untroubled by thy name?
