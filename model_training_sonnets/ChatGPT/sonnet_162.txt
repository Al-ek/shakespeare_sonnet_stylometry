VIII

O cruel fate, that takes what heart holds dear,
And leaves the soul to wander in despair;
Yet even in the shadow of my fear,
Thy love shines through, unbroken and aware.
Though time may wring the body to decay,
The memory of thee remains intact;
No night can quench the brilliance of thy day,
Nor shadow cloud what light cannot subtract.
So let my verse, a fragile testament,
Stand firm against the tide of passing years;
A pledge of faith, and love that never went,
A witness strong through all our mortal fears.
Though life is brief, thy essence shall endure,
And in my words, forever be secure.
