XLIV

If the dull substance of my flesh were thought,
I could with ease o’erleap the widest space;
No distance then would hold thee long unsought,
But I would fly to thee in swift embrace.
Yet being bound to earth by mortal frame,
I must endure the lag of heavy clay,
Whilst nimble thought, unchained by time or aim,
Doth reach thee in a moment far away.
Thus two in me do wage unequal strife:
The mind that soars, the body that must stay;
The one enjoys the freedom of thy life,
The other grieves the slowness of delay.
O that my form could match my spirit’s flight,
And join thee ever in thy constant sight.
