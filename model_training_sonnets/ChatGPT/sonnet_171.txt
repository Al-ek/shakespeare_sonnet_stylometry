XVII

The summer sun may scorch the trembling earth,
And skies may darken with the storm’s demand;
Yet in thy presence blooms perpetual mirth,
A constant spring no heat can overstand.
Though fleeting shadows cross the heart of man,
Thy light prevails, a beacon clear and bright;
And every hour I treasure all I can,
For none may match the joy of thy delight.
So let my sonnet, humble though it be,
Reflect thy virtues in their purest form;
A faithful witness of what I can see,
And guard against the ever-changing storm.
Though time may come to dim the mortal eye,
Thy memory shall never fade nor die.
