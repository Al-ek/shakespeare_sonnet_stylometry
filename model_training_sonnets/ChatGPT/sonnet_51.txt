LI

Then can no pace, nor beast, nor cunning art,
Outstrip the swiftness of my loving mind;
For thought leaps forth and leaves the rest apart,
No weight of flesh its eager course can bind.
In instant flight it reaches where thou stay’st,
And feeds upon the sweetness of thy grace;
Whilst I, left lagging, measure out the waste
Of time and space that keeps me from thy face.
Yet though my thought arrives before my frame,
It cannot bring me fully to thy side;
For half of me still lingers in its claim,
And makes that swiftest journey incomplete.
Thus speed and slowness in one breast contend,
Till both in thee their parted powers do end.
