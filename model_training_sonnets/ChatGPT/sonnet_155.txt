I

Upon the morning’s veil, the sun doth rise,
And gilds the dew upon the trembling grass;
Yet still thine eyes outshine the brightest skies,
As fleeting hours through fleeting moments pass.
The heart, untrained, doth wander and doth stray,
Yet thine own presence holds it firm and fast;
No storm nor shadow can thy light betray,
Nor let the fleeting hours of life outlast.
O gentle muse, that moves within thy grace,
Teach me to frame my soul in subtle art;
That I may wear thy memory in place
Of hollow praise, or idle, shallow heart.
Then let my verse, though humble, thus commend,
A love unending, neither start nor end.
