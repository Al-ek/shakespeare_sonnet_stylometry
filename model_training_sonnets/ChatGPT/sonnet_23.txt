XXIII

As one unskilled upon a crowded stage,
Whose fear forgets the lines he should impart,
So I, oppressed by love’s too weighty rage,
Stand dumb, though meaning overfills my heart.
My tongue, that should thy excellence declare,
Grows faint and falters in thy noble sight;
Whilst inward thoughts, more rich than words can bear,
Do press for utterance yet evade the light.
O, let my silent looks thy grace unfold,
And read the passion writ within mine eyes;
For speech is poor where feeling grows too bold,
And truth in quiet more than noise doth rise.
Then judge my love not by my faltering breath,
But by the strength that silence witnesseth.
