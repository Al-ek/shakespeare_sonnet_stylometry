L

How heavy do I journey on my way,
When what I seek lies distant from my tread;
Each step seems slower than the last delay,
And longing weighs like stone within my head.
The horse I ride, though strong in other need,
Moves dull, as if it shared my inward care;
As though it felt my heart’s reluctant speed,
And pitied that I go, yet not to where.
No spur can quicken what my soul denies,
Nor force can drive the will that would remain;
For where thou art, there only pleasure lies,
And all beside is but a path of pain.
Thus forward move I, backward still in thought,
Divided by the distance I have wrought.
