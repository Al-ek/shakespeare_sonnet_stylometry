XL

Take all my loves, my dearest, if thou wilt,
And find in them no lessening of my store;
For what I gave was never free from guilt,
Since loving thee, I could not give thee more.
If thou dost take what once was mine alone,
Thou tak’st but that which first belonged to thee;
For in thy grace all lesser loves were grown,
And drew their life from thy supremacy.
Yet in that taking lies a subtle pain,
That what I held is now no longer mine;
Though reason tells me it returns again
To rightful source, and flows from love divine.
Thus I am wronged, yet cannot call it so,
For all I lose is what I owe to thee.
