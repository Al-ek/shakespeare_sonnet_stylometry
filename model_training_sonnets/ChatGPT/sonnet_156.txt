II

When winter’s chill doth nip the tender rose,
And silent winds do pierce the frozen air,
My thoughts of thee in gentle warmth repose,
And bid my spirit rise above despair.
Though time shall mar the beauty of the earth,
Yet in thy name, eternity takes root;
Thy grace becomes my solace and my mirth,
A sacred flame that darkness cannot mute.
No mortal hand may hold what hearts compose,
Nor chain the rivers of affection’s tide;
So let this sonnet, like a faithful rose,
Bloom where thy virtues and my thoughts reside.
Though seasons pass, thy memory remains,
A star unmoved amidst the worldly pains.
