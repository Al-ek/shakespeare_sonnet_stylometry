XLI

Those petty faults that youth and beauty breed
Attend thee still, as shadows follow light;
For where such grace doth flourish and exceed,
Temptation waits to prove its wandering might.
Thy sweetness draws the gaze of every eye,
And hearts, once free, grow captive at thy view;
Small wonder then if thou, being tempted, try
The paths where others would be drawn by you.
Yet though I know the cause of thy misdeed,
And see how beauty pleads thy soft excuse,
My wounded love doth inwardly still bleed,
Though reason bids me not thy faults accuse.
For charm may err, yet still remain most fair,
And love must pardon what it cannot bear.
