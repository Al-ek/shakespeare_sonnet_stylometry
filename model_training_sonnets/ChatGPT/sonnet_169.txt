XV

The springtime blooms awaken from the snow,
And fill the air with fragrance pure and sweet;
Yet none so fragrant as thy gentle glow,
Nor light so fair, nor movement half so fleet.
The world delights in change, yet I remain,
A constant witness to thy steadfast grace;
Though storms arise, and sorrow leaves its stain,
Thy memory no force of time can chase.
So let my verse, in humble measure wrought,
Encircle thee with words both firm and kind;
A faithful record of the love I’ve sought,
A gift eternal for the fleeting mind.
Though mortal hands may fail, my thought endures,
Preserving thee through all life’s varied lures.
