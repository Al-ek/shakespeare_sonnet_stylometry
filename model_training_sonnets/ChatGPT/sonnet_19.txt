XIX

O morning light that kisses all the hills,
And wakes the valley from its slumb’ring dream;
Thy gentle warmth the frozen heart instills,
And turns the barren soil to silver stream.
Though men may toil and never see reward,
Their labor echoes in the song of time;
And every step that treads the earthly sward
Is mirrored in a verse, a quiet rhyme.
Let then my lines ascend like morning sun,
And chase the darkness from the mind’s domain;
For in each word a subtle truth is spun,
A fleeting grace that mortal hands retain.
And when the day departs in golden fire,
The soul shall rest, fulfilled of its desire.
