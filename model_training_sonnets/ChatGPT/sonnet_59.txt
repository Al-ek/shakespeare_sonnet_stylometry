LIX

If there be nothing new beneath the sun,
But all that is hath been before in time,
Then hath some former age already done
The praise I now attempt in humble rhyme.
Some ancient poet must have sung thy grace,
And set thy beauty in eternal lines;
Yet if that verse could truly match thy face,
Why fades its fame while still thy light outshines?
Perhaps those pens, though skillful in their day,
Did lack the fire that now thy presence gives;
Or time hath worn their noble works away,
While in my verse thy living spirit lives.
Thus though all things repeat what once was known,
In thee I find a truth that stands alone.
