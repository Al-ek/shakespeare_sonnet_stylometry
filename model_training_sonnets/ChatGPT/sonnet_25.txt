XXV

Let those who boast of honors at the court,
And bask in favor of a prince’s eye,
Delight in triumphs fortune doth consort,
And build their pride where fickle glories lie.
I envy not their pomp nor gilded name,
Nor seek the wreath that crowns ambition’s race;
For I possess a richer, steadier flame,
In thy regard, that no decline can chase.
The great may fall when princes change their will,
And glory fades as quickly as it came;
But love, that stands upon affection still,
Outlives the wreck of title, power, and fame.
Then let them lose what fortune once did give,
In thy sure love eternally I live.
