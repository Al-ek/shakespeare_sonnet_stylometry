XLI
What need have I of any other art
Than this plain study of thy face and ways,
What other school could so instruct the heart
Or better spend the currency of days?
The philosopher doth pore on dusty tomes
And wrest his wisdom from another's page,
The mariner by calculation roams
And finds his truth in instrument and gauge.
But I have all my learning here in thee,
Each glance a lecture and each word a text,
I am enrolled in no academy
Save this sweet school where I am always vexed.
Teach on then, love, for I am never done;
The lesson ends not till the last breath's run.
