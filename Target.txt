When dawn, with timid gold, unseals the sky,
And whispers light upon the drowsing earth,
My thoughts, like swallows restless, learn to fly
Toward thee, the sovereign cause of all their mirth.

For in thy gaze such quiet fires reside
As shame the sun’s proud claim to rule the day;
And in thy voice, where softer truths abide,
The storms of doubt and sorrow fade away.

Yet cruel the hour that bids my sight depart,
And leaves me captive to remembrance still;
For absence carves thy image in my heart,
More sharp than presence ever shaped my will.

Then come, sweet thief, restore what thou hast ta’en,
Or leave me rich in longing’s gentle pain.
