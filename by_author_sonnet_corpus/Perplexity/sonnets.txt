I
When dawn unfolds her robes of blushing gold,
And whispers wake the slumbering fields anew,
The world forgets the cruel night so cold,
And learns once more what tender light can do.
Yet beauty fades though morning loves to stay,
Its promise bound to noon’s consuming flame;
So too shall joy with time be swept away,
And none can keep the gift that none can claim.
But still within the heart a spark remains,
Unworn by years, unshadowed by regret;
For though the body bends beneath its chains,
The soul remembers what it can’t forget.
O fleeting days, your leaving teaches clear—
To love each moment as it first drew near.

II
The stars attend the court of sleepless night,
Arrayed in silence, solemn, pure, and kind;
Each gem a thought that flickers into sight,
A secret echo of the dreaming mind.
Thou art among their number, yet more rare,
For mortal clay in thee finds heaven’s tone;
When darkness blinds, I sense thy spirit there,
A light that teaches stars they’re not alone.
If fate should steal the firmament away,
And night be draped in endless shade unkind,
I’d forge new stars from words I dare to say,
To hang thy likeness high, and thus outshine.
For all the heavens gather at thy call,
And yield their light to one who owns it all.

III
I sought for truth within the scholar’s art,
And found but mirrors clouded by repute;
The truest lore was written in the heart,
Whose pulse play’d tutor to the mind’s dispute.
Books teach the past, but passion teaches now,
And ink cannot confine what breath imparts;
The soul learns best where love will yet endow
Its tender script upon the finest hearts.
So come, dear Muse, and write thy verse in me,
That time may fade but never thee erase;
For what is writ in mortal memory,
Finds afterlife upon another’s face.
The heart’s own language needs no studied art,
For love’s plain tongue outlasts the richest chart.

IV
A winter sun hung low in sullen skies,
Its warmth but rumour on the frozen air;
Yet in thy glance a gentler summer lies,
And melts the chill that reason cannot bear.
Thou art the ember on my ashen year,
The flame that dares the cold to stay its hand;
Through thee my frost-born doubt dissolves to clear,
And spring renews the long-forgotten land.
No wind can still the promise in thy eyes,
Nor snows erase the laughter of thy voice;
Though years may bury youth ‘neath age’s guise,
They cannot dull the glow of mutual choice.
Let winter rage, so long as thou art near,
For love can warm the longest, starkest year.

V
The painter labors o’er his trembling art,
And in his hues the breath of time is caged;
Yet never stroke could match the beating heart,
Nor brush revive what ages have assuaged.
I, too, would capture beauty on a page,
To fix thy passing grace in perfect form;
But words grow frail when faced with mortal stage,
And melt like snow before a summer storm.
Thus learn I this: to write is but to plead,
To beg the sun to linger in the west;
No poem lives save where true hearts concede,
That what is lost in ink may yet be blessed.
So if my verse grows dim when thou art gone,
The failing light shall still declare thee shone.

VI
The river runs, forgetting where it flows,
Its mirror holding neither night nor day;
So too the heart forgets the pain it knows,
When love returns to wash old scars away.
Each grief once thought eternal finds release,
Each wound, though deep, surrenders to the stream;
For time redeems what sorrow shall decrease,
And life rebuilds the ruins of its dream.
Yet some remains—a tender ache, a chord,
That hums of joy once tasted, now set free;
Such pain, though sharp, becomes its own reward,
A proof of what the heart was taught to be.
The sweetest songs are born of tears that fell,
When loss became a truth the soul could tell.

VII
When I behold the passing of the moon,
Her borrowed glow content to fade from sight,
I see in her my own departing boon,
A beauty fed by some more constant light.
For thou, my sun, hast lent thy grace to me,
And all my radiance is but thy gift;
Shouldst thou withdraw, my gleam would cease to be,
My orbit lost, my core bereft, adrift.
Then stay, fair source, until the dawn is done,
And let my borrowed beam still hum thy tone;
For though I shine, my shining comes from one,
Whose fire crowns the heavens as their own.
If I must fade, then let it be with thee,
The light of love my sole eternity.

VIII
Time’s subtle knife carves marks none else can see,
And youth, like spring, yields fruit to summer’s call;
Yet love defies that stern mortality,
It blooms anew when all the flowers fall.
Thy cheek may pale, thy hair may silver turn,
Yet inward light preserves thy prime unbroke;
The fire within shall all the ashes burn,
And laugh at years that dream to dim thy smoke.
O heart that beats against the march of age,
Write boldly in the sand of fleeting hours;
Though tides erase the words, they not the wage,
That joy once paid to beauty’s airy powers.
We age in form, but essence stands divine—
The rose still breathes when gone the outward shine.

IX
I walked among the ruins of delight,
Where joy had built its temples long ago;
Their echoes whispered softly through the night,
Of vows the morning winds refused to know.
Yet in that hush I felt thy presence near,
A ghost of laughter lingering in the hall;
And suddenly the dark grew bright and clear,
As though the past forgave me after all.
O memory, thou fickle, precious friend,
That wounds and heals in one uncertain turn,
Teach me what lies beyond each lover’s end,
When embers sleep but never cease to burn.
For love, though buried deep in time’s decay,
Still hums its tune beneath the world’s dismay.

X
In dreams I chase what waking hours conceal,
The tender glance that never met my own;
Each phantom kiss the night would half reveal,
Then fade before the dawn could make it known.
Yet sadness sweetens where such visions grow,
Their ache a gift the heart would not resign;
For though I wake to emptiness below,
The thought of love renews my faith divine.
So let me dream, though dreams be doomed to part,
And taste again what day would soon deny;
A fleeting truth still warms the watcher’s heart,
Though built of shade beneath the morning sky.
What dream can do, reality denies—
To teach the soul what all the waking tries.

XI
When envy stirs, I turn to gaze on thee,
And all its poison melts before thy grace;
Thy calm rebukes my restless vanity,
And peace descends upon my troubled face.
For beauty’s charm is but a fleeting loan,
Yet virtue roots where tempest cannot tread;
Thou art the ground where gentle seeds are sown,
And every word thou speak’st makes sorrow fled.
So envy dies, replaced by reverence deep,
And in its grave I plant contentment’s bloom;
Thy worth is watch I vow my soul shall keep,
A lantern lit within this mortal gloom.
Thy presence cures what all the world impairs—
A grace that grows the more another shares.

XII
The mind would rule, yet love will not obey,
For passion mocks the scholar’s measured tongue;
Reason may chart the order of the day,
But heart writes laws by which the soul is sung.
I strove to cage my fondness in a guise,
To speak of calm while thunder filled my breast;
But silence broke where that sweet tempest lies—
Confession proved what prudence had repressed.
Then let it rage; no storm can last too long,
And from its crash the truest peace is born;
The heart must burn before it learns the song
That only ashes properly adorn.
Love, thou unruly tutor, wild yet wise,
I learn thy truth when every reason dies.

XIII
I plant my hope where soil is yet untried,
And water it with patience day by day;
Though seasons mock and tempests turn aside,
Still faith compels the root to find its way.
For love grows slow where haste would see it bloom,
And depth is born of trials long endured;
The bud that braves the harshness of its doom,
Will yield a stronger scent, by pain matured.
So smile not at the labour of my care,
Nor call me fool who tends to barren ground;
The fruit shall come when few believe it fair,
And joy shall rise where silence most is found.
For dreams that wait beneath the hardest stone,
Are those that crown the patient soul alone.

XIV
O hour of dusk, most tender time of day,
When sun and star conspire on one dim line,
Thou teachest well how opposites can stay,
And share their peace as if by grand design.
The dark comes soft; the light resists not fate,
They meet, embrace, and yield in equal grace;
So may our hearts through argument abate,
And harmonize where once was sharp debate.
What night can break, if day consents to blend?
What storm can thrive where calm and passion meet?
In union’s art all wrath shall find its end,
As dusk makes night and day alike complete.
Then hold with me this twilight’s gentle tone,
And make our quarrels seeds that love has sown.

XV
A poet’s quill may tremble with its truth,
Yet still it dares to tell what tongue would hide;
Such is the fire that burns in tender youth,
And leaves but ash for wisdom to deride.
Still, I would risk the scorch to grasp that flame,
For better burned than never once alight;
To live half-safe is but to die in shame,
While passion gives the weary world its sight.
So write I still, though ink and heart may bleed,
My letters marked with longing’s restless stain;
The wound I make is also what I need,
A proof my soul’s alive despite its pain.
If words be sin, then let me sin once more,
For sin so sweet must lead to something pure.

XVI
Beneath the boughs of April’s newborn green,
We talked of days that never yet had been;
The air was youth, the breeze a song unseen,
And all the world confessed itself to dream.
O gentle time, thou thief of careless hearts,
Whose passing leaves no footprint but desire,
Return in thought, though distant now thou art,
And warm once more the ashes of that fire.
Though now we part where once we were entwined,
My soul keeps vigil at that meadow’s gate;
For memory grants what distance undermines,
And turns lost joy to faith in future fate.
If spring must fade, then still its echo stays—
A bloom preserved beyond the count of days.

XVII
The sea, unruly mirror of my thought,
Reflects the tempest reason could not still;
Each crest declares the battle passion fought,
Each trough the calm that follows trembling will.
Yet as I stand upon that endless shore,
I find my heart akin to wave’s return;
For though it breaks, it rises all the more,
And learns from fall the strength that tides discern.
So beating life, repeat thy wild refrain,
Crash, mend, renew—be constant through unrest;
The soul that yields to rhythm, not to pain,
Shall find in storm the peace it once suppressed.
What ocean hides beneath its face so deep,
Is strength to fight and faith enough to keep.

XVIII
Thou art not lost when distance bars the way,
For thought may travel swifter still than feet;
I hold thy absence dearer by the day,
And find in lack a presence more complete.
For memory builds what sight would soon destroy,
And absence serves what closeness might betray;
The heart grows fond when withheld from its joy,
And silence speaks the words we cannot say.
Thus parting is no death, but tested grace,
A forge where love is tempered, true, and wise;
When next we meet, the light upon thy face
Will prove what distance never could disguise.
For separation is but space to grow,
And love returns more certain for its woe.

XIX
The clock declares what mortals cannot keep,
Each toll a whisper of forgotten years;
It sings of birth, of laughter, and of sleep,
Of joy made brief by sorrow’s slow arrears.
Yet time, though thief, imparts a hidden gift,
It teaches worth by showing what must end;
Through loss we learn what moments most uplift,
And count our blessings not by wealth, but friend.
So turn, sweet world, and mark thy numbered round,
Each passing hour but deepens what remains;
Though years grow dim, their music still is sound,
Resounding soft through memory’s sweet plains.
The clock may cease, but not the love it taught—
What time withdraws is never wholly lost.

XX
Now ends my verse but not my heart’s intent,
For words are mortal though their will aspires;
The ink grows pale, but passion stands unspent,
And lights the dark with self-renewing fires.
Each sonnet born was but a breath of flame,
A spark released to kindle hope anew;
They fade, yet leave the ash of whispered name,
The proof that love once lived, and still was true.
So take these lines, dear reader of my dream,
Their mirror made for hearts that seek the same;
What I have lost in truth, I have in theme—
A love disguised in every sound and frame.
Though silence comes to claim the poet’s art,
The echo stays within another’s heart.

XXI
When morning’s breath unfolds the tender bloom,
And shadows flee from night’s departed care,
The world seems new, freed from the heart’s doom,
As if all grief were washed from earthly air.
Yet every hour that paints the sky with gold
Reminds my soul how swift such hues decay,
For beauty’s flame grows faint as seasons old,
And love seems summer’s leaf grown pale and gray.
Still, in thy eyes I find a sun so kind,
That winter’s frost no longer chills my breast,
Thy gaze unbinds the paling cords of mind,
And all my thoughts in thee at last find rest.
So let the dawn forget its borrowed light,
For thou alone dost make my darkness bright.

XXII
The sea was calm, yet held a secret fire,
Its waves like whispers hid beneath the foam,
I stood enthralled, consumed by vain desire,
To find in depths a truth I’d call my home.
Each crest that broke seemed echo to my will,
Each sigh of wind rehearsed my heart’s complaint,
That longing’s song must play, though time stand still,
While sweet assurance wanes, forever faint.
But from that shore I learned a peace unknown,
That love’s true breadth lies not in what we claim,
For hearts unbound shall know themselves alone,
Unlit by pride, unscarred by want or shame.
So let the ocean keep what dreams depart,
Its calm now beats the rhythm of my heart.

XXIII
I wrote thy name upon the morning sand,
And saw it fade beneath the kissing tide,
As though the sea had stretched her moistened hand,
To steal the hope that in my breast did hide.
Yet though the letters died, their breath remains,
For memory guards what water cannot keep,
And in my thought eternal ink enchains
All sweetness stirred from slumber’s gentle sleep.
O fickle surf, thou teach’st a mortal truth,
That marks of love by chance are oft erased,
But deep within the soul’s unending youth,
No wave nor age nor death can make displaced.
The sea forgets, but I forever know,
Thy name shall bloom where silent waters flow.

XXIV
Thy voice, the echo of some far-off spring,
Hath wakened buds that slept in frost’s command,
It stirs my heart as bright seraphic wing,
Might brush the dust from sorrow’s burdened hand.
I once believed the year too cold for song,
That hope must perish with the leaves’ decline,
But thou hast shown that love, though held too long,
Can warm in ashes still with flame divine.
Now time, that tyrant bent on dimming hue,
Hath lost its rule upon my faithful soul,
For what is aged when each thought of you
Renews my spirit whole where shadows roll?
Thy sound, though mortal lips may cease to frame,
Lives on through silence, ever still the same.

XXV
Behold the night, that queen of shaded hour,
She weaves the stars like pearls in sable guise,
And guards the dream that peace may not devour,
While grief sleeps soft beneath her tranquil eyes.
Yet even she must fade when morning wakes,
Her crown dissolved in sun’s unclouded reign,
And all her tender calm the east forsakes,
To yield to light that burns with sweet disdain.
So too my soul, by rest and joy betrayed,
Must welcome dawn though darkness kindly shields,
For love, like day, must pierce what fear hath laid,
And chase all doubt from cold forbidden fields.
So when night dies, my heart with thee shall rise,
To build a dawn within thine endless eyes.

XXVI
When years have bowed my body’s noble frame,
And whispered through my veins the hush of peace,
Let time record not triumph, wealth, nor name,
But how thy glance made every sorrow cease.
The seasons steal their petals one by one,
Yet thou remain’st, unfading though they part,
Thy kindness more enduring than the sun,
Thy grace the constant rhythm of my heart.
Though youth depart and beauty shrink with cold,
Thy shining worth outlives all mortal hue,
And in the book of love’s unnumbered fold,
My soul shall write eternity in you.
Then mark this vow, by aging hand twice sealed,
Thy love shall bloom when death himself is healed.

XXVII
I saw the hawk ascend through azure skies,
Its wings unfurled, a prayer upon the day,
And thought how love through passion’s storm must rise,
Or lose the air where truth would freely stay.
For hearts like anchors drag the soul to rest,
But those that trust in wind grow strong and free,
Though held by faith that trials may test,
Still lift their flight through fate’s uncertain sea.
Thus might we walk, unshackled yet entwined,
Two spirits curved like arches of the dawn,
Our union wrought by will and not confined,
A bond that’s bent but never wholly gone.
So soar we forth, and let no fear remain,
For love unbound doth never fly in vain.

XXVIII
How oft the moon doth mock my sleepless thought,
Her silver gaze recalling all I lack,
Each whisper asks of dreams desire hath sought,
Yet time and pride do hold my heart aback.
For though I long to speak, my voice is chained,
By reason’s law that bids emotion still,
And all my art, by silence harshly trained,
Can paint no truth but love’s unspoken will.
Yet in that quiet there’s a music rare,
A pulse that hums through shadows soft and dim,
It tells that hearts, though parted, secret share,
One faithful song beyond death’s measured hymn.
So let her shine; I’ll watch nor make reply,
For in her light my hidden hopes do lie.

XXIX
The rose may boast of beauty’s briefest stay,
Its scent a spell the moment it is born,
Yet through the dusk it yields itself away,
And leaves the stem in grief and scent forlorn.
But thou art not such fragile bloom of June,
Thy grace endures when all the garden dies,
Thou art the dawn, perpetual as noon,
Whose truth survives the fall of fading skies.
Let envy praise the fleeting reds of spring,
Or mourn the wilt of pride’s unsteady flame,
For I shall know no season’s less’ning sting,
While love bears fruit that time shall not reclaim.
Thus bloom thou still, the flower none uproot,
Whose breath makes death itself take tender root.

XXX
Come sit with me upon the emerald field,
Where thought and laughter gently intertwine,
And all the aching hours that life may yield,
Dissolve like mist before thy glance divine.
Thou hast that art which tranquil moments teach,
To mend with joy what sorrow left undone,
Thy simple hand can calm what none could reach,
And light the path too dark for any sun.
So while this earth takes measure of its years,
I’ll weight my hours not by the clock’s decree,
But by the peace that rises when thou’rt near,
And turns the tide of fate to harmony.
O time, be still, for life is ever fair,
When every breath doth keep thee near and there.

XXXI
There is a mirror buried in the heart,
Where truth reflects in shapes the eyes deny,
Its glass reveals what lips may ne’er impart,
And teaches love to see and not to sigh.
O strange device, that binds both joy and fear,
And shows the face transformed by passion’s flame,
Through its clear depth I see thy likeness near,
Yet know thou art no shadow of my claim.
For love is not possession, nor design,
But light that burns where longing learns to yield,
It casts no net, though hearts therein entwine,
And turns the wound itself to mercy’s shield.
Thus let thine image dwell where self departs,
A mirror shining in two kindred hearts.

XXXII
The poet’s hand may scratch his fleeting rhyme,
And weigh his thoughts upon the scales of fame,
But no bright word can overthrow the time,
Or draw from dust the life it long did claim.
I envy not the laurel or the praise,
That crowns proud brows for sounds of passing grace,
For in thine eyes I find the lyric’s blaze,
And all of verse meets silence in thy face.
The ink shall fade, but thou dost never pale,
Thy breath itself the meter of the dawn,
Thy pulse the rhythm no scribe could unveil,
When mortal voice to immortal soul is drawn.
So write thee not; the page shall come undone,
Thou art the line that time cannot outrun.

XXXIII
I’ve watched the year unfold its painted scheme,
The buds of May, the crimson of July,
Each hue contrasts the pall of winter’s dream,
Yet none could match the brightness of thine eye.
No poet’s brush, nor painter’s mortal hue,
Hath found the shade that grace in thee displays,
They chase the sun, yet capture but a clue,
While thou dost weave the light through subtle ways.
Thy beauty lives not bound by outward view,
But like the wind is felt, though none may see,
It stirs the soul, revives the sense anew,
And teaches hearts to trust what eyes can’t be.
In thee I find the art that time forgot,
A living dream by every dawn begot.

XXXIV
Thy silence holds more counsel than all speech,
It speaks in calm where chaos rends the air,
And though no sound thy gentle lips may teach,
Thy every glance makes rhetoric of care.
The loudest tongue breeds reason without worth,
Its noise conceals what truth would show in peace,
But thou dost walk as heaven visits earth,
And from thy stillness doubts at last release.
O grant me strength to mirror such repose,
To weigh my words with patience like thine own,
And in each breath let quiet thought disclose,
A wisdom deeper than the mind hath known.
No thunder need attend what hearts discern,
For peace’s flame makes even stars to burn.

XXXV
If ever grief should draw my gaze to ground,
And freeze the pulse that hope had set aflame,
Thy touch returns, the sweetest cure yet found,
Reviving faith where fear had won her game.
For thou dost speak in kindness, not in pride,
And guide my step through night’s unyielding maze,
Thy presence grows where all the world denied,
A sun reborn in sorrow’s midnight haze.
I’ve known the stars to dim beneath the rain,
And every shining joy to fade at will,
Yet when I think on thee, no loss remains,
The wound is gone, the sky once more stands still.
So love may heal what time could not restore,
Through thou, my heart, now grieves no more.

XXXVI
There is no gold that matches virtue’s glow,
No crown as rich as humble thought of thee,
For those who seek in fame their hearts to show,
Know not the height to which true souls may see.
Thine worth unspoken makes the mighty small,
And humbles pride more sharp than sword or art,
No robe nor gem adorns the grandest hall,
So fair as love that clothes the faithful heart.
Let courts applaud the glitter of their prize,
Let merchants praise the measure of their gain,
For only you, who truth in silence ties,
Make loss itself to bloom, and want a reign.
Thy virtue’s fire, though lowly, burns so wide,
It lights the path that time cannot divide.

XXXVII
When age hath worn the nerve of fervent youth,
And silver threads replace the burning hue,
The heart yet beats its rhythm unto truth,
And finds its spring eternal born in you.
Thou art the sun that warms what years make cold,
The flame that kindles time’s departing coal,
So though each breath be numbered, I behold,
A grace that lives beyond the mortal whole.
What care I then for wrinkles or for years,
When love itself still kind and constant proves?
For every mark of age the soul reveres,
As proof of depth that suffering removes.
Grow old with me; in thee all tempests cease,
Where time grows frail, love only grows in peace.

XXXVIII
O Memory, thou friend and traitor both,
Thou fill’st the mind with honey and with gall,
Thy sweet recall reveals what longing doth,
Yet leaves the heart bereft in echoes’ call.
Still, I must praise thy fleeting, cruel grace,
For though thou wound, thou teach’st me to endure,
Thy mirror shows the beauty time erased,
And thus makes grief itself to feel more pure.
When I recall the touch, the vow, the gaze,
Though tears may rise, yet joy beneath them blooms,
For pain, when true, the heart’s devotion sways,
And loss itself the flame of love resumes.
Then let me dream of hours too dear to stay,
For memory feeds what hope would steal away.

XXXIX
From tempest’s mouth and storm’s unyielding hand,
The ship of mortal hearts is cast astray,
Yet thou dost shine, a light above the land,
That guides through dark the soul to tranquil bay.
No wave too fierce can drown the thought of thee,
No wind erase the calm thy name imparts,
For even chaos holds serenity,
When sheltered by the compass of thy heart.
And though the sea may rage, the night may fall,
Still anchored deep I hold my steadfast creed,
That love outlasts the loudest ocean’s call,
And grows through strife as roots through stone take seed.
So let the storm proclaim its wild decree,
My faith stands firm, for thou remain’st to me.

XL
Now come the end of verses still and fair,
Their breath released upon the tide of air,
Though words decay, their meaning shall not die,
For truth in love finds endless harmony.
I sought through rhyme the shape of thine embrace,
Through measured pause the echo of thy grace,
But language fails where feeling takes command,
For hearts write lines that thought can scarce withstand.
So let the ink dissolve, the page be torn,
Let silence hold what syllables adorn,
For every sound that from these lips did fall,
Was born of thee, and thou art all in all.
And though the quill now sleeps, the song shall stay,
While love through ages chants thy bright array.
