XV

The morning dew upon the meadow lies
Like scattered pearls the dawn has gently spilled;
So rests my hope within thy tender eyes,
A treasure by no earthly measure filled.
For when I gaze upon that quiet light,
I see the promise of a brighter day,
A beacon shining through the darkest night
And guiding all my doubts to fade away.
Let others chase the riches of the earth
And count their fortunes by the gold they hold;
I measure wealth by love’s enduring worth,
A value neither tarnished nor grown old.
Thus in thy glance I find my richest store,
A wealth that grows the more I seek it more.
