VIII

Oft have I walked the shore at eventide
And watched the waves embrace the fading light,
Each crest a promise rising with the tide,
Each fall a sigh that softens into night.
So moves my love, in rhythms slow and deep,
A pulse that answers to the moon above;
It wakes when thou art near, and when I sleep
It murmurs still the constancy of love.
Let tempests rage and scatter foam and spray—
The sea endures, though shaken for a while;
And so my heart, though troubled by the day,
Finds peace again within thy tender smile.
For love, like oceans, cannot be confined,
But shapes the world according to its mind.
