The world may praise the warrior’s iron hand
Or crown the scholar for his learned lore,
But I, who hold no kingdom, wealth, nor land,
Find greater triumph in the love I bore.
For what are victories carved in stone
Compared to joys that live within the soul?
The proud may rise, yet stand at last alone,
While gentle hearts achieve a nobler goal.
Thus do I boast not of my fleeting deeds,
Nor seek the laurels others chase in vain;
I measure worth by how my spirit feeds
The love that grows despite the world’s disdain.
And if I win thy heart, though all else fail,
I claim a prize no earthly crown can scale.
