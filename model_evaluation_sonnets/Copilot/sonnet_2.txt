II

The winter wind may shake the stubborn tree
And strip its branches bare of summer’s pride,
Yet still the root holds firm beneath the sea
Of frozen earth where secret strengths abide.
So fares my soul when fortune’s tempests roar:
Though outward hopes lie scattered on the ground,
Thy voice, a warmth no storm can wrestle sore,
Revives the seed where future blooms are found.
Let others dread the season’s icy breath;
I fear it not while thou art near to me.
For love, well tended, mocks the threat of death
And thrives where lesser hearts would cease to be.
Thus in the cold I flourish all the more,
For thou art spring behind my winter’s door.
