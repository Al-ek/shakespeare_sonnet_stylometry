IV

The moon, though pale, commands the midnight air
And draws the tides with but a borrowed gleam;
So thou, whose beauty seems divinely fair,
Dost rule my thoughts as moonlight rules a stream.
Yet unlike her, whose glow is not her own,
Thy radiance springs from fires within thy breast,
A sovereign flame by no reflection shown,
That burns the brighter when by love caressed.
If heaven’s lamp should falter in the sky
And leave the world to wander in the dark,
Thy presence still would teach my soul to fly
And set ablaze the night with one small spark.
For though the moon may wane and fade from view,
The light I cherish never wanes in you.
