III

I sought in books the wisdom of the wise,
In marble halls the counsel of the great,
But found no truth to match thy earnest eyes
Nor any law more just than simple fate.
For fate it was that led my steps to thine,
And bound our wandering spirits into one;
A quiet pact no mortal hand could sign,
Yet sealed as surely as the rising sun.
What need have we of sages long since dead,
Whose dusty tomes grow brittle with the years,
When every word thy living lips have said
Outshines their lore and silences my fears?
Thus do I learn, by grace of thy sweet art,
The deepest truths are written in the heart.
