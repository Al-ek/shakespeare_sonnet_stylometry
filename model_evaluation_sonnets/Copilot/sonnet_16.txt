XVI

The restless world may hurry to and fro
And chase the fleeting shadows of desire,
But I, content with what I truly know,
Find all my longing quenched by one small fire.
For love, once kindled, asks for little more
Than honest hearts that beat in gentle time;
It thrives not on the wealth that men adore,
But on the quiet trust of the sublime.
So let the world pursue its frantic pace,
Let ambition drive its ceaseless race;
I choose instead the calm of thy embrace,
A refuge from the storms that others chase.
For in thy love I find a peace so true
That all the world seems new when I am with you.
