XIV

The quill may scratch its ink upon the page
And strive to capture what the heart would speak,
Yet language falters, bound by mortal cage,
And fails to show the truth my spirit seeks.
For how can words, mere shadows of the mind,
Portray the fire that burns when thou art near?
How can a verse, though lovingly designed,
Express the depth of all I hold most dear?
Still must I try, compelled by fond desire,
To shape my love in syllables and rhyme;
Though every line falls short of what I tire
To say, I trust it shall endure through time.
For though my art may never equal thee,
It stands as witness to my loyalty.
