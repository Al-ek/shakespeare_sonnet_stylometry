XX

When evening folds her cloak across the land
And stars awaken in the quiet sky,
I feel thy presence take me by the hand
And lead my wandering thoughts to where you lie.
Though miles may stretch between our resting place,
No distance dims the bond our hearts have spun;
For love, unbounded by the laws of space,
Unites what time and travel cannot shun.
So let the night grow deep and shadows fall,
Let silence rule the hours till morning’s gleam;
My heart still hears thy voice above it all
And walks beside thee in a waking dream.
For though the world may keep our forms apart,
No force can sever love that joins the heart.
