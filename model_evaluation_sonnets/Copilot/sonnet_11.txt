XI

When shadows stretch across the weary ground
And twilight weaves her veil of dusky blue,
I hear within the hush a softer sound—
The echo of my longing calling you.
For though the day may scatter us apart
And bid our steps to wander different ways,
Thy presence lingers in my faithful heart
And lights my thoughts with unforgotten rays.
Thus even in the hour of fading light,
When silence rules and all the world is still,
Thy memory rises like a star at night
And warms the chill that evening cannot kill.
So though the dark may claim the sky above,
It cannot dim the constancy of love.
