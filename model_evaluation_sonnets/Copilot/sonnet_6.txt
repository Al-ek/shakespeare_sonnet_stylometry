VI

When first I saw thee walking through the crowd,
A hush fell over all my restless mind;
The world, once clamorous, grew soft and bowed
As though in awe of something rare refined.
I knew thee not, yet felt a stirring deep,
A whisper rising from a hidden place,
As if my heart, long roused from troubled sleep,
Awoke to greet a long‑forgotten face.
Since then, each day has shaped a sweeter tune,
A melody that follows where I tread;
And though the sun may hide behind the moon,
Thy memory lights the path my feet must tread.
For chance may start the story of our days,
But love alone sustains its endless praise.
