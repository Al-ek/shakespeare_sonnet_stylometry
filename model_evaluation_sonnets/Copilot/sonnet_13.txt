XIII

Thy laughter is a fountain in the air,
A silver music dancing on the breeze;
It lifts the weight of sorrow and of care
And turns the heaviest hearts to gentle ease.
No minstrel’s tune, though crafted with delight,
Could match the sweetness of that merry sound;
It brightens noon and softens deepest night,
And plants a field of joy on barren ground.
So let the world grow weary with its woes,
Let storms arise and tempests shake the sky;
Thy laughter still shall blossom like the rose
And teach my troubled spirit how to fly.
For in that sound my soul finds liberty,
A song of hope that keeps me ever free.
