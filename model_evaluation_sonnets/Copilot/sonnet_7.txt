VII

The painter strives with brush and tempered hue
To catch the fleeting wonder of the morn,
Yet fails to match the loveliness of you
Whose glance outshines the day when it is born.
No sculptor’s hand, though guided by the gods,
Could carve the softness of thy gentle smile;
No poet’s verse, though freed from mortal odds,
Could trace the charm that makes my life worthwhile.
Yet still I try, compelled by fond desire,
To honor thee with all my humble art;
And though my words fall short of what I tire
To show, they speak the fullness of my heart.
For though no craft can mirror what I see,
My love itself becomes my mastery.
