XIX

The desert sun may scorch the barren plain
And leave the earth to thirst beneath its glare,
Yet still a single bloom may rise again
And prove that life persists in places bare.
So does my hope endure when trials rise,
Though hardships press like sand upon my soul;
For in thy love a hidden fountain lies
That keeps my weary spirit strong and whole.
Let others falter in the heat of strife,
Let doubt consume the hearts that cannot cope;
I draw my strength from thee, the well of life,
And drink the waters of unending hope.
Thus even in the harshest days I see
A promise of renewal born from thee.
