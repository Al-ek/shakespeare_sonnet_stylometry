XVII

If every tear I shed could water spring
And coax a blossom from the barren ground,
Then would the earth in sudden glory sing
And bloom with colors rich and unconfined.
But tears alone cannot restore the past,
Nor mend the wounds that time has carved so deep;
Yet love, more gentle than the shadows cast,
Can soothe the heart and lull its grief to sleep.
Thus when I mourn the moments lost to fate,
I turn to thee and find my solace there;
Thy presence lifts the burden of my state
And teaches me the art of quiet care.
For though the past may haunt with bitter sting,
Thy love renews the promise of the spring.
