IX

If I could gather all the stars that gleam
And bind them in a garland for thy hair,
The heavens would grow jealous of the dream
And curse the night for losing what was fair.
Yet thou hast no need of celestial light,
For thou thyself dost teach the stars to shine;
They borrow splendor from thy presence bright
And pale beside the radiance that is thine.
So let them keep their distant, lofty throne—
Their cold perfection suits them all too well;
I choose the warmth of love that I have known,
A fire no cosmic brilliance can dispel.
For though the stars may glitter from above,
Thy glow alone can satisfy my love.
