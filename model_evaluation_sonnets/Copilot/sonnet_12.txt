XII

I’ve seen the proudest towers bow to dust
And mighty empires crumble into sand;
I’ve watched the treasures forged by mortal trust
Slip through the trembling grasp of human hand.
Yet one small truth withstands the wreck of years:
That love, once kindled, cannot be undone.
It weathers sorrow, triumphs over fears,
And shines more brightly than the setting sun.
So let the monuments of kings decay,
Let time erase the glories they have known;
For though their splendor fades and slips away,
The love we share endures when they are gone.
Thus in a world where nothing seems to last,
Our hearts remain unshaken by the past.
