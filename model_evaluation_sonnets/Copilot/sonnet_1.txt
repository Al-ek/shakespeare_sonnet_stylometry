I

When dawn unbars the gates of silent night,
And spills her gold upon the drowsing land,
My thoughts, long wandering in uncertain light,
Return to thee as ships return to sand.
For though the world may tempt my roving eye
With fleeting wonders dressed in borrowed grace,
No charm endures beneath the changing sky
Save that which lives within thy constant face.
Thus am I bound, though free in outward show,
A pilgrim sworn to tread thy gentle sphere;
And every hour that bids my spirit go
Finds half my heart refusing to appear.
So let the morning rise or evening fall—
Thy love remains the compass of it all.
