XVIII

The nightingale may charm the dusky air
With melodies that wander through the trees,
Yet sweeter still the whispers that you share
When twilight settles on the evening breeze.
For though the bird may sing with perfect art,
Its song is but a moment’s fleeting grace;
Thy voice, more constant, lingers in my heart
And leaves a warmth no silence can erase.
So let the nightingale resume its tune
And fill the forest with its silver cry;
I need no music from the wandering moon
While thou art near to lift my spirit high.
For though the world may echo with delight,
Thy voice alone can sanctify the night.
