V

Time is a thief that steals with gentle hands,
Unseen until the plunder is complete;
He creeps through gardens, ravages the lands,
And leaves the fairest blossoms at his feet.
Yet though he claims the roses of the field
And dims the glow of youth’s unguarded fire,
Thy spirit holds a beauty none can yield,
A grace untouched by age or by desire.
For what is time to one whose heart is true,
Whose kindness grows though seasons rise and fall?
The years may change the face that I once knew,
But not the soul that shines beneath it all.
Thus let him take what outward forms he will—
Thy inner loveliness defies him still.
