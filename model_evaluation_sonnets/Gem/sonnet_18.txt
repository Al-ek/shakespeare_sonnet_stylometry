XVIII

As one who seeks a haven from the storm,
And finds a quiet harbor in the bay,
So do I find within thy lovely form,
A refuge from the troubles of the day.
For thou art like a star that shines above,
To guide the weary sailor on his way;
A beauty that is more than mortal love,
And through the darkness leads a brighter day.
So let the winds of fortune blow their fill,
And all the glory of the earth depart;
Thy image shall remain a constant still,
Within the quiet temple of my heart.
    For as the sun returns to wake the day,
    So shall thy love provide a constant stay.
