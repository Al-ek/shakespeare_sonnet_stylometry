XIV

As one who looks upon a painted scene,
And finds a beauty that is more than art,
So do I see within thy gentle mien,
The quiet image of a noble heart.
For thou art like a poem yet unread,
That holds a world of wonder in its lines;
A light that through the darkness still is shed,
And through the veil of mortal beauty shines.
So let the world its empty praise bestow,
On those who seek the glory of the earth;
I am contented with a deeper know,
And find in thee a more than mortal worth.
    For love is like a song that never ends,
    But through the ages still its music sends.
