XIX

When I behold the flowers that bloom in spring,
And see the meadows dressed in living green,
I think of all the joy that love can bring,
To those who find a beauty so serene.
For thou art like a garden in the sun,
Where every flower of virtue finds a place;
And though the race of life be almost run,
I see a springtime in thy lovely face.
So let the winter of our age come on,
And time perform his work of slow decay;
Our love shall flourish when the rest are gone,
And through the shadows find a brighter way.
    For as the sun gives life to all he sees,
    So shall our love survive the world’s decrees.
