VI

O how I fear the thievish hand of time,
Which steals the jewels from thy lovely brow;
And makes a ruin of thy beauty’s prime,
Which is the world’s chief ornament even now.
But then I think of this my gentle verse,
Which shall thy name to future ages bear;
And in its numbers all thy praise rehearse,
To show the world that thou wert passing fair.
So shall thy memory outlive the tomb,
And flourish when the monuments are gone;
A flower that shall in every season bloom,
And through the darkness lead the spirit on.
    For as the sun gives life to all he sees,
    So shall my verse give life to thy decrees.
