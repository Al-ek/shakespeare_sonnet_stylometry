II

As one who seeks a jewel in the sand,
And finds at last a gem of price untold,
So did I wander through a lonely land,
Till I beheld thy beauty’s purest gold.
Now am I rich beyond all earthly kings,
Possessed of wealth that time cannot impair;
For love a more than mortal treasure brings,
To those who find a spirit half so fair.
Let others boast of titles and of state,
And seek the empty praise of fickle men;
I am contented with a happier fate,
Which far transcends the reach of mortal pen.
    For in thy love I find my only prize,
    The star that guides me to the heavenly skies.
