VIII

The rose is fair but soon its petals fall,
And leave the stalk all withered and forlorn;
So is the fate of every thing and all,
That is within this mortal world ever born.
But thou hast found a more enduring grace,
Which shall not with the seasons pass away;
For in the quiet beauty of thy face,
I see a light that knows no winter day.
So let the winds of fortune blow their fill,
And time perform his work of slow decay;
Thy spirit shall remain a constant still,
And through the shadows find a brighter way.
    For love is like a rock within the sea,
    That stands unmoved for all eternity.
