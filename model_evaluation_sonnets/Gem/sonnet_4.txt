IV

The clock strikes ten and tells the passing hour,
And warns us that the day is almost spent;
So does the world lose every lovely flower,
Which for a little moment here was lent.
Then why shouldst thou thy beauty’s wealth refuse,
To those who seek to share it with their own?
Since what we keep we are most sure to lose,
And only what we give is truly known.
Be like the sun that scatters light around,
And makes the earth to flourish in his rays;
So shall thy name with honor still be found,
And live within the songs of future days.
    For love is not a thing to hide away,
    But like the sun should shine a constant day.
