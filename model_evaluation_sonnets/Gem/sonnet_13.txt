XIII

Why should I count the hours that pass away,
And mark the progress of the thievish sun?
Since in thy presence I have found a day,
Which shall remain when all the rest are done.
For thou art like a spring that never dries,
But flows for ever with a sweet increase;
A beauty that is more than mortal eyes,
And brings a quiet sense of perfect peace.
So let the years roll on their weary course,
And time perform his work of slow decay;
Our love is drawn from a more heavenly source,
And shall not with the shadows fade away.
    For truth and beauty are of one accord,
    And find in love their only sure reward.
