V

When I behold the stars that shine above,
And think of all the worlds that they behold,
I wonder at the power of human love,
Which is a story that is never old.
For though the heavens themselves may pass away,
And all the glory of the earth depart,
Yet love shall find a more than mortal stay,
Within the quiet temple of the heart.
So let the seasons change and years roll by,
And time perform his work of slow decay;
Our love is fixed within a higher sky,
And shall not with the shadows fade away.
    For truth and beauty are of one accord,
    And find in love their only sure reward.
