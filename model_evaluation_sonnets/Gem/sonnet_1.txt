I

When golden dawn doth lift his burning head,
And wakes the world from night’s dark heavy sleep,
Then is the shadow of my sorrow fled,
Which in the silent hours I used to keep.
For in thy face I see a brighter sun,
Than that which gilds the meadows with his light;
And though my race of life be almost run,
Thy beauty makes my autumn days seem bright.
So shall the winter of my age be stayed,
By the sweet summer that within thee lives;
And though the flowers of youth must surely fade,
Thy presence still a second springtime gives.
    Then let not time thy lovely form decay,
    Since in my heart thou liv’st a constant day.
