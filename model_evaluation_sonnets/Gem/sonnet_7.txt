VII

Why should I seek for words to tell thy praise,
When all the world can see thy beauty’s light?
And though I spend the remnant of my days,
I cannot make thy image seem more bright.
For thou art like a star that shines alone,
Within the silent watches of the night;
A beauty that is more than can be known,
By any mortal sense of touch or sight.
Then let my silence speak what words cannot,
And let my heart its own deep secret keep;
For love is often found in every spot,
Where quiet waters run both still and deep.
    And though my tongue be tied by love’s constraint,
    My spirit shall thy lovely image paint.
