III

Look in thy glass and see the face of youth,
Before the wrinkles of old age appear;
For there is written in the lines of truth,
The story of a beauty held so dear.
But do not let that beauty waste away,
Like flowers that wither in the summer heat;
But rather seek to make a longer stay,
By leaving here a likeness more complete.
So shall thy image live when thou art gone,
And speak to ages that are yet to be;
A light that through the darkness still shall shine,
To show the world what I beheld in thee.
    Then be not cruel to the coming race,
    But in a child preserve thy lovely face.
