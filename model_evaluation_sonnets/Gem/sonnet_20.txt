XX

The end of all things is a quiet sleep,
Where all the troubles of the world are done;
And those who used to wake and used to weep,
Find that the race of life is finally won.
But though the body must return to dust,
And all the glory of the earth depart,
Yet there is still a quiet sense of trust,
Within the inner chamber of the heart.
So let the years roll on their weary course,
And time perform his work of slow decay;
Our love is drawn from a more heavenly source,
And shall not with the shadows fade away.
    For truth and beauty are of one accord,
    And find in love their only sure reward.
