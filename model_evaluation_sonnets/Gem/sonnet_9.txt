IX

When in the sessions of my lonely thought,
I summoned up the ghosts of things long past,
I found that all the prizes I had sought,
Were only shadows that could never last.
But then I turned my eyes to look on thee,
And found a substance that was real and true;
A light that through the darkness I could see,
And every day my spirit did renew.
Now am I happy in my present state,
And do not fear the changes of the years;
For I have found a more than mortal fate,
Which far transcends all human doubts and fears.
    For in thy love I find my only rest,
    The quiet haven of a troubled breast.
