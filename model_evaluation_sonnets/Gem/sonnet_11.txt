XI

O let me not to changing minds admit,
That love is only for a little while;
For there is written in the holy writ,
That truth is found within a gentle smile.
So though the world may alter and decay,
And all the glory of the earth depart,
Yet love shall find a more than mortal stay,
Within the quiet temple of the heart.
Be then as constant as the stars above,
Which keep their places in the silent sky;
And let the world behold our perfect love,
Which is a thing that can never surely die.
    For as the sun gives light to all he sees,
    So shall our love survive the world’s decrees.
