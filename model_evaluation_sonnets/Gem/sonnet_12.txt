XII

The summer leaves are falling to the ground,
And winter’s breath is felt within the air;
So is the fate of every beauty found,
That was within the springtime passing fair.
But thou shalt find a more enduring prime,
Which shall not with the seasons pass away;
For in my verse I make a war on time,
To keep thy image from a slow decay.
So shall the world behold thy beauty still,
When all the flowers of youth are dead and gone;
A light that shall the future ages fill,
And through the darkness lead the spirit on.
    For as the sun returns to wake the day,
    So shall my verse thy lovely form portray.
