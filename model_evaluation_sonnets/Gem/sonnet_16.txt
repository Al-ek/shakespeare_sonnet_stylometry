XVI

O how I wish that I could find the words,
To tell the world of all thy beauty’s grace;
And sing a song more sweet than any birds,
To show the quiet glory of thy face.
But since my tongue is tied by love’s constraint,
And all my numbers are but weak and poor,
I let my silence all thy virtues paint,
And in my heart thy lovely form endure.
So shall the future ages know thy name,
And wonder at the beauty I beheld;
A light that shall outlast the empty fame,
Which by the breath of fickle men is swelled.
    For as the sun gives light to all he sees,
    So shall my heart perform thy sweet decrees.
