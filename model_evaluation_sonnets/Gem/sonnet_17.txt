XVII

The glass will show thee how thy beauty wears,
And tell the story of the passing years;
But do not let it fill thy heart with cares,
Or move thy gentle eyes to salty tears.
For though the outward form must surely fade,
And all the glory of the youth depart,
Yet there is still a quiet beauty made,
Within the inner chamber of the heart.
So let the world its empty prizes seek,
And time perform his work of slow decay;
Thy spirit shall remains both mild and meek,
And through the darkness find a brighter way.
    For love is not a thing of outward show,
    But finds its home where quiet waters flow.
