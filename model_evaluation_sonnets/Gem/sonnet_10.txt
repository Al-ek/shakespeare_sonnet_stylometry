As one who travels on a lonely road,
And sees the light of home from far away,
So does my spirit cast its heavy load,
When I behold the dawning of thy day.
For thou art like a beacon in the night,
That guides the weary traveler to his goal;
A beauty that is more than mortal sight,
And brings a quiet comfort to the soul.
So let the world go on its busy way,
And seek the empty prizes of the earth;
I am contented with a brighter day,
And find in thee a more than mortal worth.
    For love is not a thing of time and space,
    But finds its home within a lovely face.
