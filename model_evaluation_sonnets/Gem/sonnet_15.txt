XV

When I consider all the things that grow,
And how they flourish for a little space,
I think of how the world is but a show,
Where every beauty finds a fleeting place.
But thou art like a rock within the sea,
That stands unmoved against the rising tide;
A beauty that is fixed and sure to be,
And in whose light my spirit shall abide.
So let the seasons change and years roll by,
And time perform his work of slow decay;
Thy image shall remain within the sky,
And shall not with the shadows fade away.
    For as the sun returns to wake the day,
    So shall thy love provide a constant stay.
