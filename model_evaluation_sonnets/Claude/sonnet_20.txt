XX

Now let these lines go out into the world
As ships go out that trust the uncertain sea;
Their sails with no great confidence unfurled,
Their cargo nothing more than thee and me.
I have not written well enough for thee,
No sonnet earns its subject, least of all
The subject who is most entirely free
Of any cage that verse can raise or fall.
But this is what the lover does with time,
Turns longing into stanzas, ache to art,
And hopes that some imperfect borrowed rhyme
May carry the full weight of a full heart.
  If these lines fail, the love in them is true;
  I wrote them all, and wrote them all for you.
