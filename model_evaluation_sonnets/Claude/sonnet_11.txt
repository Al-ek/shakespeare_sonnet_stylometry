XI

When first I loved I thought that love was simple,
A matter of the heart that asked no art,
No more complex than the forming of a dimple,
No labour but the opening of the heart.
But love, I find, is architecture vast,
A cathedral raised by patient, aching hands,
Whose walls must bear the weather and the blast
And yet stand firm on love's uneasy sands.
The new-made lover enters love as fields,
But finds it is a country deep with range;
What easy joy the first acquaintance yields
Must deepen into something fine and strange.
  So I have laboured long and built thee well,
  A house for love no winter wind can fell.
