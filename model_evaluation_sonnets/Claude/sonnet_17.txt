XVII

There is a silence richer than all speech,
That speaks between the sentences we say,
A language that no grammar rules can teach,
A thing we understand but cannot weigh.
Thou art the master of that silent tongue,
Canst say with half a look what hours would fail
To speak in verse or story, new or sung,
Or all the words that speech could ever scale.
And I have learned to listen in that way,
To hear the pause, to read the quiet air,
To understand what neither of us say
And love thee more for what is only there.
  Between us lives a language never taught:
  The deepest truths are those most rarely brought.
