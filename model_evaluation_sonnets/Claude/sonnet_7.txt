VII

To write of beauty is to court defeat,
For beauty, living, mocks the poet's art;
The verse stands cold while beauty walks the street
And quickens eye and lung and speaking heart.
The pen is slow where beauty is so fleet,
The metaphor falls short of what it means,
The rhyme arrives too late, the measure neat
But false against the truth of living scenes.
Yet what would beauty be without its praise,
Without the tongue to name it and declare
That in this fleeting and unnumbered maze
Of mortal things, this thing is passing fair?
  I write thee poorly, but I write thee still,
  For silence would undo me more than skill.
