VI

When thou art absent from this daily scene
And I must fill the hours without thy face,
The world grows thin and all the spaces between
The things I do grow wider, cold, and base.
The morning coffee, drunk without a word,
The street that stretches empty to the square,
The song from somewhere distant, barely heard,
The chair across from mine, too neatly there.
What small despairs attend the common day
When common day is stripped of thee, its sun;
How long the afternoon seems forced to stay,
How late the evening comes before it's done.
  Yet every absent day reminds me plain:
  No hour holds worth until thou art again.
