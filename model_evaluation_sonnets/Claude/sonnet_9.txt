IX

That love grows cold is what the cynics claim,
Who nurse their disappointments into law
And raise their private losses to a frame
Of truth, as if their wounds had made them wise.
But love that grows is not the love they knew,
Theirs was a fire that flared and died to ash;
The love I hold is of a different hue,
It does not flash and perish in the flash.
For love that lasts is rooted, not ignited,
Is built from days and grown from shared small things;
Not stars above, but candle-flame, well-lighted,
That warms the room on which no candle sings.
  Let cynics cold their coldness proudly wear;
  My love is warm and will remain so there.
