VIII

Those eyes of thine, which I have read so long,
Contain more text than any scholar's shelf;
Each glance a chapter, every silence song,
Each blink a meditation on the self.
I have not read thee through, for thou art vast,
A volume whose last pages hide from me;
I turn thee slowly, savouring what hath passed,
Yet knowing still how much I'll never see.
What joy attends the reader of great works
Who finds upon each reading something new,
Some shadow in the margin that still lurks,
Some meaning deeper than the one he knew.
  So I return to thee with eager eye,
  And read thee ever fresh until I die.
