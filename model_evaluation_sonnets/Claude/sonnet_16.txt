XVI

Let others chase the thing they call renown
And build their towers skyward out of pride;
Let others wear ambition like a crown
And conquer every rival left beside.
I want no crown that time must take again,
No tower built on the applause of men,
No glory won by some forgotten pen,
No victory that rust must end by then.
Give me instead this small and certain light,
This one room warm, this one voice in the dark,
This ordinary and extraordinary night
Shared quietly as a candle or a spark.
  The conqueror's tomb is stone and cold and plain;
  My home is thee, and I shall not complain.
