XV

What is it age doth take that youth held dear?
Not beauty only, though it takes that too;
Not only strength, nor yet the unmarked year,
But something harder still to name or view.
It takes the sense that futures stretch ahead
Like fields upon a morning clear and wide,
And leaves instead the knowledge, cold as lead,
That fields have edges, however bright the ride.
Yet thou hast given me back that stretching sense,
That morning feeling, acres without end;
With thee the future is again immense,
And I am young again around the bend.
  Love is the art of making futures seem
  As wide as any young and hopeful dream.
