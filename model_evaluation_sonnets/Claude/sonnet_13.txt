XIII

Thou art the argument I never lose,
The answer waiting past the question's end;
When all the world hangs grey with weary hues,
Thou art the colour reason cannot mend.
For reason builds its houses out of stone
And counts its windows carefully by light,
But love constructs from laughter and from moan
A home that stands more warmly in the night.
I have been reason's servant long and well,
Have measured out my days in careful sums,
But reason cannot do what only love can tell,
Cannot explain the joy when evening comes.
  So I surrender reason to thy grace,
  And find my truest logic in thy face.
