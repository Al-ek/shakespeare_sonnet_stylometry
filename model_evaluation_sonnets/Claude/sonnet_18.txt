XVIII

Come, let us count the faults of all our days
And lay them out like relics on a cloth:
The hasty word, the too-convenient praise,
The patience traded early for the froth.
I have been careless where I should have cared,
And careful where I should have thrown all caution;
I have been muted where I should have dared
And loud and clumsy in the quieter portion.
Yet love, if thou canst look upon this pile,
This heap of small mishandlings, and remain,
Then thou hast chosen with the truest guile
And loved me not for gold but for the grain.
  To be known fully, faults and all displayed,
  And loved regardless—that is not betrayed.
