I

When winter's breath doth frost the morning glass
And strips the oak of all its gilded pride,
No summer's warmth nor any season's pass
Can warm the cold that settles deep inside.
Yet in thy face I find the sun renewed,
As March reclaims the barren sleeping earth,
And all that age and sorrow have subdued
Returns upon the sight of thy sweet worth.
What frost may do to field and fallow ground,
What time may carve upon the brow of man,
Cannot undo that which in thee is found,
Nor undo love since love's long life began.
  Then let the cold do all that cold can do;
  My warmth is kept so long as I have you.
