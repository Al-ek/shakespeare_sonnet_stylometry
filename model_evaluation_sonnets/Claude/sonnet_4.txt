IV

The clock upon the mantle strikes the hour
And every stroke declares what we have lost;
The rose already knows its final bower,
The ship at harbour dreams not of its cost.
So we, who count our days by candle-ends
And mark our years by faces turned to stone,
Must love the more as every season bends
Toward that cold country we must reach alone.
Then spend thyself as one who knows the price,
Pour out thy kindness ere the well runs dry,
Be lavish, love, and think not twice nor thrice
Before thou let another see thee shine.
  The sun that hoards its light earns no one's praise;
  Shine freely, love, and set the world ablaze.
