XIV

Doth absence make the heart more fond or thin?
I am the proof that both may be the case;
More fond, for I have felt thee nearer in
My longing than in nearness face to face.
Yet thin, for longing wears the spirit down
As water wears the colour from a stone,
And patience, dressed too long in absence's gown,
Grows threadbare at the shoulder and the bone.
Come back, then, and restore what thou hast worn;
Let nearness pay the debt that distance owes,
For I am frayed and thin and slightly torn
About the edges where the cold wind goes.
  Return and stitch me whole again with thee,
  For thou art all the mending that I need.
