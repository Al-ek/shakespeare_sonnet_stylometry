V

Mine eye hath loved thee longer than my tongue
Hath found the words to speak what sight hath known;
The eye perceives what speech hath left unsung,
And loves in silence what it will not own.
For words are coins that lose their face with use,
Worn smooth by every speaker's daily trade,
But sight is pure and makes no such abuse,
Nor barters what the heart hath truly weighed.
Speak love to thee? I would, but fear the air
Might cheapen what the eye so rightly prized;
For love once spoken lies exposed and bare,
And nakedness is sometimes ill-advised.
  Yet silence, too, is love's most grievous wrong:
  I'll speak, and trust the feeling beats the song.
