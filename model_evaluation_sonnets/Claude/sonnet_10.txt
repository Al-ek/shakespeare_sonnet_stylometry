What is the use of all these written years,
These stanzas stacked like timber in a store,
If not to say, beyond my dust and fears,
That beauty lived, and I beheld it more?
For I shall go the way of all men go,
As autumn goes and takes its colour down,
And what I thought and felt none else shall know
Unless these words outlive my borrowed gown.
So let these lines stand after I am done
As witnesses to one who loved and saw,
Who walked beneath this same familiar sun
And found it glorious without a flaw.
  The man is mortal but the moment stays:
  In thee I've written my most deathless days.
