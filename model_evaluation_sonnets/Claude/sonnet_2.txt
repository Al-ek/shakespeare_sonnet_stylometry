II

Shall I compare the hours I waste in thought
To all the hours I might have spent in deed?
The things I've left undone, the words unsought,
The garden left to ruin for want of seed.
Yet thinking of thee is no idle art,
No waste of hours when the mind is thine;
For contemplation plants within the heart
A crop more rich than labour's dullest vine.
The farmer toils beneath a burning sun
And reaps a harvest ere the cold descends,
But I need toil not, for what thou hast done
Is harvest me before the summer ends.
  Thus in my idleness I am most blest,
  For thinking thee, I prosper while I rest.
