XIX

The tide goes out and will return by night,
The moon pulls hard on all that waits below;
The seasons pass and yield to their own light,
And time keeps only what it chooses slow.
But some things hold against the tide and year:
The shape of mountains does not change by night,
The deep stone of devotion, quarried clear,
Remains when lesser loves have lost their sight.
So let the tide take back what tide must have,
The temporary joys, the borrowed days,
The youth that time removes as cold waves salve
The sandcastles of our youthful haze.
  When tide and time have taken all the rest,
  My love for thee endures as mountains best.
