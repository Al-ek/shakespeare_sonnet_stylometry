XII

How many men have sat as I sit now
Beside a window in the fading light,
With thoughts of one they cannot disavow
And love that keeps them restless through the night?
How many hands have taken up the pen
To scratch some likeness of a face beloved,
And found the face escape the scratching, then
Given over the task, but still unmoved?
The history of love is mostly lost,
Most lovers' names are dust and less than dust,
Their ardour paid the ordinary cost
Of mortal flesh, which is to fall and rust.
  And yet I write, as they who wrote before,
  For love must try the pen, though it fail more.
