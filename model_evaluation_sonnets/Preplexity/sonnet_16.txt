XVI

When tempest swells and reason’s beacon dies,
I steer by faith, the compass of the mind;
Though stars be veiled, my heart still toward thee flies,
To seek the calm no harbor else could find.
For thou, whose soul was constant as the north,
Dost draw my thought through turmoil into peace;
And even thus, though fate may drive me forth,
Thy memory makes the storm’s assault to cease.
Let time consume what body’s breath maintains,
Let chance deface what fortune first designed;
Yet love, that sails through all the world’s domains,
Returns to thee, unaltered, undivined.
Thus though I drift where wit and will are numb,
Thy faith shall light the sea to which I come.
