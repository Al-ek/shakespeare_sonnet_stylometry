XII

O Fate unmasked in garments fair of chance,
Thou jester deep in wisdom’s humble guise,
We turn to flee, yet yield thee new advance,
Mistaking trick for truth and truth for lies.
Yet blesséd are they who see through thy deceit,
And trust the end though all beginnings bleed;
For every snare that snares the proud and fleet,
Doth lead the meek to what they truly need.
So laugh, O Fate, for still I see thy jest,
Thy whip is but the measure of thy play;
The soul that suffers longest loves thee best,
And crowns thee kindly when thou thron’d dismay.
Then rule me so, and let thy storm be mine,
Till mercy dawns and calls thy will divine.
