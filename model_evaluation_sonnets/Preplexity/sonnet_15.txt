XV

I count the stars, yet every star is thine,
As though thy glance had kindled all the skies;
For thou hast burned thy likeness into mine,
And set thy light in every look that flies.
The night itself grows tender to the sight,
As though its dark were woven of thy hair;
And every sound that steals across the night,
Seems tuned by lips that once denied despair.
So vast thou art, so intimate thy reign,
That all creation whispers but thy part;
I fear to call thee mistress lest in vain,
I claim the world confined within one heart.
Yet here I stand, where reason dares remove,
The small but boundless universe of love.
