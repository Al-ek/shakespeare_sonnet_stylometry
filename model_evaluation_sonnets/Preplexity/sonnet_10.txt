X

If art were truth, and truth could take a hue,
To please the roving eye of mortal sight,
Then each deceit were fair, each fair were true,
And darkness praised for shadowing the light.
Yet beauty’s law condemns the flatterer’s hand,
For only pure intent can shape the bright;
The form may fade no critic can command,
But still the soul sustains its inward right.
Then paint thy heart in virtues soft array,
And let the features of thy spirit shine;
For outward charms the passing hours betray,
While inward grace proclaims what is divine.
Thus truth, though bare, shall conquer every art,
And rule the world from thine unpainted heart.
