XIX

How strangely sweet the evening’s quiet grows,
When words are done and only glances speak;
The heart no longer strives, no passion knows,
But feels in peace what speech could never seek.
Thy nearness blooms like scent within the dark,
And fills the air with thought's unspoken prayer;
The night itself becomes affection’s mark,
And all the world is changed because thou’rt there.
Let silence reign, for silence now is true,
And every tongue that moves would mar its charm;
My very pulse is eloquent of you,
My soul a psalm that slumbers on thy arm.
So still, so near, so perfect is this rest,
That heaven looks on and deems our mortal blest.
