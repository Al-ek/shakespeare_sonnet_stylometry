XX

Now ends my verse, yet not my heart’s device,
For hearts cannot by syllables be bound;
Though rhyme conclude, yet feeling will suffice,
Where breath departs but meaning still is found.
I leave these lines as shells upon the shore,
That murmur faintly when thy hand is near;
They speak of days that shall return no more,
Yet hold their music for thy secret ear.
If some poor truth within their measure lies,
Receive it not as art but uttered love;
For what I owed to heaven for my sighs,
I pay to thee, all other debts above.
And if these songs should in thy spirit move,
Then I am blest, and silence speaks of love.
