XVII

O Death, thou stern interpreter of love,
That speak’st in silence all our mortal fear;
Thy hand divides yet joins what lay above,
And chastens sight to make the vision clear.
We bless the dawn, yet shame to bless the night,
Which holds the promise dawn itself denies;
So teach us, Death, to read thy deeper light,
And see through loss where life immortal lies.
For every parting is but folded grace,
That waits to bloom in some eternal field;
And hearts once stricken learn the brave embrace,
By which all wounds unto their healing yield.
So let me not fear thy appointed art,
For death but ends the body, not the heart.
