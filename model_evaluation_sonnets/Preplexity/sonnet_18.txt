XVIII

The earth was mute, till music learned to grieve,
And grief gave birth to melodies divine;
Thus only those who love and then must leave,
Can shape the chord that binds the human line.
Our joys are brief, but through their swift decay,
We catch the tune eternity begins;
The harp of sorrow, sounding through the day,
Transforms our losses into secret wins.
Then if I weep, these tears are not of woe,
But liquid praise for all that could not stay;
For hearts must break if love would fully show,
How light may dwell within a frame of clay.
So let me sing, though silence stops my breath,
For love is life, and endures beyond death.
