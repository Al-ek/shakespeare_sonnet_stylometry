XI

Ah, Memory, thou tyrant soft and sly,
Whose tender chains are woven out of air,
Thou bind’st the mind yet leav’st the body by,
To wander where no present joys repair.
I would forget, yet cannot quite begin,
For still thy whispers call me to the past;
Each echo stirs the dream I nursed within,
And builds again the house that could not last.
But be thou kind, and keep the good alone,
The grief has served, let beauty yet remain;
Take not from me what sweetest days have shown,
Let loss be gold refined from love and pain.
So, Memory, stay—but gentle be thy reign,
And play the harp that will not sound again.
