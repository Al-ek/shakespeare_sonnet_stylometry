XIII

When winter wraps the forest in its frost,
And silence rules the valleys white and bare,
It seems all sweetness, all delight is lost,
And music gone that once possessed the air.
Yet underneath, the unseen roots endure,
While sleeping buds prepare the heart of May;
So hope remains, however cold and poor,
To greet again the resurrected day.
Let not despair persuade thee love is dead,
Because its flowers are hidden from thy sight;
The bloom shall lift where all the tears were shed,
And pain shall blossom into calm delight.
For faith, though famished, never dies of cold,
But feeds on promise silently unfold.
