XIV

How shall I praise thee, knowing words may fall,
Like sparrows dazed against the vaulted sky?
Each syllable half-born, too frail, too small,
To bear the light of what they would imply.
Thou art the thought that silence comprehends,
The flame that burns where speech has lost its voice;
So let my heart declare what sense defends,
That love hath made my poverty its choice.
Better to stammer truth than fluent lies,
Better a sigh that owns what it confesses;
For eloquence in vain with beauty vies,
Where feeling reigns and not the tongue’s caresses.
Then let this verse, though weak, at least be true,
It lives for thee, and dies proclaiming you.
