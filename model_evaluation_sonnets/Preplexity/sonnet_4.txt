IV

The rose that blooms when no man stands to see,
Retains its fragrance though it fade unseen;
So virtues live though hid in poverty,
And truth is fairest clothed in humble mien.
The garden’s praise belongs not to the crowd,
But to the sun that lights each silent seed;
For fame’s bright boast is but a trumpet loud,
That covers love’s more gentle, secret deed.
Thus would I rather dwell outside renown,
And serve unknown within the heart’s retreat;
For crowns may dazzle, yet their gold may drown,
The tender soul that wanted but the beat,
Of one kind heart, more precious than a throne,
Whose glance can make the lowliest mine own.
