VII

When first I spied thy face through noon’s soft haze,
The air about thee trembled into song;
My heart forgot the burden of its days,
And joy confessed it had been silent long.
Thine eyes declared a harmony of rest,
Thy voice was calm, as though the world were kind;
And yet I felt through all thy gentle breast,
A fiercer grace that mastered all my mind.
So, having loved thee, none may say I erred,
Though fate denounce the hour that we met;
For in thy glance my inmost soul was stirred,
And still it wakes where time would bid forget.
The day may fall, the stars grow faint above,
Yet none can quench the witness of that love.
