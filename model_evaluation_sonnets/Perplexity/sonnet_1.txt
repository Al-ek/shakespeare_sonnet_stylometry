I

When evening sighs and lays her silver hand,
Upon the brow of day’s departing fire,
The stars attend their monarch’s soft command,
And guard the sleep of weary earth’s desire.
So comes remembrance, delicate and deep,
To stir the ashes where our passions burn;
The heart half-wakes from its enchanted sleep,
And to the haunted dusk will still return.
Then in thy thought, if I but faintly live,
Allow my ghost one breath of tender cheer;
Though time may take what I alone could give,
Let absence prove that love outlasts the year.
For those who loved beyond their mortal part,
Bear endless summer folded in their heart.
