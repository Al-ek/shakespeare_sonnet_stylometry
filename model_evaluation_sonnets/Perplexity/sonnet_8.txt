VIII

I wrote thy name upon the streaming sand,
And watched the tide unwrite it from the shore;
So passes all that vanity hath planned,
And human pride forgets what once it bore.
Yet though the water mocked my mortal art,
The sea confessed thy likeness in its hue;
For every wave that touched my patient heart,
Returned its brightness, faithful and as true.
Thus is thy love no fleeting line of chance,
But deep as depths where silence rules the tide;
Though earth erase, the ocean’s vast expanse,
Records thy being where her tides abide.
So perish words, but not the love they tell,
For heaven keeps what sea and earth expel.
