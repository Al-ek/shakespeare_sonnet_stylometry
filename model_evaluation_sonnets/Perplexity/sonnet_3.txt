III

If words were ships, I’d send them out to thee,
Across the tempests of delay and change;
But fear that winds of silence shall decree,
Their harbor lost, their mission dark and strange.
Yet shall I trust their sails of faith to fly,
And dare the distance memory cannot cross;
For in the vast unmeasured of the sky,
No soul is lost that mourns its proper loss.
Each letter cast into this boundless sea,
Is swept, I know, by unseen currents’ grace,
Till somewhere, faint yet pure, it rests with thee,
And whispers comfort to thy dreaming face.
So let them wander, these poor words of mine,
To find thee still, and call thy name divine.
