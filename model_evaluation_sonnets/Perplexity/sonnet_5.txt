V

Why should we chase the shadow life bestows,
If in the light itself we fear to stay?
Each pleasure fled as swiftly as it grows,
And hope but guides us deeper into day.
For joy, like dew upon the early leaf,
Shines most before the burning sun of truth;
And grief, that stern yet faithful counter-thief,
Steals all delight but leaves the heart its youth.
Thus do we learn, when fortune rides amiss,
That loss redeems what bounty could not teach;
The soul through sorrow finds its proper bliss,
And love through silence learns the tongue of speech.
Then welcome change, though cruel to the view,
For naught remains save what the heart makes true.
