II

The morning came with trumpets of the sun,
That broke the dark’s retreat in scarlet flame;
All toil resumed, all dreams of night were done,
And life repeated its unending game.
Yet thou, whose gaze could turn the dawn to peace,
Didst halt my thought upon the edge of day;
In thee I found all striving seek release,
And labor’s song grew sweeter by thy sway.
O beauty born not out of vain display,
But out of truth which time cannot confound,
Thy simple grace outshines the proud array,
That courts the eyes yet leaves the soul unsound.
Thus learn the heavens from thy quiet art,
To shine more gently, lest they break the heart.
