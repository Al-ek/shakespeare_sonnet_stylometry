VI

I saw a child at play beside the sea,
Who laughed to chase each unremitting wave;
Though every surge returned with mockery,
His joy was boundless, innocent, and brave.
O heart of man, remember such delight,
When age would teach thee wisdom for thy pain;
For still the tide obeys the hand of Night,
And all we lose shall surely rise again.
Be thou the child whose faith the storm endures,
And loves the depth though all the foam be wild;
For only love through endless change assures,
That heaven itself was made as for a child.
Then play, O soul, till all thy strife be done,
And thou art sea and laughter and the sun.
