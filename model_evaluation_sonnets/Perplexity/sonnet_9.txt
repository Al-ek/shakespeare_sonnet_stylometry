IX

How rich is time, that from decay can weave,
A blossom fairer than the first of spring!
Though autumn sigh, yet she shall not bereave,
The root that waits renewal’s secret ring.
For thus does life, through death’s uncertain trance,
Conceal its growth in seasons of despair;
Till every grave becomes a seed’s advance,
And tears but melt the clod for things more fair.
O tremble not when all seems turned to dust,
The phœnix builds her cradle in the flame;
And hearts once broke shall find again their trust,
When pain hath taught them mercy’s gentler name.
So live that loss may prove the world’s design,
And death itself bear witness thou art mine.
