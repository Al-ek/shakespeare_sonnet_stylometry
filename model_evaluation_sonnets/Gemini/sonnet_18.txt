XVIII

The clock doth tick away the pulse of youth,
And mocks us with its steady, rhythmic beat,
It cares not for the beauty or the truth,
Nor all the bitterness of our defeat.
It brings the morning and it brings the night,
In one relentless and unending ring,
Until the spark of life has lost its light,
And winter comes to kill the hope of spring.
But love can stand outside the reach of clocks,
And live within a moment ever vast,
It fears not all the turning of the locks,
That hold the future and the buried past.
One hour with thee is worth an age of years,
A joy that triumphs over all our fears.
