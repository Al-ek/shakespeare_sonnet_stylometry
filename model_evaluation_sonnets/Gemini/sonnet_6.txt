VI

When I behold the mountains capped with snow,
And see the rivers rushing to the deep,
I wonder where the weary spirits go,
When they have fallen into final sleep.
Doth any part of us remain behind,
To whisper to the ones who linger here?
Or are we lost like whispers in the wind,
And drowned within a sea of doubt and fear?
But then I look into thy shining eyes,
And see a light that death can never quench,
A promise written in the vaulted skies,
Which even deepest sorrow cannot wrench.
Whatever end the Fates for us decree,
My soul shall find its dwelling place in thee.
