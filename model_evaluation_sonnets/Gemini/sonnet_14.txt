XIV

When in the chronicles of wasted time,
I see the brave and noble pass away,
I seek to find a reason in my rhyme,
To keep the dark oblivion at bay.
The poet’s pen is but a fragile reed,
Against the howling of the desert wind,
And yet it is the only thing we need,
To leave a trace of what we were behind.
I write of thee so that the world may know,
How fair a spirit dwelt among us here,
That even when the grass begins to grow,
Above thy head, thy light shall still appear.
This verse shall be thy monument and shrine,
To keep thy memory through the reach of time.
