XIX

Why should I weep for what I cannot hold,
Or mourn the passing of the summer rose?
The story of the earth is ever old,
And every chapter hastens to its close.
But in the turning of the heavy page,
I find a verse that speaks of thee and me,
A line that defies the touch of age,
And points toward the life that is to be.
For we are more than dust and shifting sand,
More than the shadows on a sunlit wall,
Held firmly in a great and loving hand,
That will not let the smallest sparrow fall.
So dry thy tears and look upon the sun,
The journey is but only just begun.
