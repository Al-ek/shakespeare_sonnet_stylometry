XI

Is it a crime to love what must decay,
To give the heart to what the worm shall feed?
To chase the fleeting phantoms of the day,
And plant the garden with a dying seed?
The rose must wither and the lily fall,
The oak must bow before the winter blast,
For death is but the master of us all,
And claims his heavy tribute at the last.
Yet who would trade the rapture of the spring,
For all the cold endurance of the stone?
Who would not hear the summer thrush to sing,
Because he fears to sit at last alone?
Though beauty dies, the love of it remains,
To wash away the dust of mortal stains.
