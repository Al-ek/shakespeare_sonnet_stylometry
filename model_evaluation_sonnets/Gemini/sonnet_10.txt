The moon doth borrow light from out the sun,
To guide the traveler through the lonely night,
And when the work of busy day is done,
She sheds a soft and melancholy light.
So do I borrow all my light from thee,
When darkness gathers round my heavy heart,
For thou art all the sun I need to see,
And all the joy that nature can impart.
Without thy beam I wander in the dark,
A ship without a compass or a sail,
Waiting for the morning’s tiny spark,
To rise above the shadows, thin and pale.
Thou art my sun, my moon, my guiding star,
No matter where the tides of fortune are.
