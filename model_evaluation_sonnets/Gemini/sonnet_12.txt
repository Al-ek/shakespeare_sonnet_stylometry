XII

Let not the world persuade me thou art changed,
Because thy brow is furrowed by the years,
Or that thy heart from mine is now estranged,
By all the weight of human toil and tears.
The outward form is but a painted screen,
That hides the inner glory of the soul,
Where all the truth of what we are is seen,
And every broken part is rendered whole.
I love thee not for what the eye perceives,
The fleeting bloom, the smoothness of the skin,
But for the faith that never once deceives,
The noble spirit that resides within.
Though time may rob the temple of its grace,
It cannot dim the light within the place.
