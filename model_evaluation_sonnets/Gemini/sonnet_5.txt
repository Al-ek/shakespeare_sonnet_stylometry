V

The lark that soars to meet the rising sun,
Doth sing a song of triumph to the sky,
Before the race of mortal life is run,
And all the earthly visions fade and die.
I look upon the wonder of thy youth,
And marvel at the light within thy gaze,
For thou art born of beauty and of truth,
To lead me through the dark and tangled maze.
The world may offer riches and renown,
And jewels bright to deck a monarch’s brow,
But I would cast away a golden crown
To be with thee as I am with thee now.
For wealth is but a shadow on the wall,
While love is sovereign master over all.
