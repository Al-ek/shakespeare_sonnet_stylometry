VIII

O how much fairer is the simple flower,
That blooms unseen within the forest glade,
Than all the pomp of royalty and power,
Which in a moment’s passing time shall fade.
Thy beauty is a quiet, holy thing,
That asks for nothing but to simply be,
Like water flowing from a hidden spring,
To find its way into the restless sea.
The world is loud with vanity and pride,
And men do strive for things of little worth,
But I would lay my heavy cares aside,
To walk with thee upon the greening earth.
For in thy presence every storm is still,
And peace descends upon the weary will.
