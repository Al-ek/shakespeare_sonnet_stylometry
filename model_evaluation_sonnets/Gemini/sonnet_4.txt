IV

How like a dream the summer fades away,
To leave the world in autumn’s golden shroud,
The brightness of the long and lingering day
Is hidden now beneath a heavy cloud.
The birds have flown to find a warmer clime,
And left the woods to silence and to rest,
We are but players in the hands of time,
By fleeting joys and sudden sorrows blessed.
But in the quiet garden of my thought,
Thy image blooms in everlasting hue,
The lessons that thy gentle soul hath taught
Are ever fresh and ever bright and new.
No winter wind can strip the boughs of grace,
Wherein I find the memory of thy face.
