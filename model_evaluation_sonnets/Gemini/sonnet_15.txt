XV

The sea doth hunger for the crumbling land,
And eats the cliffs with every rising tide,
She scatters all the works of human hand,
And swallows up the vanity of pride.
So doth the vast eternity of space,
Consume the little moments of our life,
And wipe away the memory of a face,
In one short hour of struggle and of strife.
But love is not a creature of the earth,
To be devoured by time or by the sea,
It hath a higher and a holier birth,
And partakes of the soul’s eternity.
Though mountains melt and oceans turn to dust,
In love alone we place our final trust.
