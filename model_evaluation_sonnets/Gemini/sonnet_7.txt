VII

The potter molds the clay with steady hand,
To fashion vessels for the master’s use,
Some are for honor in a noble land,
And some for common toil and dark abuse.
So hath the Great Creator fashioned thee,
From finest earth and breath of holy fire,
To be a mirror of the soul’s decree,
And object of the world’s most pure desire.
But even clay must crumble into dust,
And finest gold must lose its brilliant glow,
In iron time we place our mortal trust,
While toward the silent grave we blindly go.
Yet shall thy virtues live in every line,
And make the mortal part of thee divine.
