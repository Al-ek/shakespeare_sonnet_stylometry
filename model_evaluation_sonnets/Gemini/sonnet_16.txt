XVI

The shepherd leads his flock to pastures green,
And guards them through the heat of summer day,
Beneath the shade of ancient trees unseen,
He pipes a tune to pass the hours away.
So would I lead thy spirit to the light,
And shelter thee from every biting cold,
To be thy champion in the darkest fight,
And bring thee safely to the quiet fold.
For thou art dearer than the wandering sheep,
To one who loves the music of thy voice,
A treasure that the heart shall ever keep,
The only one of all the world’s fair choice.
Though wolves may howl and stormy winds may blow,
My love shall guard thee everywhere thou’st go.
