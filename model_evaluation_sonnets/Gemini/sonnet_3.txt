III

As waves do break against the rugged shore,
And spend their fury on the uncaring stone,
So do the years consume what went before,
And leave the heart to wander all alone.
But thou art like a cliff that stands so tall,
Against the surging tides of cruel fate,
Thou dost not fear the tempest nor the fall,
Nor all the bitterness of human hate.
For in thy soul a steady fire doth burn,
That warms the coldness of the passing hours,
And though the seasons to the earth return,
Thy love is fairer than the spring’s first flowers.
Let time devour the forest and the sea,
It cannot touch the gift thou gavest me.
