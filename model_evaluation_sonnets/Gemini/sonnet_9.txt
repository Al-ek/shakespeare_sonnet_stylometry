IX

If I should count the moments we have spent,
In sweet communion by the fire’s glow,
I would not mourn the riches I have lent,
To buy the joys that only lovers know.
For time is but a merchant, hard and cold,
Who trades our youth for wisdom and for gray,
And gathers in his bags of tarnished gold,
The precious hours of every passing day.
But what he takes he never can replace,
The warmth of touch, the kindness of a word,
The light of love upon a well-loved face,
The sweetest music that was ever heard.
So let him steal the years that yet remain,
Thy love is worth a thousand years of pain.
