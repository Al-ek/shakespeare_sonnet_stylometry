XVII

Think not that when my breath at last is spent,
And I am laid within the silent ground,
That all my love for thee was only lent,
Or that its limit ever can be found.
For love is like the air that wraps the world,
Invisible but felt in every part,
A banner that is never truly furled,
A pulse that beats within the cosmic heart.
I shall be in the rustle of the leaves,
The scent of clover in the summer rain,
The spirit that the lonely heart perceives,
To comfort thee in every hour of pain.
Death is a gate that opens on the sky,
Where love is born and never more shall die.
