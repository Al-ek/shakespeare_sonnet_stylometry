I

From winter’s chill the budding rose doth spring,
To mock the frost that bit its tender leaf,
So time shall take what summer’s joy did bring,
And turn our golden harvest into grief.
The sun that climbs the ladder of the day
Must sink at eve beneath the western hill,
And all the blooms of April’s sweet array
Are subject to the scythe of winter’s will.
Yet in the glass where thou dost view thy face,
I see a beauty time can ne’er subdue,
For though the years depart with heavy pace,
Thy spirit doth its morning light renew.
If age should steal the luster from thine eye,
My love shall live when all the seasons die.
