XIII

The miser hoards his gold in iron chests,
And counts his coins by flickering candle-light,
He fears the thief who in the shadow rests,
And trembles through the watches of the night.
But I have riches that no thief can steal,
A wealth of memories stored within my breast,
The joy of every kindness that I feel,
The peace of knowing I am truly blessed.
Thy love is more than all the silver mines,
Or pearls gathered from the deep lagoon,
A treasure that in every season shines,
More constant than the changing of the moon.
Let others seek the world’s decaying prize,
My wealth is written in thy loving eyes.
