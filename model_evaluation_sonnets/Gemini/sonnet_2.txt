II

When silver stars do gaze upon the field,
And shadows dance beneath the moon’s pale light,
I think on all the secrets night hath sealed,
Within the silent chambers of the night.
Thy name is whispered by the restless wind,
A melody that haunts my weary soul,
For in thy grace a sanctuary I find,
That makes my broken spirit once more whole.
The world may spin in madness and in strife,
And kingdoms fall like leaves upon the ground,
But thou art all the music of my life,
The only peace that ever can be found.
Though mortal breath is but a fleeting spark,
Thy love shall be my lantern in the dark.
