II

If eyes could hold the ocean in their gaze
And lips could speak the music of the spheres
Then would my soul abandon common ways
And rise above the fetters of the years
Yet mortal bounds do chain my wandering thought
And all my dreams return to dust and clay
Though longing burns for what cannot be bought
I tread the path where fleeting shadows play
O gentle heart, be patient with thy woe
For seasons turn and stars forget not thee
The world, though cruel, shall teach thee what to know
And love, though veiled, reveals what may yet be
Thus hope survives, though darkness seemeth near
A lantern bright to guide the trembling sphere
