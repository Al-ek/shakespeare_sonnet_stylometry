XI

If I could hold the wind within my hand
And shape the clouds to mirror thy delight
Then would I carve a kingdom from the sand
And make eternal every passing night
Yet mortal strength is bound by fragile bone
And human will cannot command the sky
So must I wander, often lost, alone
And learn to see the world with patient eye
O fleeting grace, thy presence lights the way
And lifts the heart from sorrow’s heavy weight
Though shadows lengthen, threatening all the day
Thy memory shall guide me to my fate
In every breath, thy whisper softly lies
A star that shines beyond the mortal skies
