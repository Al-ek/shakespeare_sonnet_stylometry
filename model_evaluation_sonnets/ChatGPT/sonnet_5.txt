V

O gentle friend, if thou shouldst hear my plea
Take not my words as hollow or in vain
For every syllable I send to thee
Is borne of hope that thou mightst ease my pain
The world is harsh and oft unjust to men
Yet in thy presence, solace may be found
A place where sorrow loses weight again
And tender hearts are lifted from the ground
So let us walk through twilight hand in hand
And speak of dreams that neither time can mar
Though fleeting be the hourglass of the sand
The love we share outshines both moon and star
For in thy smile, the weary heart takes flight
And finds its home in ever-enduring light
