VI

The river winds through valleys deep and wide
Its waters hum a song of endless flow
And like the tide, my heart is tossed aside
Yet yearns to find a shore where love may grow
O fleeting glance that kindles secret flame
And stirs the soul to dance in quiet fire
Though mortal life be fraught with grief and shame
The heart persists, as though by some desire
To hold the beauty that escapes the hand
And make it thine, though Time doth intervene
For in the grasp of love we understand
What mortal men have sought, yet rarely seen
Thus let the river carry all my care
And bear it forth unto thy gentle air
