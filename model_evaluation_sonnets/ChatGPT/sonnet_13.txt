XIII

The lark ascends upon the morning breeze
And sings a hymn that stirs the silent grove
Its music lingers in the trembling trees
A song of hope, of sorrow, and of love
O gentle bird, thy flight inspires the heart
To rise above the mortal toil and care
And see the world as more than torn apart
Where beauty dwells and kindness still is there
Though men may falter, weak in thought or deed
Yet nature’s voice proclaims a higher way
And in its song, the soul may find the seed
Of courage to endure the night and day
So let the lark’s sweet carol be my guide
And lift my spirit with the morning tide
