I

Upon the morning's breath, I cast my eye
Where golden beams do spill on dewy grass
And wonder if such light can ever die
Or fade as fleeting hours of men do pass
The rose, in secret, hides its blushing cheek
While summer winds do whisper through the air
And I, in silence, hear what none will speak
Yet feel the tender weight of beauty rare
For Time, relentless, stalks with creeping hand
And steals the blossoms ere their prime is known
But still the heart defies his stern command
And clings to joy though mortal flesh be thrown
Thus, love and life entwined in fleeting grace
Do leave their trace upon our human face
