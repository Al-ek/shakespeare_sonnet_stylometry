IX

When winter comes with all its frost and gray
And life seems shrouded by the icy veil
I seek the warmth of memory’s bright day
And let its fire within my breast prevail
For though the cold may nip at mortal skin
And winds of sorrow pierce the soul’s domain
There lies a light where hope can rise within
And cast the shadow from the heart again
O faithful love, that neither time nor pain
Can dim or mar, though earthly years decay
Thou art the constant star in every strain
The solace of the night, the guide of day
So in thy grace, I trust the fleeting hours
And find the spring amid the frozen flowers
