VIII

The wind doth stir the branches of the tree
And whispers secrets that the earth has kept
A symphony of shadow, air, and sea
That sings while mortals lie in restless slept
O gentle breeze, convey my yearning heart
To one who dwells beyond my mortal view
And tell how love, though torn, shall not depart
But rises ever pure, forever true
Though Time may steal the tender hours we know
And cast our days into the endless night
Still shall the flame of hope within me grow
A beacon burning steadfast in thy sight
For though the world may falter, fade, and fall
The heart, unbound, defies the tyrant’s call
