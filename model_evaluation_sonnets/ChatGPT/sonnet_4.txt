IV

When summer dies beneath the autumn’s hand
And leaves like gold do carpet all the ground
The air grows chill and pale the softened land
Yet in such change a silent grace is found
For even death of color holds delight
A promise made that life shall yet renew
Though shadows lengthen and eclipse the light
The heart remembers all that once it knew
So must I guard the fragile joys I hold
Though seasons take what once was fair and free
For love is not diminished by the cold
Nor by the winter’s harsh and bitter sea
In fleeting hours, eternity resides
And in our mortal breath, the world abides
