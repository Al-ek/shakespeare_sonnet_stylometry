The sun doth sink beneath the crimson west
And paints the clouds with hues of fire and gold
Yet in its setting lies a quiet rest
A promise that new light shall yet unfold
O mortal eyes, that fail to see the truth
The world’s delight is hidden in the small
The fleeting smile, the tenderness of youth
The whispered word, the gentle shadow’s call
Though kingdoms fall and time devours all men
Yet love survives where hearts are brave and free
It grows within, beyond the reach of ken
A quiet strength that shapes eternity
So let us treasure what the day bestows
And find in fleeting hours what ever grows
