VII

If I could write the stars upon the page
And bind the moon within a golden line
Then would I conquer Time and all its rage
And make this fleeting world forever mine
Yet pen and ink are frail against the night
And human hand cannot command the skies
So must I learn to treasure what is right
And see the beauty hidden from my eyes
O fleeting joy, thy passage leaves a mark
A memory that lingers past the pain
Though nightfall casts its ever-growing dark
The heart remembers sunlight after rain
In every sigh, a tender truth is shown
And mortal souls may glimpse the world unknown
