III

The nightingale upon the bough doth sing
A melody that stirs the quiet air
And makes the weary spirit rise on wing
As if the soul itself might vanish there
The moon doth hang like silver in the sky
Reflecting dreams that mortal eyes behold
While men, in vain, do search for reasons why
The heart grows faint and pleasures turn to cold
Yet in the dark, a spark of joy remains
A whisper soft that tells of what is true
Though life be bound by sorrow’s secret chains
The soul may find a dawn in every hue
O fleeting time, thy burden yet is sweet
For in thy flight, our hearts and stars do meet
