XII

The morning dew upon the grass doth gleam
As if the earth itself had learned to weep
And in its shimmer lies a tender dream
That wakens hearts from sorrow’s restless sleep
O fleeting joy, that comes but for an hour
Thy sweetness lingers in the mind’s domain
Though Time may crush the bloom of every flower
Thy fragrance in the soul shall yet remain
So let me hold thee close, though brief thy stay
And learn thy lesson in the passing light
For even shadows teach the perfect day
And darkness shows the brilliance of the bright
In every fleeting hour, love leaves its trace
A quiet echo in the heart’s embrace
